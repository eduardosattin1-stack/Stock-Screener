# Forensic Dossier — Boyd Gaming (BYD)

*A note up front on identity: the ticker BYD here is Boyd Gaming Corporation, the U.S. regional casino operator (Las Vegas locals, Downtown Las Vegas, the Midwest and South, plus online and managed businesses). It is not the Chinese electric-vehicle maker that shares the abbreviation. Every transcript, segment, and number below is Boyd Gaming.*

## 1. Narrative Arc

Across the six quarterly calls on file (first quarter 2025 through first quarter 2026), management told a remarkably steady story — almost to a fault. The core message never wavered: a diversified regional casino business carried by a resilient "core customer," disciplined cost control that holds property margins near 40%, and a $100 million-a-year growth-capital program funding new and renovated properties (Cadence Crossing, Suncoast modernization, Norfolk Virginia resort, Ameristar St. Charles convention space, Sky River expansion fees).

The one genuine narrative event was financial, not operational. In July 2025 the company announced the sale of its 5% stake in FanDuel to Flutter for $1.755 billion in cash, extending its market-access agreements through 2038 in the process. That single transaction reshaped the balance sheet — leverage dropped below 2x, and roughly $1.4 billion in after-tax cash funded an aggressive buyback. By the fourth-quarter 2025 call management could report it had returned more than $800 million to shareholders in 2025 and cut the share count by 11%.

What quietly shifted underneath the steady prose is the source of growth. Early in 2025 the story leaned on broad strength across Las Vegas. By late 2025 and into 2026 the engine had narrowed: the Midwest and South segment does the heavy lifting (people "staying and spending closer to home"), while Las Vegas is openly described as soft — destination visitation down, Fremont Street pedestrian traffic off about 11% year over year, and the Orleans plus the Suncoast renovation each acting as a drag. The growth driver introduced most recently is essentially financial engineering: a planned ~$650 million-plus of annual capital return ($150 million of buybacks per quarter plus dividends), now that the FanDuel cash is in hand.

## 2. Claims vs Financials (forensic)

The headline tension is between a screener that flags a "369% margin of safety" and a stock sitting at its all-time high. The reconciliation is the FanDuel gain.

The clean read: this is a steady, low-growth, cash-returning operator whose reported 2025 earnings were distorted upward by a single asset sale. The "deep value" signal is a scan artifact created by feeding one-time EPS into intrinsic-value models.

## 3. Tone & Confidence Trajectory

**Stable, leaning cautiously defensive.** Management's confidence is genuine but is increasingly expressed in defensive terms — balance sheet strength, cost discipline, "no concept of fixed costs," and the ability to "manage through" uncertainty — rather than offensive growth ambition.

- Q1 2025: "we have not seen any meaningful shift in consumer behavior," paired with an acknowledgment of "an increased level of economic uncertainty." Confident but hedged.
- Q2 2025: the most upbeat quarter — "the strongest [property-level growth] in more than 3 years," plus the FanDuel windfall. Offensive tone.
- Q3 2025 onward: the language turns to managing softness — "softness in destination business," repeated emphasis that the weakness is "a focus on the Orleans," and persistent caveats about the Suncoast renovation disruption.
- Q1 2026: openly concedes the Suncoast disruption became "such a big bite," walks back earlier hopes that locals could "eke out a little bit of growth," and effectively guides the Street to model a down locals segment.

Guidance specificity actually *increased* over time (they now give segment-level EBITDAR ranges), which is a credibility positive — but the content of that guidance turned more conservative.

## 4. Guidance Credibility

**High.** This is the strongest part of the file. Management consistently under-promises on near-term color and is candid when things turn. The earnings-beat rate of 6 of 7 quarters (86%) corroborates a sandbagging-then-delivering pattern. When the Suncoast renovation drag they had downplayed finally materialized, they said so plainly ("we'll let you know when we see it… and so we're seeing it"), even quantifying it ($1.5M in Q1, $2–3M per quarter ahead). The FanDuel proceeds were used exactly as promised — pay down debt below 2x, then return capital. Reliability: High.

## 5. Analyst Pressure Points

Analysts circled three themes repeatedly, and management's answers were honest but did not relieve the concern:
- **Las Vegas destination softness bleeding into the locals business.** Pushed every quarter from Q3 2025 on. Management insists it is contained to the Orleans and the IP Biloxi, but conceded a ~$6M locals shortfall in Q1 2026.
- **What to do with the post-FanDuel balance sheet.** Analysts pressed on capital allocation at 1.5x leverage; management deflected with "thoughtful discussions… could be M&A, could tick back up," giving no concrete plan beyond buybacks.
- **The locals-growth question.** Steve Wieczynski and James Hardiman pushed on whether locals can grow in 2026; management effectively walked the Street down to flat-to-negative.

## 6. Red Flags / Green Flags

🔴 Stock at the 97th percentile of its 52-week range and at an all-time high, with insiders selling (24 open-market sells, ~802k shares; zero buys) — no insider conviction at the top.
🔴 The "deep value" / 369%-margin-of-safety signal is a model artifact built on one-time FanDuel EPS; underlying free cash flow is *falling* (~−30% YoY).
🔴 Core Las Vegas (locals + downtown) is in a destination-driven slump; the growth is migrating to the Midwest/South, which is itself a "stay-close-to-home" recession-tell.
🔴 Online segment EBITDAR guidance cut roughly in half for 2026 after the FanDuel revenue-share reset.

🟢 Best balance sheet in company history (≤2x leverage) after a well-executed asset monetization.
🟢 Disciplined, credible management with an 86% beat rate and a long buyback track record (share count down ~33% over 4.5 years).
🟢 Genuinely diversified, cash-generative regional footprint with high operating leverage and a sensible $100M/yr growth-capex program at ~25% after-tax returns (Treasure Chest cited).

## 7. Hidden Signals

The terminology drift is the tell. "Destination business" went from an aside to a recurring excuse line. The repeated framing of weakness as "just the Orleans" and "just the IP" is a containment narrative analysts kept probing. Most telling: the pivot in the growth pitch from operational ("core customer growth") to financial ("$650M+ of annual capital return") — when a mature operator's headline becomes the buyback, it is signaling that organic growth is scarce. Management's own M&A commentary (sellers now demand a share of synergies; Boyd positioned as a buyer of any Caesars/MGM forced divestitures) frames Boyd as a consolidator, not a target.

## 8. Capital-Allocation Verdict (for the director)

**STABLE.** Boyd is a well-run, conservatively-financed, low-single-digit-growth regional casino operator that just executed a clean asset sale and is funneling the proceeds into buybacks. It is neither strengthening (organic growth is flat-to-soft and free cash flow is falling) nor deteriorating (the balance sheet is the strongest ever and management is credible).

**The single most important thing to weigh before sizing:** the entire "deep value" thesis is a measurement error. The stock is at an all-time high, trading essentially *at* its normalized DCF value and in line with analyst targets — the apparent 369% margin of safety vanishes the moment you strip the one-time FanDuel gain out of EPS. There is no mispricing to harvest here.

Moat: a regional casino operator's edge is a **regulatory-license + local-density** moat — real but narrow. Limited gaming licenses per jurisdiction and entrenched local-customer relationships (the 50-year Hawaii-to-Downtown pipeline) provide durable, if modest, protection. The evidence says the moat is *stable*, not widening: ROIC is a steady ~12% (not rising), gross margin is flagged contracting, and there is no Macau/Singapore growth premium. Secular threat is **manageable** — regional gaming faces a slow, partial substitution risk from iGaming/online (which management argues is complementary and in which it participates via Boyd Interactive), not a terminal-value impairment. This is a mature cyclical, not a melting ice cube.
