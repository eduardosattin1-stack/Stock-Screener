# AAUC — Allied Gold Corp. | Interrogator Dossier (Event-Driven Special Situation)

**Catalyst class:** Merger-arb / foreign-regulator close. **Super-cluster:** Deal-completion.
**As of:** 2026-06-22. Live $25.64 (Jun-18 close) / $25.65 pre-mkt Jun-22 · Deal terms C$44.00 cash · Target ≈ $31.08 USD (C$44 @ USD/CAD 1.4146) · Floor $23.00 · Hard date 2026-07-29 (37–42d).

This is NOT a quality/compounding thesis. AAUC is a single-asset-concentrated African gold miner being taken out for cash; the thesis is the **spread between $25.64 and the C$44 deal value, gated on the last remaining condition — China NDRC outbound-investment approval — firing before the July 29 outside date.** The franchise is irrelevant; the catalyst carries the trade. The dossier scores the EVENT, not the moat.

## 1. THE EVENT — IS IT REAL, DATED, AND BINDING?

**REAL & SIGNED.** On **2026-01-26**, Allied Gold entered a **definitive arrangement agreement** to be acquired by **Zijin Gold International** (the HK-listed gold arm of China's Zijin Mining) at **C$44.00/share in cash**, ~C$5.5B equity value, a **friendly, board-recommended, all-cash** offer — a 27% premium to the undisturbed 30-day average. This is a binding plan-of-arrangement (Canada), not a non-binding LOI. Court and shareholder approvals are obtained/non-issues for a friendly recommended cash deal; the gating items are regulatory.

**DATED.** Outside Date **extended to 2026-07-29** (from the original ~May-2026 target). Critically, per the 2026-05-29 release, **"any further extension of the Outside Date will require the parties' mutual agreement"** — i.e., July 29 is a real, mutually-hard wall, not a unilateral auto-roll. This is the forcing function.

**BINDING — but on ONE remaining third-party condition.** Approvals already SECURED: Investment Canada Act (completes the Canadian review), ECOWAS regional competition clearance, COMESA competition clearance, and African host-country approvals "obtained or in advanced stages." The **single load-bearing outstanding condition is China's NDRC** (National Development and Reform Commission) outbound-investment approval.

## 2. THE LOAD-BEARING RISK — NDRC

The deal's entire residual risk collapses to one binary: **does Beijing's NDRC clear Zijin's outbound capital?** Reporting (InvestingNews, NAI500, Ecofin, 2026-06-02) is explicit that NDRC **delayed** approval, questioning:
- the **27% premium** Zijin is paying (Beijing scrutinizing outbound M&A pricing/capital outflow), and
- **Mali political risk** — Mali accounts for **~half of Allied's gold output**, and Mali's military junta has arrested foreign mining executives and forcibly renegotiated contracts (Barrick/Resolute precedent). NDRC is reluctant to bless a full-price outbound buy of a half-Mali asset.

This is a **third-party, single-binary, sovereign-discretion** condition — the worst kind for an arb because it is not a checklist clearance but a judgment call by a regulator with non-economic (capital-control, geopolitical) motives. The May→July extension was driven by exactly this delay.

## 3. THE FLOOR — WHAT BACKSTOPS THE PRICE IF NDRC SAYS NO

On the **2026-06-02 delay news**, AAUC fell 6.5% to **C$37.31** (~$26.4 USD) — i.e., the market did NOT mark it to a deal-break floor; it stayed near deal value, signaling the base case is still "closes." The **break floor** is the standalone-miner value: AAUC produces gold into a **record-high gold price** environment, and its undisturbed pre-deal level was ~C$34–35 (the C$44 is a 27% premium on the 30-day avg). Standalone equity, derated for Mali risk but lifted by record bullion, supports a floor in the **low-to-mid-$20s USD**. The dossier's **$23.00** floor is defensible — roughly the undisturbed USD-equivalent with a Mali-risk haircut, and it is NOT zero: a break does not impair the underlying gold ounces, only strips the premium. There is no going-concern, no covenant breach, no ATM/dilution flagged; the company is cash-generative at current gold prices.
