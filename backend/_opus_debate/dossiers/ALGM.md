# Interrogator Forensic Dossier — Allegro MicroSystems (ALGM)

*Prepared as of 2026-06-22. Source: Q4/FY2026 earnings release & call (fiscal year ended March 27, 2026), Q1–Q4 FY2026 commentary, and live fundamentals/valuation. Transcript source: web. ALGM is a normally-listed Nasdaq equity (ticker ALGM); ~71% owned historically by Sanken Electric with a long-running secondary-sell-down overhang now largely worked through.*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

Allegro entered FY2026 as what the market filed under "automotive/industrial magnetic-sensor and power-IC supplier levered to xEV content" — a SAAR-plus content-growth story whose multiple lived and died on EV adoption and the auto inventory cycle. Across FY2026 that story did not so much shift as **acquire a second engine**: the data-center vertical went from a rounding error to the headline. Management's framing migrated from "we beat SAAR by 7–10 points on content" (still true and still repeated) to a **dual-narrative**: (1) the durable auto/industrial content compounder, plus (2) an emergent, fast-ramping AI-data-center power/sensing franchise.

The arithmetic of that pivot is the whole story. Data center **quadrupled in FY2026 to ~10% of total sales, hitting 14% of Q4 sales (+41% sequential)**, with Q1 FY2027 guided to **16–17% of sales (20–25% sequential growth)**. Within data center, the *current-sensor* sub-segment went from "virtually 0 entering FY'23" to **~20% of data-center revenue by Q4** — i.e., the company found a brand-new SAM (current sensing + isolated SiC gate drivers for 800V HVDC racks) that maps directly onto NVIDIA's published 800-VDC architecture roadmap. The load-bearing content claim: **"$150 per rack today → over $425 in next-generation AI configurations."**

The legacy engine did not get dropped — it strengthened. Total auto +17% in FY2026 (well above SAAR+7–10%); **Focused Auto (xEV/ADAS) +30%** to $349M; industrial & other +38%. TMR sensing (~30% of new product releases, including the "industry-first 10MHz TMR current sensor," EDN 2025 Sensor Product of the Year) and isolated SiC gate drivers each carry a **"2–3x dollar content uplift"** claim as customers move to 800V xEV platforms and steer-by-wire ADAS. So the arc is genuine *expansion*, not substitution: an old content-growth story that added a hyperscaler-capex optionality leg mid-year.

**Where the narrative is RICHER than the trajectory justifies:** the per-rack "$150 → $425" content figure and the data-center TAM are *opportunity* framing, not booked revenue — the stock is now priced as if a large fraction of that converts. **Where it is HONEST:** management explicitly flagged "**somewhat thin inventory in automotive**" with "**no clear signs of restocking at a broad level**," i.e., they did NOT pretend the auto cycle has fully turned. That candor is a credibility green flag.

**Note on stale third-party data:** some aggregators still show TTM revenue ~$725M and GAAP losses — that is the FY2025 trough lagging through TTM windows. The FY2026 release ($890M, non-GAAP profitable, $125M FCF) is the current state and is the correct basis.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rising.** The confidence build across FY2026 is unmistakable and, critically, *earned by the print*. Early-year language defended the content-over-SAAR thesis against a soft auto tape; by Q4 the CEO (Mike Doogue) is leading with data-center records and "multiyear-high backlog," and the CFO (Derek D'Antilio) is quantifying the current-sensor ramp ("virtually 0 entering FY'23 … ~20% of DC business in Q4"). The shift is from **defensive** (defend content growth through an inventory destock) to **offensive** (new TAM, content-per-rack, robotics design wins). Importantly the offense is *paired with* a defensive admission on auto restocking — confidence without bravado. No tonal red flags.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Medium-High.** Through FY2026, Allegro delivered into a sequentially improving guide and beat into Q4 ($243M actual vs a guide that implied low-$240s; non-GAAP EPS $0.17 vs Street ~$0.15). The margin trajectory (guided ~49–50%, delivered 49.4% FY / 50.0% Q4) was hit precisely despite the gold headwind — a sign the guide is built on real cost/utilization visibility, not hope. The Q1'27 guide ($245–255M, GM 50–51%, EPS $0.19–0.23, opex *down* sequentially) is internally consistent and not obviously sandbagged. The one structural caveat: a meaningful and rising share of the guide now leans on data-center sequential growth (16–17% of sales), a line with one year of history and high hyperscaler-capex beta — so guidance *reliability* is high but guidance *durability* is less tested. Reliability: **Medium-High**.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **Data-center durability / customer concentration:** analysts will (correctly) press on how much of the DC ramp is one or two hyperscaler programs, design-win-to-revenue conversion, and whether the "$425/rack" content is contracted or aspirational. Management quantifies the ramp but is light on named-customer disclosure.
- **Auto restocking timing:** management's own "no clear signs of restocking" is the honest answer to the recurring "is the auto cycle turning?" question — but it also means a chunk of the bull case (auto re-acceleration *on top of* DC) is not yet in hand.
- **Gold/input-cost pass-through and gross-margin ceiling:** the 200 bps gold headwind, absorbed this year, is a recurring pressure point on the path to a >52% GM target.
- **China/robotics optionality:** two Chinese humanoid-robotics joint design wins are a "watch-this-space" line analysts will want sized; today it is optionality, not a number.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🟢 **Margin expansion against a real cost headwind** (GM +140 bps *despite* −200 bps gold) — operational quality.
🟢 **Honest auto-restocking caveat** — management is not over-claiming the cycle.
🟢 **Record FCF + voluntary deleveraging + loan reprice** — disciplined capital allocation, clean balance sheet.
🟢 **Genuinely new TAM (DC current sensing / SiC gate drivers) mapped to NVIDIA 800-VDC roadmap** — not a re-labeled legacy line.
🟢 **TMR technology leadership** (10MHz TMR current sensor; ~30% of releases) — a durable, hard-to-copy intangible/IP edge in magnetic sensing.
🔴 **Valuation has fully discounted the optionality** — fwd P/E ~56x, EV/Sales ~12.5x, EV/EBITDA >100x on trailing; the stock "soared" alongside MPWR in June 2026. The AI-capex optionality is *in the price*.
🔴 **Single-factor dependence:** the marginal growth + the multiple both now hinge on AI-data-center capex durability — the same macro factor under most of the basket.
🔴 **Data-center revenue is one year old and lumpy** — a hyperscaler digestion pause would hit the highest-multiple part of the story first.
🔴 **Auto end-market still soft** — the legacy 71% of revenue is recovering off a trough, not booming; an auto air-pocket would undercut the base while the DC multiple is extended.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- The CFO's specific, unprompted quantification of the current-sensor ramp ("virtually 0 entering FY'23 → ~20% of DC by Q4") is a *deliberate* signal that the DC story is a current-sensing story, not just gate drivers — i.e., Allegro is leveraging its core magnetic-sensing IP (its true moat) into the new TAM, which is more defensible than competing head-on in commodity power switches.
- "Opex *down* sequentially" in the Q1'27 guide alongside +23% revenue is a quiet operating-leverage signal — management is choosing to let margin flow through rather than spend the upside.
- Robotics/humanoid design wins introduced as a *new* vocabulary item late in the year = the company is seeding the *next* optionality leg (sensing for actuators/joints) before the DC leg is even mature. Pattern of a management team that keeps finding adjacent content.
- The persistent "SAAR +7–10%" anchor, repeated even as DC takes over the headline, signals management still wants to be valued on *content*, not cycle — a tell that they know the auto tape is soft.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Trajectory: STRENGTHENING.** Accelerating revenue (+23%), expanding margins against a cost headwind, record FCF, active deleveraging, and a genuinely new, fast-ramping TAM grafted onto a durable sensing-IP core. This is a high-quality, well-run franchise hitting an up-cycle with a real AI-capex optionality leg.

**The single thing the director must weigh before sizing:** *the quality is real but the price has already paid for the optionality.* At ~56x forward earnings and ~12.5x EV/Sales, ALGM is priced for the data-center content ramp to convert AND the auto cycle to re-accelerate AND margins to keep climbing — a triple. The marginal buyer is underwriting AI-capex durability, the exact shared macro factor saturating the rest of the scale-out basket. The risk is not the business; it is that there is little margin of safety and the thesis is not resolution-independent.

**Moat:** Magnetic sensing (Hall/TMR) is a genuine **intangible/IP + design-in switching-cost** moat — automotive and now hyperscaler power designs qualify Allegro's sensors into multi-year platforms, and TMR leadership is hard to replicate. But it is a **NARROW** moat: in raw power switching / gate drivers Allegro competes against larger, deeper-pocketed players (ON, Infineon, TI), and its returns are cyclically recovering, not durably high (ROIC is normalizing off a trough, not compounding above peers). The moat is real where the IP is (sensing) and thin where it is scaling fastest (power).

**Moat trend: STABLE-to-widening on the sensing IP** (TMR releases, content uplift), but I will not call it WIDENING company-wide because the fastest-growing dollars (DC power) are in the more contested layer.

**Secular threat: NONE/manageable.** ALGM is a *beneficiary* of electrification + AI-power build-out, not a victim of a secular-decline force. The real risk is **cyclical (AI-capex digestion + auto cycle)**, not terminal. Cheapness is not the question here — the opposite; this is a quality name whose terminal value is intact but whose *price* embeds the optionality.
