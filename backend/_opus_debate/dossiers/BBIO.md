## 0. CATALYST CORRECTION (load-bearing)
The Catalyst-Watch board labeled the load-bearing event "acoramidis PDUFA." **That is wrong and must be corrected.** Acoramidis (Attruby / BEYONTTRA) is **already approved and commercially launched** in the U.S., E.U., Japan, Switzerland, Brazil, and U.K. — it is the *revenue engine*, not a pending decision. The actual hard-dated **2026-11-27 PDUFA** belongs to **BBP-418 (ribitol prodrug) for LGMD2I/R9** (limb-girdle muscular dystrophy type 2I/R9). I verified this on the BridgeBio 2026-05-27 press release (GlobeNewswire): "FDA Acceptance and Priority Review of NDA for BBP-418," **PDUFA target action date November 27, 2026**, Priority Review granted, **no advisory committee planned** (an incremental positive). The catalyst is REAL and DATED — it is simply a *different drug* than the board recorded.

## 1. IS THE CATALYST REAL, DATED, AND BINDING?
- **Real & dated:** Yes. NDA accepted, Priority Review, hard PDUFA **2026-11-27** (163 days out, inside window). No AdCom scheduled — removes a public-vote wildcard and slightly de-risks the decision.
- **Clinical backing:** BBP-418's Phase 3 **FORTIFY** study read out **positive** (2025), and the company filed off a met-endpoint package. This is *not* a coin-flip pre-data binary — the pivotal data already cleared, so the PDUFA is a regulatory-approval gate on a positive trial, which is why win_prob 0.85 is defensible (approval-given-positive-Ph3-priority-review base rates run high). First-and-only therapy for LGMD2I/R9, ~7,000 patients U.S.+EU, clear unmet need.
- **Binding:** A PDUFA date is an FDA commitment, not a management aspiration. Slippage risk exists (CRL / minor extension) but the date is institutional, not self-imposed.
