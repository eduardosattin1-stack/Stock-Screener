# Interrogator Forensic Dossier — onsemi (ON)
*Scale-out Basket D (PWR-SEMI). In-universe rationale: SiC. Prepared as Opus 4.8, capital-allocation grade. Sources: Q1 2026 earnings call + release (2026-05-04), FY2025 results, live market data (2026-06-22).*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

onsemi spent 2023–2025 as a *defensive* story: a SiC + automotive/industrial power-analog franchise riding out the worst auto-semi inventory correction in a decade. For roughly seven consecutive quarters automotive revenue *declined*, industrial was weak, and the entire narrative was "structural cost actions, fab rationalization (FabRight), exit non-core, protect gross margin through the trough." Revenue fell from a ~$8.3B peak (2023) to ~$6.0B (FY2025). Management's refrain was utilization discipline and FCF defense — they deliberately under-shipped to hold price.

Q1 2026 is the pivot quarter — and management says so explicitly: CEO El-Khoury calls it *"a clear inflection point."* Three threads turned at once: (1) **automotive returned to YoY growth (+5%)** for the first time after seven down quarters, led by SiC content gains in China (~55% SiC share of new EV models at the April 2026 Beijing Auto Show); (2) **AI data center revenue grew >30% sequentially, nearly double the high-teens management had guided**, and is now expected to *double YoY in 2026* off a 2025 base that itself doubled; (3) **gross margin expanded a third straight quarter to 38.5%** even in a seasonally-down quarter. The story has shifted from "survive the trough and protect margin" to "inflection + a brand-new AI-data-center growth leg layered onto the recovering auto-SiC core." Notably, the AI-data-center leg was barely mentioned 18 months ago and is now the lead headline — a genuine thesis *expansion*, not a repackaging.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rising — decisively, and for the first time in two years.** Through 2024–25 the tone was defensive ("disciplined," "protect," "trough," "under-ship"). Q1'26 flips to offensive: *"clear inflection point," "accelerating," "nearly double our expected growth rate," "we are the preferred power solution," "all leading hyperscalers."* The CEO is now naming specific design wins (Treo/T1S Ethernet for a North American zonal architecture; Geely/NIO 900V; FlexPower >30 active programs). The CFO pairs it with hard operating metrics (utilization 77%, lead times 26wk, opex −7%) rather than vague reassurance. This is a credibility-positive shift: confidence is rising *and* being tethered to specifics.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Medium-High.** Q1'26 beat the guidance midpoint on revenue ($1.513B) and EPS ($0.64). The AI-data-center segment *materially* over-delivered (30% vs high-teens guide) — i.e., they sandbagged the hottest line, which is the *good* kind of miss-to-the-upside. Q2 guide ($1.535–1.635B, EPS $0.65–0.77) implies a return to sequential growth and continued margin expansion. The multi-year >50% GM bridge is the one piece to hold skeptically — it depends on volume recovery + mix + fab divestitures the company does not fully control. Reliability on the *near-term* line: **High.** On the *long-term margin target:* **Medium.**

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

The recurring tension is **"how big and how durable is AI data center, really?"** Management gives growth rates (30% q/q, 2x y/y) but *not the absolute dollar base* — a deliberate non-disclosure that lets the story run while keeping the denominator vague. Second pressure point: **the >50% gross-margin bridge** — analysts will keep pressing on the volume assumption embedded in it (38.5% at 77% utilization implies a lot of operating leverage *if* volumes return, but that's a cyclical bet). Third: **auto-SiC pricing in China** — 55% design share is impressive, but China EV is a brutal price war; analysts worry content gains get offset by ASP erosion. One Seeking Alpha note already flagged ON as *"overbought ahead of a slower fundamental recovery"* — i.e., the stock re-rated faster than the fundamentals.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🟢 Insider/buyback signal: $346M repurchased at avg **$60.54** vs ~$122 now — management bought the bottom; few clearer value tells.
🟢 Margin up on *falling* utilization (77%) = structural, not cyclical, cost win; huge operating leverage if volume returns.
🟢 Auto inflection real (+5% YoY after 7 down quarters) and SiC design share ~55% at Beijing 2026.
🟢 AI-data-center optionality genuine: all major hyperscalers, >30 FlexPower programs, doubling-on-doubling.
🟢 Record FY25 FCF $1.4B / 24% margin through the *trough* — cash machine even at the bottom.
🔴 GAAP losses (Q1 GAAP EPS −$0.08; FY25 GAAP op margin 1.4%) — restructuring/impairment heavy; the clean story is non-GAAP only.
🔴 AI-DC base undisclosed — the load-bearing growth line has no published denominator.
🔴 Stock +117% in 12 months and re-rated to ~28–40x forward — the cheap-trough entry is gone; much optionality now *priced in*.
🔴 China auto-SiC is a price-war end-market; share ≠ durable margin.
🔴 Sequential revenue still −1% in Q1 — inflection is asserted on YoY + order signals, not yet on sequential dollars.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

The vocabulary migrated from *"fab,"* *"utilization,"* *"trough"* (a manufacturing-defense lexicon) toward *"PowerTree,"* *"FlexPower,"* *"XPU vendors,"* *"hyperscalers,"* *"zonal architecture,"* *"software-defined vehicle"* — a deliberate repositioning from cyclical power-discrete supplier toward *secular AI-and-EV power-systems* company. The framing of capital allocation also hardened: explicit *"return 100% of FCF over time"* + buying at $60 = a management team that thinks its stock was cheap and says so with cash. The CFO's note that non-core exits ("$50M Q1, $30–40M Q2, done in 2026") are *ending* signals the portfolio-pruning phase is closing and the growth phase opening.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**STRENGTHENING.** Operationally and in capital discipline this is a clearly improving franchise: margin expanding through the trough, record FCF, value-accretive buybacks at the bottom, a real auto-SiC inflection, and a credible (if small) AI-data-center growth leg attached to every major hyperscaler power tree. The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing: **the stock has already done the re-rating** (+117% in a year to ~28–40x forward, ~$48B cap) — the trough-value entry buybacks captured at $60 is gone, and much of the AI-data-center optionality is now in the price. This is no longer a cheap stock; it is a good business at a full multiple whose thesis now *requires* the AI-capex leg to keep compounding.

**Moat:** Scale-cost + intangible (design-in switching costs) in SiC/power-analog — onsemi is one of two vertically-integrated SiC leaders (vs STM), with a real share lead and substrate-to-module integration. ROIC ~11–12% (FY25, trough-depressed) is *below* the >15% that would mark a wide moat, but it is *cyclically* depressed and *rising* off the trough with margins. The moat is real but **NARROW** (commodity-adjacent power discretes; intense Chinese SiC entrants — BYD-affiliated, StarPower, etc. — and STM/Infineon competition) and the *trend* is improving (margin + design-share gains). **MOAT_TREND: STABLE-to-widening** — call it STABLE pending durable ROIC > WACC.

**Secular threat:** None terminal — onsemi sits on the *winning* side of two seculars (EV electrification + AI-data-center power). The relevant structural risk is *competitive* (Chinese SiC commoditization compressing the margin bridge), not demand-displacement. **SECULAR_THREAT: manageable.** No secular-decline theme from the taxonomy applies (it is an energy-transition *winner*, not a loser).
