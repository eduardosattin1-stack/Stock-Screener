## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

Modine has executed one of the cleanest portfolio pivots in industrials. Two years ago this was an 80/20 legacy thermal-management supplier — heat exchangers for trucks, off-highway, autos, HVAC — carrying a single-digit EBITDA multiple as a cyclical Tier-1. The "80/20" operating system (segment-rationalize, exit low-margin, redeploy to high-growth) under CEO Neil Brinker reframed the story around **Climate Solutions**, and inside it, **Data Centers** (the Airedale-by-Modine chiller / CDU / coil franchise, built on the 2021 Airedale acquisition and bolt-ons).

The arc has only steepened: data-center revenue went from a rounding error to **>$1B in FY2026** (~30%+ of group), grew **158% YoY in Q4 FY2026**, and management now guides **+60–80% in FY2027** toward a **$2B FY2028 target** at ~80% utilization on ~$2.5B of installed capacity. The story has *expanded*, not contracted — and the company has put structural proof points behind the words: a **$4B long-term capacity agreement (CY2027–29) with a hyperscale customer, with $165M cash upfront** (2026-05-26), and a **Reverse Morris Trust spin of Performance Technologies into Gentherm** (announced 2026-01-29) that converts MOD into a **pure-play data-center + commercial HVAC&R** company. The legacy "off-highway cyclical" frame is being deliberately jettisoned.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rising.** No bundled transcript was provided, so per-quarter quotes cannot be reproduced verbatim; from the dated releases the posture is unambiguously **offensive** — guidance has been *raised twice* during FY26, the DC target has *moved up* (to $2B FY28), and management committed real capital (new Franklin WI facility; $2.5B capacity plan) and signed binding customer commitments rather than hedging. This is growth/investment language, not the efficiency/cost/macro defensive register. The only defensive note is the deliberate *shedding* of the cyclical Performance Tech book — which is itself an offensive portfolio move, not retreat.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**High.** Four consecutive earnings beats; Q4 FY26 adj EPS $1.71 beat the $1.51 consensus by ~13%; full-year delivered a 4th straight record across the three headline metrics. Management has *raised* DC guidance twice intra-year and still beat — a sandbag-and-deliver pattern, not aspiration-without-execution. The one open credibility question (flagged by Barchart): the FY28 $2B DC target embeds growth "that has never shown up in guidance — yet," i.e. it is a stated *ambition* above the formal guide. But the $4B/$165M-upfront contract converts a chunk of that ambition into contracted backlog, which materially de-risks it. Reliability: **High.**

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- The **$165M upfront + $210M RMT cash** is the tell that management is de-risking the capital plan with *other people's money* — customer pre-funds capacity, spin throws off cash. That is sophisticated, founder-grade capital allocation and signals the customer's own conviction in the demand.
- Performance Technologies revenue went *flat-to-down* (−3% FY26) — the spin is being timed to exit a decelerating book at ~6.8x post-synergy EBITDA precisely as the DC book inflects. Clean sequencing.
- "Airedale by Modine" branding consolidation = the moat is being built on a *named product franchise* (CDUs, chillers, fan-walls, coils — full thermal stack incl. liquid cooling), not commodity heat exchangers. That is the difference between an eroding Tier-1 and a spec'd-in component supplier.

**Single most important thing to weigh before sizing:** how much of the FY28 $2B DC ramp is *already* in the ~38x forward multiple and the 3.3x run off the low. This is a *quality* story whose **price has caught up to the thesis**; the asymmetry is gone at the top of the range. The catalysts that drive a re-rate (the $4B deal, the spin announcement) have *already fired into the tape* — the deal print added 17% in a day. Sizing must respect that you are paying a full multiple for a name whose entire thesis shares ONE resolution driver (AI-capex) with the rest of the basket.

**Secular threat:** MANAGEABLE / none on terminal value — MOD is a *beneficiary* of the dominant secular force (AI/data-center thermal demand), not a victim. The risk is **cyclical/over-build (AI-capex digestion)**, not structural erosion of terminal value. The cheapness question is moot — it is *not* cheap; the risk is a fully-priced beneficiary, not a value trap.
