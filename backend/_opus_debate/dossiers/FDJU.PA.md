# Forensic Dossier — FDJ United (FDJU.PA)

*A deep dive on management and the setup, built from three consecutive results calls (March 2025 full-year 2024, July 2025 half-year, February 2026 full-year 2025) cross-checked against the screener's hard financials.*

## 1. Narrative Arc

The story FDJ United tells about itself changed character completely over these three calls, and the change is the whole point.

In **March 2025**, the tone was triumphal and offensive. Management had just rebranded the old French lottery monopoly (Française des Jeux) into "FDJ United," a self-styled "European champion in the gaming industry," on the back of the debt-funded takeover of Kindred (the Unibet/32Red online betting group). The headline framing was growth, diversification, and a hard-won win: the European Commission's multi-year state-aid investigation into the fee FDJ paid for its exclusive French lottery rights had just closed with "no state aid," costing only a €97 million adjustment already booked. Chairwoman Stéphane Pallez called it closing "this uncertainty about the risk that we had." That was the load-bearing catalyst of the old bull case — and it had already fired.

By **July 2025**, the language pivoted hard from offense to defense. Revenue on a like-for-like basis (as if Kindred had always been owned) was already *down 2%*, and the new vocabulary was "transition year," "cost efficiency plan," "performance plan," and "regulatory headwinds" in the UK and Netherlands. Management introduced the "Play Forward 2028" plan with a softened medium-term goal of ~5% organic revenue growth by 2028 (i.e. not now).

By **February 2026**, the defensive crouch was complete. Pallez openly conceded 2025 was "significantly impacted by major adverse factors" — tax increases in France, the Netherlands and Romania worth "more than €50 million" — and described Dutch regulation as "ill considered." The growth-driver language of March 2025 (omni-channel accounts, new retail banners, EuroMillions momentum) is still present but now functions as reassurance, not as a thesis. The single most-emphasized number became the **cost-efficiency plan, raised from €120M to €150M of recurring savings by 2028** — a margin-defense story replacing a growth story.

The cleanest single read: revenue up 20% YoY, EPS down 56% YoY. When the top line grows that fast and the bottom line falls that hard, the company is keeping less and less of every euro it takes in. That is the fingerprint of regulatory-rent compression, not a temporary stumble.

## 3. Tone & Confidence Trajectory

**Declining.**

- Mar-25: confident, expansive — "we want to be a European champion," dividend raised 15% to €2.05.
- Jul-25: hedged — "definitely confident in our capacity to achieve our full year guidance" (the repeated word "confident" is itself a tell; you reassure when you are being doubted), revenue already −2% restated.
- Feb-26: damage-control — "fair to recognize… our results were significantly impacted by major adverse factors," and the 2026 margin target was subsequently cut from 24.5% to a **23–24% range** (per the April 2026 Q1 release). Language shifted decisively from growth/investment to efficiency/cost/discipline.

The confidence is being *transferred forward in time* — to 2027 and 2028, when the tech-platform migration (KSP) finishes and the cost savings fully land. Everything good is always "more heavily in 2027" or "by 2028."

## 4. Guidance Credibility

**Medium, trending down.** The screener shows a 75% earnings-beat rate (3 of 4 quarters), so they hit their own bars more often than not — but the bars themselves keep dropping. The 2025 full-year guidance was technically met ("EBITDA margin over 24%," dividend raised to €2.10), yet the company quietly walked the 2026 margin target down to 23–24% at Q1 2026 and now guides a *slight revenue decline* for 2026. Crucially, the €20M cost-savings target set in March 2025 was raised to ~€50M actually delivered for 2025 — genuine over-delivery on costs. So credibility on the controllable (cost-cutting) is high; credibility on the uncontrollable (revenue, tax) is low, because the external environment keeps overrunning the plan. The pattern is honest disclosure of bad news, not sandbagging.

## 5. Analyst Pressure Points

Three topics recur and management repeatedly soft-pedals them:

1. **The gap between growth ambition and delivery.** CIC's Christian Devismes (Mar-25) pushed directly on why 2025 growth (~2% ex-tax) is "below the long-term guidance between 4% to 5%." Management blamed comparables and tax — fair, but the gap persisted and widened.
2. **When the cost savings actually show up.** Bernstein's Sabrina Blanc and Morgan Stanley's Ed Young pressed on timing; the honest answer was always "2026 beginning… more heavily in 2027." The benefit is real but deferred well past the current pain.
3. **Online casino legalization in France.** Pallez (Jul-25) flatly judged a re-opened consultation "rather unlikely… given the political context." Analysts kept probing because legal French online casino would be a genuine new growth vertical — and management is signaling it is *not* coming. Meanwhile the government did the opposite of legalizing: it **raised** the online-casino tax to 40%.

## 6. Red Flags / Green Flags

🔴 EPS collapsed 56% YoY (to €0.95) while revenue grew 20% — the rent is being taxed away.
🔴 Net debt ~€1.47B where the balance sheet was net cash before Kindred; first-ever bond issuance.
🔴 Escalating, permanent, multi-country tax hikes — France online casino 21%→40%, levies up across the board — with ~€90M further 2026 drag guided.
🔴 Margin target cut (24.5% → 23–24%) at Q1 2026; 2026 revenue guided to *decline slightly*.
🔴 UK Kindred revenue −24%, Netherlands −20% on regulation/tax; the acquired online business is the weakest part.
🔴 The headline catalyst (EU state-aid clearance) already fired in October 2024 — the re-rating it could have caused is spent.

🟢 The core French lottery (LSF) is a genuine regulatory monopoly with ~36% EBITDA margin, 6% iLottery growth, and quasi-utility stability — a real, durable franchise.
🟢 Cost-efficiency over-delivery: €20M target → ~€50M delivered in 2025; €150M cumulative target by 2028.
🟢 Dividend raised again to €2.10 (80% payout) and leverage already below the 2.0x ceiling — they are deleveraging on schedule.
🟢 Sales-force internalization and tech-stack (KSP) migration are structural margin levers that genuinely complete by 2027.

## 7. Hidden Signals

- The rebrand from "Française des Jeux" (a French national institution) to "FDJ United" (a pan-European betting group) is itself a tell: management chose to dilute the safest, highest-margin asset they own — a state lottery monopoly — by stapling it to a lower-margin, heavily-regulated, tax-exposed online betting business. The diversification narrative masks a *mix shift toward worse economics*.
- The word "happily" slipped out (Feb-26) when the CFO noted UK revenue is 85% online casino — said just before the new UK casino tax bites hardest there. A small, revealing stumble.
- Repeated deferral language ("2027," "2028," "by the end of 2027") across all three calls signals the team itself does not expect the numbers to look good in the near term.
- Management's near-relief that they are "not at all in the sports betting U.S. market" — framed as prudence — also quietly admits FDJ United has no exposure to the one large growth market its online peers are chasing.

## 8. Capital-Allocation Verdict (for the director)

**Trajectory: DETERIORATING (with a durable core).** This is a high-quality lottery monopoly being slowly diluted and out-run by a permanent, escalating tax/regulatory assault on the gaming sector it chose to expand into. The single most important thing to weigh before sizing: **the headline cheapness (≈7.4x price-to-free-cash-flow) is priced against a base of earnings that is structurally shrinking on the taxed parts, not a temporary dip — so the discount must be earned segment by segment, not taken at face value.**

**Economic moat: NARROW, and the trend is ERODING.** The moat *type* is a regulatory license — the French lottery concession is a true legal monopoly (genuinely wide and durable on that segment alone). But the consolidated entity's returns are unmistakably falling (EPS −56% YoY, gross margin contracting, ROIC high but declining), because management bolted a thin-moat, tax-exposed online betting business onto the monopoly. On the blended evidence the durable earnings base is narrow and being compressed.

**Secular threat: MATERIAL (not yet terminal).** The structural force is *regulatory-rent / reimbursement-style compression* — governments across France, the UK, the Netherlands and Romania are legislating the tax take on gross gaming revenue sharply higher (French online casino 21%→40% in a single step), permanently lowering the cash each euro of stakes returns to operators. This is the gaming-sector analogue of healthcare reimbursement cuts: a policy-driven, cohort-wide de-rating, not a tech disruption and not a cyclical dip. The lottery monopoly insulates terminal value from being *destroyed*, which is why this is material rather than terminal — but the cheapness is substantially a sector-wide fear-print shared with every regulated gaming peer, not idiosyncratic single-name alpha.
