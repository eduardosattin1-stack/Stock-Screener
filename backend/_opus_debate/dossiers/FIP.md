# FIP — FTAI Infrastructure Inc. | Interrogator Dossier (Event-Driven Special Situation)

**Frame:** This is NOT a quality/compounding judgment. FIP is a cheap-and-bad-by-design holdco — negative total equity (−$303.1M as of 3/31/26), $3.8B total debt, a Q1 2026 net loss of −$127.2M (driven by 9.75% term-loan interest, a $45.9M loss on debt extinguishment, and preferred dividend accretion). The thesis is the SPREAD between the live equity price and the deleveraging-resolved NAV, gated on a HARD-DATED, BINDING corporate event: the sale of Long Ridge Energy & Power to MARA Holdings.

## 1. THE CATALYST — IS IT REAL, DATED, AND BINDING?

**REAL & SIGNED.** On **April 30, 2026**, FIP announced a **definitive agreement** to sell Long Ridge Energy & Power LLC to a subsidiary of **MARA Holdings (NASDAQ: MARA)** for a total transaction value of **~$1.52 billion**. This is not a hoped-for refinancing or a process — it is an executed purchase agreement with a named, well-capitalized strategic buyer (MARA is a large-cap bitcoin-miner pivoting to digital-infrastructure / data-center power; it has publicly framed Long Ridge as a "premier digital infrastructure campus" — strong strategic motivation to close).

**DATED.** Expected to close **Q3 2026** (i.e., by ~September 30, 2026 — the dossier's 105-day hard date is corroborated). MARA has already launched a noteholder consent solicitation tied to financing the deal — a concrete, money-on-the-table step that signals intent to close, not optionality.

**BINDING, with a financing-contingency caveat.** Closing is subject to customary conditions: **FERC approval, HSR clearance, Surface Transportation Board** coordination on related railroad agreements, plus MARA's consent solicitation. The one live wrinkle: on **May 29, 2026**, the PJM Interconnection's independent market monitor urged FERC to approve only if MARA pledges to keep the plant's output in PJM markets. This is a **conditioning request, not opposition** — it can delay or attach undertakings, but does not threaten deal economics for FIP (FIP is the seller; the price is fixed). Base case: this slips timing by weeks, not quarters, and does not change the $1.52B consideration.

## 2. THE FLOOR — WHAT ACTUALLY BACKSTOPS THE PRICE?

The verified downside floor is the **52-week low of $3.90** (stock currently $4.81; range $3.90–$7.935). This is the empirical clearing level the forced-seller / negative-equity supply found before the deal de-risked the balance sheet. Below it sits the deleveraging math: pro forma for Long Ridge, **total debt falls from $3.814B to $2.377B** (~$1.4B eliminated: ~$1.15B asset-level + ~$300M parent), corporate leverage drops from **9.5x to 7.4x parent cash flow** (near-term target 5.1x), and annual corporate fixed charges fall from $132M to $103M. The floor is real because the deal *removes the going-concern overhang* that produced the $3.90 print — a deal-break would re-expose it, but the signed-and-financed status makes break risk low.
