# GE Vernova (GEV) — Interrogator Forensic Dossier

*Prepared as of 2026-06-22. Source: GEV Q1 2026 earnings call (2026-04-22), Q1 2026 8-K / 10-Q, FY2025 results, investor materials, live market data. No bundled multi-quarter transcript pack was supplied; this dossier reconstructs the narrative arc from the Q1 2026 call plus prior-period guidance/results found on the web, and flags where a claim could not be cross-referenced to a full transcript series.*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

GE Vernova spun out of GE in April 2024 as the "energy" leg — Power (gas/nuclear/hydro), Wind (onshore/offshore), and Electrification (grid, transmission, transformers, power-conversion). The arc since the spin is one of the cleanest narrative *strengthenings* in the large-cap industrial complex:

- **2024 (spin year):** the story was "a fixed-it-up turnaround" — kill the loss-making offshore wind book, harvest the gas-turbine installed base via services, and prove the conglomerate's three legs could stand alone. Skeptics framed it as a wind-loss cleanup with a gas cash-cow attached.
- **2025:** the narrative *expanded* decisively into the AI-electricity thesis. Gas-turbine slot reservations became the headline KPI; "the grid is the bottleneck" replaced "fix the wind book" as the lead. FY2025 revenue ~$38B, FCF ~$3.75B (vs an initial $2.0–2.5B guide — a >50% beat), and management began routinely raising guidance.
- **Q1 2026 (most recent):** the story is now unambiguously *the physical-layer beneficiary of AI/datacenter capex*. Orders $18.3B (+71% organic), book-to-bill ~2.0x, backlog $163B with the $200B target pulled forward a full year (2027 vs 2028). Datacenter electrification orders of $2.4B in one quarter exceeded *all of FY2025's* datacenter orders. Gas contracts under reservation went 83→100 GW in the quarter; slot reservations 43→56 GW. Pricing on new 2026 orders is "10 to 20 points higher … on a dollar per kW basis" vs Q4 2025.

What was *quietly dropped*: the offshore-wind growth story is gone — Wind is now openly a managed-decline/loss-contain segment (~$400M EBIT loss guided for 2026). What was *added*: nuclear/SMR (BWRX-300 at OPG Darlington under construction; up-to-$40B US/Japan SMR support; Clinch River NRC license expected 2026) and "integrated EMS" (generation + substation + automation in one order) as a genuine new growth vector.

**Caveat / the one number to handle carefully:** Q1 2026 *net income* of $4.75B (vs $264M PY) is NOT operating earnings — it is dominated by a discrete item (deferred-tax / valuation-allowance release of the type common post-spin). TTM net income of ~$4.9B therefore makes trailing P/E (~32x) and trailing EV/EBITDA (~85–115x across data vendors) **misleadingly cheap and misleadingly expensive respectively**. The honest gauge is **forward**: on the raised 2026 guide (~$5.4–6.4B adj. EBITDA, FCF $6.5–7.5B), EV ~$292B implies ~46–54x forward EV/EBITDA and ~40–52x forward P/E. That is a growth-industrial multiple, not a value one.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rising — emphatically.** The progression from "fix the wind book" (2024) → "the grid is the constraint" (2025) → "we are raising every line of guidance and pulling the $200B backlog target forward a year" (Q1'26) is a textbook offensive-language escalation. CFO Parks volunteering that "April Power equipment orders ≈ the entire Q1 booking" is a confidence tell — management rarely front-runs the next quarter's order print unless the pipeline is overflowing. The *one* defensive note is Wind: Parks "difficult to call an inflection point in U.S. orders … permitting delays and tariff uncertainty" — honest, contained, and ring-fenced from the bull thesis.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**High.** The pattern is consistent under-promise / over-deliver: FY2025 FCF guided $2.0–2.5B, delivered $3.75B; 2025 segment margins guided conservatively then beaten; 2026 guidance has now been *raised* one quarter in. Slot-reservation and order KPIs have converted to backlog on schedule. There is a mild **sandbagging pattern** — the initial post-spin guides were deliberately low to rebuild credibility — which is the *good* kind of credibility risk. Reliability: **High.**

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **Gas-turbine slot conversion durability:** analysts repeatedly probe whether 56 GW of *reservations* convert to firm contracts and at what cancellation risk. Management answers with the 83→100 GW conversion and the Q2 "10–15 GW" guide — well-addressed so far, but this is the line item the bears will attack first if AI capex wobbles.
- **Datacenter concentration / 2027–2028 air-pocket:** the obvious unasked-loudly question is what the order book looks like if hyperscaler capex digests in 2027. Management leans on the $163B backlog and the 2027 $200B target as the buffer.
- **Wind drag:** persistent, but management has successfully reframed it as a known, bounded ~$400M annual headwind rather than an open-ended hole.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🟢 Book-to-bill ~2.0x with backlog visible to 2027 — multi-year revenue is largely *de-risked*.
🟢 +10–20% pricing on new orders flowing straight to segment margins — pricing power is real and showing in the P&L.
🟢 ~$6B net cash, $1.3B/qtr buyback, raised FCF guide — balance sheet is a fortress, not a constraint.
🟢 Nuclear/SMR optionality (BWRX-300 under construction, Clinch River NRC license expected 2026) is free in the model.
🔴 Wind remains a structural loss segment (~$400M EBIT loss 2026) with no called inflection.
🔴 Single-factor exposure: the order surge, pricing, and the entire re-rate are **one shared macro driver — AI/datacenter electricity demand.** A hyperscaler capex guide-down would hit GEV through the same channel as every other scale-out name.
🔴 Q1 net income flatters trailing multiples (discrete tax item) — naive screens will misprice the stock both ways.
🔴 The stock has *already run* — +132% off the 52-week low ($479→$1,113), within 6% of its all-time high. Much of the optionality is priced.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- The shift from "slot reservations" to "GW under contract" as the headline KPI is a deliberate hardening of the disclosure — management is steering the street toward the *contracted* (harder) number, a sign they expect it to keep beating.
- R&D +25% YoY with the largest dollars going to **Electrification** (EMS, solid-state/stability blocks, solid-state transformers) tells you where management sees the durable moat — the grid-software/power-electronics layer, not the gas turbine. This is the 800V-DC / power-conversion adjacency the scale-out thesis cares about.
- "Integrated EMS" first order (generation + substation + automation bundled) is a quiet move up the value chain toward systems-integration lock-in — a switching-cost moat in the making, distinct from the installed-base services moat.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Classification: STRENGTHENING.** Every operating, order, margin, cash, and capital-return line is moving the right way, guidance is being raised, and the backlog de-risks revenue to 2027. The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing: **how much of the AI-electricity optionality is already in the price** — the stock is up >130% off its low, trades at ~46–54x forward EV/EBITDA, and its thesis depends on the *same* AI-capex factor as the rest of the scale-out basket, so it adds little resolution-driver independence.

**Moat:** Wide and *widening* — the gas-turbine installed base (3,000+ GW, multi-decade services annuity), an oligopoly in HV transformers / HVDC / grid equipment (GEV / Siemens Energy / Hitachi Energy), genuine pricing power (+10–20%) flowing to *rising* segment ROIC and *expanding* margins (+390–590 bps), and a building systems-integration/EMS switching-cost layer. This is the textbook profile of a *widening* moat (rising returns + expanding margins), not an eroding one.

**Secular threat: none (it is the secular *winner*).** GEV sits on the right side of electrification/energy-transition — it is the picks-and-shovels supplier to AI-datacenter power AND grid decarbonization. The structural force here *expands* its terminal value rather than impairing it. The real risk is **cyclical** (AI-capex digestion), not secular/terminal.
