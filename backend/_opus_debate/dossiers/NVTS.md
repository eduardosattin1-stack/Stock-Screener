# Interrogator Forensic Dossier — Navitas Semiconductor (NVTS)

*Prepared for the investment committee · scale-out Basket D (PWR-SEMI) · data as of 2026-06-22. No transcript bundle was provided; this dossier is built from the live Q1-FY2026 earnings release + call transcript (reported 2026-05-06), the NVIDIA collaboration disclosures (2025-05-21 and 2026-06-03), and the May/June-2026 ATM equity filings, all retrieved via web. Where a multi-quarter narrative arc is asserted it is sourced from the single live transcript plus dated press releases — not from a bundled 8-quarter set, and that limitation is flagged.*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

Navitas has executed a hard, deliberate pivot. The legacy story (through 2024) was a pure-play **GaN fast-charging** supplier levered to mobile phones, laptops and consumer chargers — a commoditizing, China-exposed, price-deflating end market. That base is now in structural run-off: Q1-FY2026 revenue of **$8.6M was down ~38% YoY** (from ~$14.0M in Q1-FY2025) precisely *because* management is walking away from low-end mobile/consumer.

The current story is **"Navitas 2.0"** — a re-platforming around **high-power** applications: AI data-center power delivery, grid/solid-state transformers, EV/industrial electrification. The marquee narrative pillar is the **NVIDIA 800V HVDC collaboration**: Navitas was named (2025-05-21) as a partner on NVIDIA's next-gen 800V high-voltage DC data-center architecture, and on **2026-06-03** was named into the **NVIDIA MGX / "Kyber" rack-scale ecosystem** powering Rubin-Ultra-class GPUs, using both GaNFast (GaN) and GeneSiC (SiC). New CEO Chris Allexandre frames Q1 as "the first clear proof point" of the pivot. The growth drivers *introduced*: 800V-to-6V DC-DC power-delivery boards, 1.2kV Gen-5 SiC, 250kW solid-state transformer. The driver *quietly dropped*: mobile, which management says becomes "insignificant" by year-end, leaving "almost an entirely high-power company." Management sizes the prize at a **$3.5B SAM by 2030 at 60%+ CAGR**.

This is a credible strategic logic — but the arc is a **promise, not yet a P&L**. The 800V data-center revenue is explicitly back-half-2026/early-2027 ("800 volt is happening… at the end of the year, early next year").

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rising / offensive** — but rising on *design-win optionality*, not delivered results. The new CEO's language is forward and promotional: "when you get to 5,000 samples, it's not samples, it's [pre-]production build… I'm comfortable in the momentum." Heavy use of efficiency superlatives ("98%+," "97.5% peak," "50% power-density increase"). This is classic pre-revenue ramp framing. Notably *defensive* where it counts: management declines to quantify design-win revenue, customer names, or concentration. Single-quarter view only (no bundled multi-quarter set) caps confidence in the *trajectory* read — flagged.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

Limited track record to score against in the provided materials. The one testable instance: Q1 revenue **beat its own guide** ($8.6M above the high end) and Q2 is guided to ~$10.0M (+16% QoQ). So near-term guidance is being **met/modestly beaten** — but on a *de-risked, shrunken* base after the mobile reset, which is exactly the setup where guidance is easy to clear. Reliability: **Medium** — credible on the next one or two quarters of small sequential steps; *unproven* on the load-bearing claim (material 800V data-center revenue in H2-2026/2027).

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

The recurring deflection is **the bridge from samples to dollars**: management answers volume questions with sample counts and efficiency specs, not booked design-win revenue, ship dates, or named hyperscaler/OEM customers. The second unaddressed worry is **dilution** — a $500M ATM against a ~$5.6B cap (~9% potential dilution) launched days after the June-3 NVIDIA-MGX pop; the market re-priced this immediately (stock −6.7% on the $500M ATM news, 2026-06-13, and is ~$24 vs the $34 ATH set on the June-3 announcement day). Third: **timing risk** on 800V ("end of year, early next year") that could slip.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🔴 Revenue **−38% YoY**; FY2026 guide ~$41M can't fund the business → structural losses all year (Q1 GAAP net loss −$33.8M).
🔴 **GAAP gross margin negative (−9.3%)** — the 39% is non-GAAP only.
🔴 **$500M ATM (2026-06-13) on top of a $122M May ATM** — serial equity issuance into the AI narrative; this is how the "runway" is really financed. ~233.7M shares out and rising.
🔴 **Valuation ~136x trailing / ~100x+ FY2026E sales** — virtually all AI-capex optionality is priced in; the stock already round-tripped $34→$24 off the June-3 catalyst.
🔴 **Catalyst concentration on a single shared macro factor (AI-capex)** — exactly the basket-correlation risk the regime doc flags.
🟢 **$221M cash, zero debt** — genuine runway, can fund the pivot without a solvency wall.
🟢 **Real NVIDIA design-in** on 800V HVDC + MGX/Kyber — a hard, named, repeatedly-reconfirmed collaboration (2025-05-21 → 2026-06-03), not vaporware.
🟢 **Dual GaN+SiC** capability is genuinely rare; fabless/capital-light vs Wolfspeed's broken capex model.
🟢 Sequential revenue + non-GAAP margin both inflecting up as mobile rolls off.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

The terminology shift from "fast charging" to "power delivery / HVDC / solid-state transformer" is the whole thesis in microcosm — Navitas is repositioning from a *consumer component* to *AI-infrastructure power*. The tell to watch: management measures progress in **sample counts and efficiency percentages**, the vocabulary of a company whose wins haven't yet converted to revenue. The new-CEO voice (Allexandre) is materially more capital-markets-savvy and promotional than a pure engineering tone — consistent with a management team whose near-term job is to *finance* the pivot through equity while the design-ins mature. The $500M ATM is the loudest hidden-in-plain-sight signal: they are selling the optionality, not buying it back.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Trajectory: PIVOTING.** Navitas is mid-transformation from a melting consumer-GaN base to an unproven-but-real AI-infrastructure-power franchise. The single most important thing to weigh before sizing: **you are paying a ~$5.6B (≈136x sales) AI-infrastructure valuation for a company that today does ~$40M of *shrinking* revenue, loses money on a GAAP gross basis, and is funding itself with a $500M ATM — i.e. essentially ALL of the AI-capex optionality is already in the price, and the load-bearing 800V revenue is a 2027 promise.** The downside if the ramp slips or NVIDIA dual-sources is severe (the multiple has no support from current fundamentals); the cash balance prevents bankruptcy but not a 50%+ de-rate.

**Moat:** the durable franchise is **NARROW** — real but thin. The economic moat is *intangible/design-in* (dual GaN+SiC IP, a named NVIDIA reference-design slot, capital-light fabless). It is **not** scale-cost (Infineon/onsemi dwarf it) and **not** switching-cost yet (design-ins not locked into volume production). With **GAAP gross margin negative and ROIC deeply negative**, there is no *demonstrated* return-based moat today — the moat is prospective. **MOAT_TREND: STABLE** (potentially widening if 800V converts to sole/dual-source sockets; not yet evidenced). **Secular force:** Navitas is on the *winning* side of electrification/AI-power — there is **no terminal-decline secular threat**; the legacy mobile base is shrinking but it's being deliberately abandoned, not structurally impaired in a value-trap sense. The risk here is **cyclical/competitive and valuation**, not terminal erosion: GaN/SiC is a contested arena (Infineon, onsemi, Power Integrations, EPC, Texas Instruments) where a hyperscaler can multi-source. **SECULAR_THREAT: manageable.** The cheapness question is moot — this is the opposite of cheap; it is a richly-priced growth-optionality name, not a fear-print.
