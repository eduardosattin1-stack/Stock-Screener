# Forensic Dossier — ASML Holding N.V. (ASML)

*A deep dive on management and the setup, covering the five most recent earnings calls (April 2025 through April 2026).*

ASML makes the lithography machines that print the circuitry onto computer chips. It is the only company on earth that sells the most advanced version of these machines — extreme ultraviolet, or EUV, lithography — which makes it a genuine monopoly at the leading edge of chipmaking. The question this dossier answers is not whether ASML is a great business (it plainly is), but whether management's story has held up against the numbers, and whether the price already reflects the good news.

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The through-line across all five calls is simple and consistent: artificial intelligence is the engine, and EUV is how ASML monetizes it. What is striking is how the *tone* of that story shifts from cautious to triumphant over twelve months.

- **April 2025 (Q1):** Management led with AI as "the key driver of growth," but the dominant word was *uncertainty* — specifically tariffs. CEO Christophe Fouquet repeatedly hedged: "there is still quite some uncertainty," "the end state will be unknown for a while." The 2025 revenue range (€30-35B) was wide on purpose.
- **July 2025 (Q2):** The hedging deepened. The memorable line: "while we are still planning for growth in 2026, we cannot confirm it at this stage." China weakness surfaced — a €1.4B order cancellation tied to export restrictions.
- **October 2025 (Q3):** The pivot begins. "We have seen a flow of positive news in the last few months that have helped to reduce the uncertainty." Management committed to 2026 net sales "not below 2025" — a floor, not a ceiling.
- **January 2026 (Q4/FY):** Full triumph. "Record quarter, record year, record booking." The frustration of 2025 — hearing about AI investment but not seeing it convert into machine orders — was explicitly resolved: "The last three months have changed that."
- **April 2026 (Q1):** Guidance *raised* to €36-40B for 2026 (a "strong growth year"), with memory customers "sold out for 2026" and supply constrained "beyond 2026." EUV Low-NA output guided to "at least 60" systems in 2026 and "at least 80" in 2027.

A new theme quietly entered in late 2025: software and AI inside ASML's own tools, marked by an 11% stake in French AI lab Mistral. And a discordant note appeared in January 2026 — a reorganization cutting 1,700 jobs (freeing ~3,000 roles, creating 1,400 new ones) — framed as de-layering, not distress.

## 2. CLAIMS vs FINANCIALS (forensic)

| Management claim | The numbers say |
|---|---|
| "Record year" 2025 | Confirmed. Revenue €32.67B, up ~15.6% YoY; net income €9.61B vs €7.57B; EPS €24.71 vs €19.24 (+28.4%). |
| "Strong growth year" 2026, €36-40B | Plausible and partly de-risked. Q1 2026 already printed €8.8B (above consensus), net income €2.8B, gross margin 53%. Consensus forward EPS growth is +34.8%. The order backlog (~€38.8B entering 2026) covers roughly a year. |
| Best-in-class economics | Confirmed and then some. Gross margin 52.8% (stable), net margin 29.4%, free-cash-flow margin 32.6%, return on equity 54.1%, return on invested capital 30.6%. These are elite for capital equipment. |
| "Net cash" balance sheet | Confirmed by the live correction: net cash of about €5.67B (cash €8.38B vs total debt €2.71B). The scan's stale "net debt -11.46B" field is the cash position, not debt. |
| Capital returns | Confirmed in the transcripts: €2.7B buyback in Q1 2025 alone, dividends rising (2024 total €6.40/share). |

Where the story is *richer* than the numbers justify: the 2025 revenue *growth rate* (~15.6%) is solid but well below the "AI is exploding" rhetoric — because China was simultaneously a tailwind (pre-buying) and a looming headwind (restrictions). The screener's own fair-value models are uniformly bearish: discounted-cash-flow fair value of about €542 and a Buffett-style fair value of about €1,144 against a price near €1,795 — a *negative* margin of safety of roughly -31%, with every method (discounted cash flow, earnings power value, Graham, owner-earnings) flashing "expensive." Price-to-free-cash-flow sits near 61x. The business is flawless; the *price* is the entire debate.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rising — decisively.** The arc from "we cannot confirm growth" (Q2 2025) to "very, very, very strong... record" (Q4 2025) to a *raised* full-year guide (Q1 2026) is one of the cleaner confidence escalations in large-cap tech. Guidance also became *more* specific over time: by April 2026 management was naming hard unit targets (60 EUV units in 2026, 80 in 2027) rather than hiding behind ranges. The only defensive note is the January 2026 reorganization, but management framed it offensively (freeing engineers to build faster), and the 53% gross margin in the very next quarter supports that framing rather than contradicting it.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**High.** The screener records a beat in 5 of the last 7 quarters (71%). More importantly, the *qualitative* guidance held: management said in Q3 2025 that 2026 would be "not below 2025," then in Q1 2026 raised the bar to €36-40B — they under-promised and over-delivered on direction. Gross-margin guidance (~52% for 2025) landed almost exactly. There is a mild sandbagging pattern: the 2025 range opened at €30-35B and the year closed at €32.67B, mid-range, after a cautious start. Reliability: High.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

Three topics recurred and management consistently deflected:
- **2026 order math.** In Q1 and Q2 2025, analysts (Sandeep Deshpande, Adithya Metuku) tried to triangulate the bookings needed to underwrite 2026. Management refused every time — "I'm not going to give you a grade on your math." This was prudent, not evasive, and the later guide vindicated the caution.
- **China.** The €1.4B debooking (Q2 2025) and the structural question of how far China revenue falls. Management was candid that China demand would be "significantly lower in 2026 than 2024 and '25."
- **High-NA timing.** Analysts repeatedly probed when the next-generation High-NA machines move from R&D into volume production. Management stayed vague ("maturity is the key word," "a lot of work this year, next year") — a real, unresolved overhang, since High-NA is dilutive to margins in the near term.

The topic analysts pushed on that *became* the bad news: China export controls. The April 2026 stock drop (~6% on the print despite the beat) was about a new restriction threat — the proposed MATCH Act, which could put even China *service* revenue at risk.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🟢 Order backlog rebuilt to ~€38.8B with memory customers "sold out for 2026."
🟢 Gross margin held at 53% in Q1 2026 despite the known dilution from High-NA system revenue recognition.
🟢 Net-cash balance sheet, heavy buybacks, rising dividend, Altman-Z solvency score of 15.1 (fortress).
🟢 Long-term 2030 target reaffirmed: €44-60B revenue at 56-60% gross margin.
🔴 China is ~33% of 2025 revenue and management guides it below 20% by Q4 2026 — a large, policy-driven revenue pillar is being deliberately removed, with the MATCH Act a tail risk to the installed-base service annuity.
🔴 Valuation: every internal model says expensive; price is +39% above its 200-day average and at 87% of its 52-week range. The good news is in the tape.
🔴 The January 2026 reorganization (1,700 cuts) in a "record" year is an odd signal — benign as told, but worth watching.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

The most telling shift is linguistic: in 2025 management talked about *hearing* about AI investment but not *seeing* it convert to orders ("a bit frustrating for us"). By January 2026 that gap closed — "capacity will mean market share" for customers, who are "very eager to get the capacity as quickly as possible." That is a supplier describing a seller's market. The Mistral stake and the "software content in our systems" framing signal a strategic widening of the moat beyond hardware. Management's energy comment is also revealing — they "worry a lot more about energy cost than gold or silver," flagging power availability, not demand, as the industry's real long-term constraint. That is the language of a company whose order book is not the bottleneck.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Trajectory: STRENGTHENING.** ASML is executing a textbook up-cycle: rising revenue, defended margins, a rebuilt backlog, disciplined capital returns, and a fortress balance sheet. Management's credibility is high and its confidence is justified by the numbers.

The single most important thing to weigh before sizing: **this is a quality-at-a-rich-price question, not a quality question.** The business merits a premium; the issue is that the AI-capex re-rating has already happened — the stock is up roughly 57% year-to-date, sits near its 52-week high, trades at ~61x free cash flow, and shows a negative margin of safety on every internal model. The "catalyst" (the 2026 guidance raise) has already fired. What you are underwriting at this price is continued upside *surprise* against an already-euphoric setup, into a China revenue cliff that management itself is guiding down.

**Moat:** WIDE — ASML is the sole EUV supplier, with switching costs, scale-cost advantages, and intangible (process-knowledge) barriers no competitor can replicate this decade. The returns evidence supports it: ROIC ~31%, gross margin stable at ~53%, both holding rather than eroding. **Moat trend: STABLE** (arguably widening via software, but the financial evidence shows steady, not rising, returns). **Secular threat: none** — ASML is the *beneficiary* of the AI build-out, not a victim; the threat to its terminal value is geopolitical (export policy), which is a cyclical/regulatory risk, not a structural demand-displacement force. The cheapness question does not apply: this is not cheap. It is an expensive, durable franchise where the debate is entry price, not terminal value.
