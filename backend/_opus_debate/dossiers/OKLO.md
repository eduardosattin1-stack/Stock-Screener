# OKLO (Oklo Inc.) — Interrogator Forensic Dossier

*Prepared by the Interrogator (Claude Opus 4.8) for the investment committee. Scale-out Basket E (POWER-GEN). All figures cross-referenced against the most recent Q1 2026 earnings call (reported May 12, 2026), the Q1 2026 10-Q, and live market data as of June 21, 2026 (price ~$60.88, market cap ~$10.66B, ~174M diluted shares, down ~67% from the $193.84 52-week high).*

> **Evidence note:** Oklo is a **pre-revenue** company. There is no revenue, margin, ROIC, FCF or backlog-conversion trajectory to cross-reference in the conventional sense. The "financials" here are a cash-runway-and-dilution story, and the "narrative" is a regulatory/commercial-pipeline story. The forensic test is therefore reframed: do management's milestone claims map to *verifiable, dated* regulatory and customer events, and is the cash bridge to first revenue credible without ruinous dilution?

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

Oklo's story has migrated decisively from **"strategy" to "execution"** over the trailing year — management's own framing on the Q1 2026 call: *"the story has increasingly moved from strategy to execution."* The arc:

- **De-SPAC era (mid-2024, via AltC / Sam Altman):** a single-product story — the Aurora microreactor (~15 MWe, later upsized to ~75 MWe), a "build-own-operate" utility model selling power under PPAs rather than selling reactors. Pre-revenue, pre-license, narrative carried almost entirely by Altman's name and the SMR theme.
- **2025 expansion:** the story broadened from *one reactor* to *three business units* — (1) **powerhouses** (the Aurora fission fleet), (2) **fuel recycling / HALEU** (the differentiated fuel-flexibility angle), and (3) a near-term **radioisotope** business (Oklo's acquisition of Atomic Alchemy), explicitly introduced to pull revenue forward into 2026 ahead of first power.
- **2026 AI-pivot overlay:** the dominant new growth driver is the **AI data-center power demand** narrative. The pipeline of non-binding LOIs ballooned to **~14 GW** — anchored by a **12 GW agreement with Switch**, an **Equinix** LOI, and a headline **1.2 GW Meta** clean-energy campus in southern Ohio (Meta announced up to 6.6 GW across Oklo/Vistra/TerraPower). NVIDIA, Los Alamos and INL ("Prometheus AI platform") partnerships were layered on as "strategic enablers."

**Growth driver introduced and load-bearing:** AI-capex demand for firm, 24/7 clean power. **Growth driver quietly de-emphasized:** the original pure "sell-power-via-PPA utility" simplicity — the model is now a far more capital-intensive, multi-unit, vertically integrated (fuel + reactor + isotopes) build-out requiring **$350–450M of investing cash in 2026 alone.**

## 2. CLAIMS vs FINANCIALS (forensic)

| Management claim (Q1 2026) | The evidence | Verdict |

| *"Moved from strategy to execution"* | Groundbreaking at INL (Sep 2025); Groves isotope facility built in 229 days; NRC PDC topical report accepted in 15 days (vs 30–60 typical); DOE Nuclear Safety Design Agreement approved | **Substantiated on process milestones** — real, dated regulatory/construction progress |
| Robust **~14 GW** customer pipeline | 12 GW Switch + Equinix + 1.2 GW Meta — but all are **non-binding LOIs**, no payment obligation, no signed PPAs at fixed price | **Richer than the numbers justify** — interest, not contracted backlog. Zero revenue today |

The hard numbers: **$0 revenue; Q1 net loss $33.1M; operating loss $51.2M; EPS −$0.19** (in line, no surprise). 2026 guide: **$80–100M operating cash use + $350–450M investing.** The narrative is a multi-year option on a fleet that does not yet exist; the financials are a well-funded but accelerating burn with no top line. **The story is materially richer than the trajectory justifies — which is the entire bull/bear tension.**

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rising / offensive.** CEO Jacob DeWitte's language is consistently forward-leaning and confident: *"Nuclear doesn't have to be slow…expensive, can be done in different ways"*; *"incredible momentum"*; *"deploy faster and do more simultaneously."* The tone has shifted *more* offensive over the year — from a single-reactor licensing story to a three-business-unit, fleet-licensing, AI-anchored growth narrative. There is no defensive/cost/macro pivot in the language; if anything the risk is the *opposite* — tone is running ahead of contracted cash flows. Notably, the CEO has been a **net seller** of personal stock (~$10M over 12 months), a tonal counter-signal to the verbal bullishness.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

Oklo gives **milestone guidance, not financial guidance** (no revenue to guide). On milestones, the delivery record is **Medium-to-High**: INL groundbreaking happened; the Groves facility was built fast; NRC accelerated-review acceptances landed faster than typical; DOE OTA / Nuclear Safety Design Agreement approvals came through. The **timeline has, however, historically slipped** — the original "Aurora online by 2027" framing has repeatedly been recalibrated (the first-commercial-ops target is now **late 2027** for INL, and full NRC commercial licensing is a *stepwise, multi-year* path). On the one cash-flow item they do guide (op/investing cash use), Q1 tracked in range. **Reliability: Medium** — process milestones credible; the commercial-revenue and first-power dates carry real slip risk and have moved before.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

Recurring pressure points management tends to soften rather than fully resolve:
- **"When does revenue actually arrive, and how big?"** — answered with "isotopes in 2026" and "first power late 2027," but the magnitude vs a $10.7B cap is left vague.
- **LOI → binding-contract conversion** — the 14 GW is repeatedly cited as momentum; analysts press on *which* convert to take-or-pay PPAs at what price, and the answer stays qualitative.
- **Dilution** — the $1.2B ATM funded the quarter but the $350–450M/yr investing burn implies **continued equity issuance**; management frames the balance sheet as a strength without fully owning the per-share dilution path to a deployed fleet.
- **NRC vs DOE pathway** — the pivot to the **DOE authorization pathway** (Reactor Pilot Program) for Aurora-INL is presented as an accelerant, but it sidesteps the harder commercial NRC license, which analysts note remains pre-submission for the broader fleet.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🟢 **Green flags**
- **$2.5B cash war-chest** — multi-year runway; not a near-term solvency story.
- Genuine, **dated regulatory acceleration** (DOE Nuclear Safety Design Agreement; NRC PDC topical report accepted; INL groundbreaking; Environmental Compliance Permit).
- **Tier-1 customer logos** (Meta, Switch, Equinix) and **NVIDIA** validate the AI-power demand thesis even if non-binding.
- **Near-term revenue optionality** from the isotope unit (Groves criticality targeted July 4, 2026; first contract pending).
- Fuel-flexibility moat-in-the-making (HALEU + recycling + plutonium inventories) that "isn't possible across all reactor types."

🔴 **Red flags**
- **Zero revenue** against a **$10.7B market cap** — valuation is entirely a discounted option on execution years out.
- Pipeline is **non-binding LOIs**, not contracted backlog; conversion and pricing unproven.
- **Structural dilution**: ~174M shares (+~28% YoY), ATM funding, and a $350–450M/yr investing burn ⇒ more issuance ahead.
- **CEO net selling** ~$10M personally while the verbal narrative is maximally bullish.
- **First-power date has slipped** before; commercial NRC licensing for the fleet remains pre-submission.
- The stock is **down ~67%** from its high — the market has already repriced a chunk of the optionality, but the cap still embeds enormous future success.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Terminology shift from "reactor company" to "energy/fuel platform"** — the three-business-unit framing (powerhouses + fuel + isotopes) is a deliberate move to look like a diversified, partly-near-revenue platform rather than a single binary-license bet. This is both genuine diversification *and* a narrative hedge against Aurora slippage.
- **DOE pathway as the new hero** — management increasingly anchors on the **DOE Reactor Pilot Program / July-4-2026 criticality** framing (driven by the May-2025 Trump executive order mandating three test-reactor criticalities by July 4, 2026). This is a *politically-supplied* tailwind — powerful while the administration backs it, but it makes a chunk of the thesis dependent on a single policy regime.
- **"Shift to the left"** on Centrus fuel timing — first explicit acceleration claim on fuel; a subtle positive but still LOI-grade.
- The **AI partnerships (NVIDIA, Prometheus, Los Alamos)** are described as engineering/modeling collaborations, not power-offtake — i.e. they burnish the AI association without (yet) being commercial demand.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Trajectory: STRENGTHENING** — but from a pre-revenue base, so "strengthening" means *the option is getting more valuable and better-funded*, not *the earnings base is compounding*. Real, dated milestone progress (DOE/NRC acceleration, INL groundbreaking, $2.5B raised, marquee LOIs) genuinely de-risks the path versus a year ago.

**The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing:** this is a **pre-revenue, multi-year option priced at $10.7B with continued dilution baked in.** Nearly all value sits in a terminal-fleet DCF that requires (a) first power roughly on the late-2027 schedule, (b) LOIs converting to priced PPAs, and (c) financing the $350–450M/yr build without crushing per-share value. There is **no margin of safety in the fundamentals** — only in how much of the optionality the −67% drawdown has already wrung out.

**MOAT:** Currently **NONE→NARROW (prospective).** There is no deployed asset, no returns, no margins to certify a moat. The *potential* moat is real and specific: a **regulatory-license + vertical fuel-integration** moat (first-mover DOE/NRC approvals, HALEU/recycling flexibility, build-own-operate recurring PPA revenue with high switching costs once a customer is plugged in). But it is unbuilt. Judged on *evidence* (zero returns), it is not yet a moat — call it NARROW-and-prospective.

**MOAT_TREND:** **WIDENING** — the regulatory lead, fuel agreements and customer LOIs are accumulating; if Aurora deploys, the license + integrated-fuel position becomes a genuine wide moat. The trend is favorable even though the level is not yet established.

**SECULAR_THREAT:** **NONE (it is a secular WINNER, not a loser).** Oklo sits on the *right* side of the energy-transition and AI-power secular waves — firm, dispatchable, carbon-free baseload is exactly what hyperscaler AI buildout is short of. The genuine risks here are **execution, dilution, timeline-slip and competition** (NuScale, TerraPower, X-energy, Kairos, the SMR field), **not** terminal-value secular erosion. The cheapness vs its own high is an **execution-risk / de-hype print**, not a structurally-shrinking base.
