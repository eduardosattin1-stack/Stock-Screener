# INTERROGATOR FORENSIC DOSSIER — NuScale Power Corp (NYSE: SMR)

*Prepared for the investment committee. As of 2026-06-22. Price ~$11.7, market cap ~$4.3B, 365M shares. Pre-revenue/pre-commercial-sale nuclear technology developer. Scale-out Basket E (POWER-GEN) — the only NRC-certified SMR design.*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

NuScale's story has run through three distinct chapters. **Chapter 1 (2023–early 2024):** the UAMPS / Carbon Free Power Project (CFPP) — the company's first and flagship commercial deployment — was the entire thesis. That project was **cancelled in November 2023** when subscription fell short and escalating cost estimates ($89/MWh and rising) made it uneconomic. The stock collapsed; the original narrative was effectively destroyed.

**Chapter 2 (2024):** survival and reset. Management pivoted hard to cost-cutting (a ~28% workforce reduction in early 2024), preserving cash, and re-marketing the NRC-certified design to data-center / hyperscaler-adjacent demand. The narrative shifted from "we have a flagship customer" to "we have the only certified technology and the AI-power wave is coming to us."

**Chapter 3 (late 2025–2026, current):** the AI-capex re-rating. The **September 2025 TVA / ENTRA1 announcement** (up to 6 GW of NuScale SMRs — potentially the largest US nuclear program ever) re-ignited the stock, which ran from single digits to a $57 high. The current narrative is entirely forward-looking: a TVA **PPA expected by year-end 2026**, an **OEM contract** to follow that flips the company "operationally cash-flow-positive by end of 2026," plus RoPower (Romania, 6 modules) and a Framatome fuel supply chain. The story today is **100% catalyst-dependent and 0% revenue-validated** — TTM revenue is $18.7M (down 62% YoY), and the company has never sold a commercial reactor.

The forensic core: this is a story stock where **every operational metric is deteriorating** (revenue down, losses widening, cash burning ~$300M/quarter on working-capital swings) while the **narrative is at an all-time high of ambition**. The financials justify a development-stage option, not a commercial enterprise.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rising / aggressively optimistic — and increasingly specific in a way that creates accountability risk.** CEO Hopkins: discussions "advancing well"; CFO Hamady put a hard stake in the ground — "I hope to be operationally cash flow positive by the end of this year." That is far more specific guidance than the 2024 survival-mode tone. The shift is from **defensive (2024 cost-cuts, cash preservation)** back to **offensive (capacity, demand, CF-positive target)**. But Hopkins also conceded the process is "complicated and slow" — a tell that management knows the timeline risk. Tone is confident; the confidence is **unbacked by signed contracts**.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Low-to-Medium.** The company's single most important historical guidance event — the CFPP/UAMPS flagship — **failed outright (cancelled 2023)**. That is the defining credibility scar: management's last big "this is happening" deal did not happen. Since then they have not had to deliver hard numbers because there is no commercial revenue to beat or miss. The current CF-positive-by-YE-2026 and TVA-PPA-by-YE-2026 guides are **untested promises riding on third-party (TVA, ENTRA1) decisions outside NuScale's control.** Reliability score: **Low** until a binding PPA or OEM contract is actually signed.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

Analysts pushed directly on **slow near-term adoption** — Hopkins admitted it is "complicated and slow." The recurring deflection is **timing certainty**: management answers "later this year" / "by year-end" without committing to a dated, binding milestone. Analysts also probe **scope/revenue at RoPower** — and management openly conceded "we're still working through what that scope actually is." The unaddressed worry: **what happens to the cash-flow-positive thesis if the TVA PPA slips into 2027**, which would force more dilution at a far lower stock price. Consensus rating is **Hold (17 analysts), PT ~$15** — the street is skeptical, not euphoric, at the analyst level even as retail/momentum drove the stock.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🔴 Q1-26 revenue **$0.6M** (down from $13.4M) — milestone-timing, but optically catastrophic for a "ramping" story.
🔴 **CFPP/UAMPS flagship cancelled (2023)** — the only prior commercial deal failed.
🔴 Liquidity built on **ATM dilution**; 365M+ shares and rising; CF-positive claim hinges on an unsigned contract.
🔴 TTM net loss **−$385.8M**; cash burn ~$300M+ in a single quarter.
🔴 Beta **2.22** — a high-volatility momentum vehicle, not a defensive utility.
🟢 **Only NRC-certified SMR design** — a real, hard, multi-year regulatory moat asset.
🟢 **Standard LEU fuel via Framatome** — sidesteps the HALEU bottleneck; supply chain de-risked early.
🟢 **TVA/ENTRA1 6 GW program** — a genuine, named, large counterparty; if a PPA signs, it is transformational.
🟢 Doosan Enerbility manufacturing already producing components — proof the supply chain is live.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

The terminology shift from "customer" to "potential customers seeking mega gigawatts" and "engagement" is the tell of a pre-contract pipeline dressed as demand. The reliance on **third-party intermediary ENTRA1** (which holds the customer relationship and OEM economics) means NuScale's economics in the 6 GW program are **a derivative of ENTRA1's deal structure**, not directly contracted — a subtle but material change in who captures value. Fluor — NuScale's longtime strategic backer — **exited its equity stake**; management framed this as liquidity, but a founding-sponsor exit at this stage is a quiet negative signal about insider conviction at these prices.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Trajectory: PIVOTING.** NuScale is a pre-commercial technology developer whose entire value rests on converting an NRC-certified design + an AI-power demand wave into the *first* signed commercial SMR order. It has pivoted from a failed flagship to a hyperscaler-adjacent demand thesis, but **has not yet validated a single dollar of commercial reactor revenue.**

**The single most important thing the director must weigh:** the TVA/ENTRA1 PPA is a **third-party, non-binding, serially-implied "by year-end" catalyst** — the same archetype (a slipping "later this year" deal) that already burned this company once (CFPP). Position sizing must assume the PPA can slip into 2027, forcing dilution at a lower price.

**Moat:** the NRC-certified design + first-mover regulatory position is a **regulatory-license** moat — genuinely real and hard to replicate, but **NARROW**: it protects the *option*, not an earnings stream, and competitors (GE-Hitachi BWRX-300 with built deployments, Oklo, X-energy, Holtec) are closing the certification/deployment gap. Returns are deeply negative (ROIC << 0, no FCF), so there is no earnings base to defend — the moat is on a pre-product asset.

**Moat trend:** STABLE-to-eroding — certification advantage erodes as peers advance through NRC and actually break ground (TVA's first SMR site, Clinch River, is GE-Hitachi BWRX-300, NOT NuScale — a pointed competitive fact).

**Secular threat:** the secular *tailwind* (AI-capex power demand) is the bull case, not a threat — terminal value is NOT structurally impaired by a secular force; the risk is **execution/dilution/timing**, not melting demand. So secular_threat = **manageable** (the demand wave is real and durable; NuScale's capture of it is the open question). It is a speculative-growth option, not a value trap and not a secular-decline victim.
