# Prosus N.V. (PRX.AS) — Forensic Dossier (Event-Driven Lens)

*Prepared by the Interrogator, as Claude Opus 4.8. Special-situation review: the catalyst on the board is a "forced seller of Delivery Hero" supply-clearing event. The job here is not to grade Prosus as a business — it is to test whether that specific catalyst is real, dated, binding, and load-bearing for the Prosus share price, and to find the verified downside floor.*

## 1. WHAT THE CATALYST ACTUALLY IS — AND THE MISMATCH AT ITS CORE

The Catalyst-Watch entry frames Prosus as a "forced seller of Delivery Hero" — the idea being that once a forced seller finishes dumping supply, the pressure clears and the price re-rates. That framing is half-right on the facts and wrong on the direction, and the direction is what matters.

The facts: when the European Commission cleared Prosus's roughly €4.1B takeover of Just Eat Takeaway in August 2025, it attached a remedy requiring Prosus to cut its Delivery Hero (ticker DHER, the German-listed global food-delivery group) holding sharply — below 10%. So yes, Prosus is a forced seller, on a hard regulatory clock. The Commission has already extended the deadline twice; the current hard date is **11 October 2026** (extension confirmed late May / early June 2026, granted at majority-owner Naspers's request after a reported Uber bid for Delivery Hero).

The mismatch: the shares being force-sold are *Delivery Hero* shares, not *Prosus* shares. A forced seller creates an overhang on the thing being sold — that supply pressure lands on Delivery Hero's stock, not on Prosus's. There is no forced selling *of Prosus*. So the classic special-situation logic ("buy the name the forced seller is dumping, wait for supply to clear, collect the snap-back") points at Delivery Hero, if anywhere — not at Prosus. Owning Prosus to play a forced-seller-clears thesis is backwards.

## 2. IS THE CATALYST EVEN MATERIAL TO PROSUS? (forensic: size it)

No — not remotely. This is the decisive finding.

Prosus is a roughly €130B+ holding company whose net asset value (NAV — the summed market value of its stakes) is dominated by a single asset: a ~23-24% stake in **Tencent**, which is on the order of **80% of NAV**. The rest is a basket of e-commerce, classifieds, fintech and food-delivery holdings.

Delivery Hero, by contrast, is now a *low-single-digit-percent* sliver of Prosus NAV. Prosus has already sold it down hard in 2026: from ~26.3% it cut to ~21.8% (4.5% sold to Uber, ~€270M, April), then a further 5% to activist Aspex Management (~€335-394M, May), leaving roughly 17%. The remaining stake is worth on the order of €1.5-2B against a €130B+ NAV — a rounding error. Whether Prosus is forced to sell the last slice by October, sells it to Uber in the takeover, or gets the remedy waived, the dollar impact on Prosus's per-share value is trivial.

In plain terms: the labeled catalyst cannot move Prosus. It can move Delivery Hero. The funnel has pinned a Delivery-Hero event onto the Prosus ticker.

## 3. WHAT ACTUALLY DRIVES PROSUS — THE REAL (NON-EVENT) STORY

Prosus's price is driven by two things, neither of which is the Delivery Hero remedy:

1. **Tencent.** With ~80% of NAV in one Chinese tech stock, Prosus is a leveraged proxy on Tencent and on the China-tech / Hong Kong tape. The stock has fallen from ~€50 in January 2026 to ~€37 now, tracking Tencent weakness and a widening holding-company discount — not anything to do with food delivery.

2. **The holding-company discount.** Prosus persistently trades well below the sum of its parts — currently cited around a **25-37% discount to NAV** (one widely shared read puts it near 36.7%). Closing that gap is the genuine value lever. Management's tool for it is an **open-ended share buyback** running since June 2022 that has returned close to US$42B and lifted NAV-per-share ~18% versus no-buyback. But a buyback is a perpetual grind, not a hard-dated event — there is no single day on which the discount is forced to close.

## 4. CATALYST STATUS, CREDIBILITY, AND RED FLAGS

- **Status:** the Delivery Hero remedy is a hard, dated obligation (11 October 2026), but it is (a) directionally aimed at Delivery Hero, not Prosus; (b) immaterial to Prosus NAV; and (c) largely already executed, with Prosus actively *asking the EU to drop the remaining requirement* (late May 2026) rather than racing to comply. A catalyst the issuer is trying to cancel, that is already ~two-thirds done, and that doesn't move the stock either way, is spent for our purposes.
- **Sponsor credibility:** management is highly credible operationally — the buyback discipline is real and NAV-accretive, and the Just Eat / food-delivery consolidation is being managed competently. That credibility is not in question. The question is relevance, and the answer is that their best lever (buyback) is not the board's catalyst.
- **Downside floor:** there is no event-specific floor here because there is no event payout. The only real floor is fundamental — Prosus's own NAV. At a ~25-37% discount the stock already embeds a large margin of safety to NAV, and the buyback puts a persistent bid under the price. But that floor is a *function of Tencent's price*, so it is not a fixed, defined-risk floor — if Tencent falls, the floor falls with it. The dossier's quoted floor of ~37.4 is essentially today's price, i.e. the asymmetry to the floor is roughly zero.
- **Red flags for the special-situation thesis specifically:** the catalyst is mislabeled (Delivery Hero event on the Prosus ticker); the spread the board cites (live ~39.3 vs target 46.21 vs floor 37.4) is a generic NAV-discount-convergence number, not an event spread, and the live price has already drifted below the quoted "live" level to ~37; and the real risk factor (Tencent / China beta) is uncapped and unhedged. None of these is a fraud or going-concern flag — Prosus is financially sound — they are reasons the *event thesis* does not hold.

## 5. VERDICT FOR THE DIRECTOR

Prosus is a legitimately cheap, well-run holding company trading at a wide discount to a Tencent-dominated NAV, with a disciplined buyback narrowing that gap over time. As a *long-term value* holding it has merit. But as the *event-driven special situation* this funnel is grading — a forced-seller-supply-clears catalyst — it does not qualify: the forced selling is of Delivery Hero (not Prosus), Delivery Hero is immaterial to Prosus NAV, the remedy is mostly executed and Prosus is trying to cancel the remainder, and there is no dated event that forces a Prosus re-rate or any defined downside floor. The single thing the director must weigh: this is a Tencent-discount value play wearing a borrowed catalyst label, not a defined-risk arbitrage. Do not size it as an asymmetric event trade.
