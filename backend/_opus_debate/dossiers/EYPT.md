# EyePoint Pharmaceuticals (EYPT) — Event-Driven Dossier

**Event type:** Late-stage clinical readout (binary). This is a special situation, not a quality-compounder thesis. EyePoint has essentially no product revenue to speak of; the entire value of the company rides on one investigational eye drug, DURAVYU, clearing its two pivotal trials. We are buying the spread between today's price and what the stock is worth if the data wins, against a floor set by the cash and pipeline value if it loses.

## 1. The Setup, In Plain Terms

EyePoint is a clinical-stage eye-disease company. Its lead drug, DURAVYU, is a tiny biodegradable insert placed in the eye that releases vorolanib (a patent-protected tyrosine kinase inhibitor — a drug that blocks the signaling that drives abnormal blood-vessel growth) slowly over time. The pitch is dosing roughly twice a year for wet age-related macular degeneration (wet AMD, the leading cause of vision loss in older adults), versus today's standard of eye injections as often as monthly. Less frequent dosing is the whole commercial case.

The catalyst is the topline (headline) result of two identical Phase 3 trials, LUGANO (U.S., ~432 patients) and LUCIA (global, ~475 patients), together enrolling over 900 patients. Both are randomized, double-masked, non-inferiority studies: DURAVYU dosed every six months versus on-label aflibercept (Eylea, the incumbent injection). The bar is non-inferiority — DURAVYU does not have to beat aflibercept on vision, only to roughly match it (average change in best-corrected visual acuity at weeks 52 and 56), while winning on dosing convenience. LUGANO reads out first, with LUCIA "to closely follow."

## 2. Is The Catalyst Real, Dated, And Binding?

Yes on real and binding; the date is firm-ish rather than a single fixed day. Management has repeatedly guided LUGANO topline to "mid-2026," and the most recent corporate communications and sell-side notes point to the readout landing around late summer 2026 (roughly August), with LUCIA shortly after. Our catalyst board carries a hard date of July 31, 2026 (about six weeks out as of late June 2026). The honest read: the readout is a scheduled, near-term, binding event — a Phase 3 database lock and unblinding is a one-time, can't-be-walked-back moment — but the exact calendar day may slip a few weeks within the "mid-2026" guide. It has not fired, and it has not slipped indefinitely.

Three things sharply raise confidence that the date holds and that the readout is close:
- **Enrollment is complete** — over 900 patients, trials no longer recruiting.
- **Patients have crossed the primary-endpoint window** — as of May 2, 2026, all active treatment-arm patients had reached the Week 32 visit (second dose), with over 35% already dosed a third time at Week 56. The endpoint is measured at weeks 52/56; the clock has effectively run out.
- **Three consecutive positive recommendations from the independent safety committee** (the Data Safety Monitoring Committee, the outside doctors who peek at unblinded safety mid-trial). The most recent was dated May 14, 2026. They have told the company, three times, to keep going unchanged.

## 3. How Credible Is Management On Hitting It?

Credible. The financing is the key tell: EyePoint reported roughly $300 million of cash and investments at December 31, 2025 (about $223 million by Q1 2026 after burn), funding operations into the fourth quarter of 2027 — well past this readout and through the next pivotal program (a separate Phase 3 in diabetic macular edema). A clinical-stage company does not need to raise money into the print, which removes the single most common way these setups get spoiled: a dilutive equity raise (selling new shares cheaply) right before the data. Management is also already producing manufacturing batches to support an NDA (the formal FDA marketing application), signaling genuine confidence and removing post-data delay.

## 4. The Verified Downside Floor

This is the crux of any special situation: what backstops the price if the drug fails. EyePoint has roughly $223 million of cash (about $2.65 per share on ~83.8 million shares) and no product franchise to cushion a miss. A failed pivotal in wet AMD would gut the lead-program value, but it would not zero the company: there is a separate diabetic-macular-edema program, the Durasert delivery platform and vorolanib IP, and the cash itself. Historically, a binary biotech Phase 3 miss like this drops the stock 60–75% in a day. From the ~$13.66 quote (June 26, 2026), that frames a bear landing zone in the mid-single digits. Our board's floor of $5.50 (roughly 2x net cash, crediting residual pipeline/platform value) is a reasonable, defensible bear case — it is real (cash is on the balance sheet) but it is not a hard contractual floor like a merger break price; it is an estimate, and a true wipe-out scenario could overshoot below it intraday.

## 5. The Upside Target

If both trials hit non-inferiority, DURAVYU becomes a credible twice-yearly competitor in a multi-billion-dollar wet-AMD market and a clear acquisition target. Sell-side targets cluster around $35–$37 (consensus ~$37), built on early commercial revenue and a high probability of approval once efficacy is shown. Our board target of $37.50 sits right at that consensus.

## 6. Red Flags / Things That Could Break It

- **It is a single binary.** Non-inferiority can still miss; eye-trial endpoints are noisy, and a confidence-interval that just clips the margin would be read as a failure by the market even if "directionally" fine.
- **Date precision.** "Mid-2026" is a window, not a fixed PDUFA day; a few-week slip is plausible and would not break the thesis, but an open-ended slip would.
- **No revenue cushion.** Unlike a deal-arb or a forced-seller situation, the floor here rests on cash plus pipeline optionality, not a contractual payout — so the downside is an estimate, not a guarantee.
- **No going-concern, covenant, or pre-data ATM-raise flags** were found — a clear green flag relative to the typical clinical-stage trap.

## 7. Verdict For The Director

This is a clean, near-term, binding clinical readout inside the window, with a live price (~$13.66) well below a credible ~$37 win target and a defensible mid-single-digit floor — an asymmetric, defined-risk special situation, which is exactly what this funnel is built to find. The credibility of the event (not the franchise) is high: complete enrollment, endpoint window passed, three positive safety reviews, and a balance sheet that removes the pre-data dilution risk. Moat and secular-decline framing are not applicable to a pre-revenue binary. The one thing to weigh before sizing: this is a true coin-flip-with-a-floor, so size it as a defined-risk bet you can hold through a 60%+ adverse move, not as a core compounder.
