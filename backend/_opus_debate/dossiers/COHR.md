# COHR — Coherent Corp. | Forensic Dossier (Interrogator)

*Prepared by the Interrogator (Claude Opus 4.8) for the scale-out committee. Scale-out Basket A (OPTICAL). Source: COHR FQ3-FY2026 earnings call (May 6, 2026) + FMP fundamentals + live web verification, June 2026. Fiscal year ends ~June; FQ3 FY26 = quarter ended March 31, 2026.*

---

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

Coherent's story over the last several quarters is a clean two-act transformation, and the management voice is new — Jim Anderson (ex-Lattice CEO, installed 2024) and CFO Sherri Luther are running an operator's playbook on what used to be a sprawling, debt-laden, multi-end-market materials conglomerate (the old II-VI that swallowed legacy Coherent in a 2022 deal that left it ~$4B+ levered).

**Act 1 (FY25, the cleanup):** deleveraging, portfolio pruning (sold Aerospace & Defense in FQ1-FY26; sold the Munich, Germany product division in FQ3 at "gross margin well below corporate"), margin-repair, and a stated >42% non-GAAP gross-margin / long-term target model laid out at the 2025 Investor Day. The pitch was "fix the balance sheet, exit the low-margin tail, expand margin."

**Act 2 (FY26, the AI-optical inflection — current):** the narrative has pivoted decisively to *AI data-center optical infrastructure as a multi-year secular growth engine.* Anderson now opens the call with "Coherent is a global leader in photonic technology, which is foundational to the performance and scalability of AI data centers." The frame is no longer "diversified photonics" — it is "the picks-and-shovels of AI optical interconnect," with Datacenter & Communications now 75% of revenue and +40% YoY.

Growth vectors *introduced and stacked* over recent quarters, in order of maturity: (1) 800G transceivers (shipping, growing), (2) 1.6T transceivers ("ramping at an incredibly rapid pace," faster than a year ago), (3) OCS / optical circuit switching (market opportunity *doubled* to >$4B at OFC; bottleneck just cleared), (4) **CPO / co-packaged optics** — the crown jewel, >$15B incremental TAM, anchored by the March-2026 NVIDIA partnership ($2B equity + multi-year supply agreement through end of decade), scale-out revenue starting H2-CY26, scale-up H2-CY27; (5) multi-rail DCI systems (>$2B TAM, revenue H1-CY27); (6) thermal solutions (Thermadite, thermoelectric generators repurposed from the industrial materials business into XPU cooling/waste-heat recovery, revenue H2-CY27).

Nothing material has been *quietly dropped* — the Industrial segment is openly acknowledged as soft (declining YoY/QoQ) but explicitly retained and re-cast as a future AI-thermal optionality. The arc is one of *expansion and acceleration*, not contraction. The single most important structural enabler woven through every answer is **indium phosphide capacity** — the 6-inch InP ramp (Sherman TX, Sweden, now Zurich) that is both the volume unlock and the gross-margin lever.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Trajectory: RISING — and notably so.** Anderson's language is the most confident in the company's recent history, and crucially it is *specific*, not hand-wavy:

- **Demand:** "Customer demand remains exceptionally strong with no signs of attenuation… another step function increase in our order book… record level." "Demand looks great. What we're doing is ramping supply very, very quickly." The bottleneck framing — *we are supply-constrained, not demand-constrained* — is the single strongest tell of a genuine up-cycle.
- **Offensive, not defensive:** every analyst question gets answered with a capacity/TAM/growth-vector expansion, not a cost/macro hedge. R&D was *raised* to 9.9% of revenue (offensive investment); SG&A *fell* to 9.4% (operating leverage). This is the classic offense-with-discipline posture.
- **Specific commitments with dates:** "double InP capacity by end of CY26… one quarter earlier than planned… more than double again by end CY27" (a quadrupling); CPO scale-out H2-CY26, scale-up H2-CY27; multi-rail H1-CY27; thermal H2-CY27. Management is willing to be held to dated milestones — a confidence signal.

The only defensive note is Industrial ("continued softness"), and even that is spun forward into the thermal-solutions opportunity. There is no hedging on macro, rates, or AI-capex durability — which is itself the *risk* (see §6).

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Reliability: HIGH.** The CFO's "7 of last 8 quarters of sequential GM expansion, +530bp cumulative" is a falsifiable, delivered track record, not a promise. The Q4 guide ($1.91–2.05B, GM 39–41%, EPS $1.52–1.72) implies *accelerating* QoQ growth and is consistent with the visible InP ramp (devices made last quarter ship as transceivers ~2–3 months later — a disclosed, mechanical latency that makes the near-term ramp legible). Management under-promised on the InP doubling (now a quarter early) — a mild sandbagging / over-delivery pattern that builds credibility. The >42% GM Investor-Day target remains the open question: they are at 39.6% and "super focused," but ~250bp of climb is still owed. No evidence of a miss pattern; this team has delivered what it guided since the leadership change.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **Gross-margin fall-through (Thomas O'Malley/Barclays, Blayne Curtis/Jefferies):** the sharpest line of questioning — "why aren't you getting *more* incremental fall-through on gross margin given 6-inch InP at half the cost and the mix shift?" Management's answer (early in the 6-inch ramp, more benefit ahead, pricing+yield+cost all contributing) is credible but *concedes the bears' point*: the margin upside is mostly *ahead*, not banked. This is the key skeptic foothold — execution risk on getting to >42%.
- **Competitive gap vs. peers on OCS/CPO (Simon Leopold/Raymond James):** "there's a perceived gap vs one of your primary competitors [read: Lumentum] in OCS and CPO forecasts." Management deflected into TAM-doubling and differentiation rhetoric without quantifying its own OCS/CPO revenue — a real non-answer. Investors cannot yet size COHR's CPO/OCS share vs LITE/CRDO.
- **Capacity → revenue conversion latency (Sean O'Loughlin/TD Cowen):** "if you're doubling InP capacity next quarter, why isn't revenue doubling?" Answered honestly with the 2–3-month device-to-transceiver lag — a good, non-evasive answer that nonetheless flags that capacity ≠ instant revenue.
- **1.6T revenue scale (Simon Leopold):** asked point-blank if 1.6T exceeded $100M in the quarter — management declined to break out data-rate revenue. Standard, but it means the 1.6T ramp is unquantified.
- **Silicon-photonics viability at 3.2T (Sean O'Loughlin):** raised "industry commentary questioning the viability of SiPho and CW lasers at 3.2T." Management rebutted with the OFC 400G/lane demo. A live technology-roadmap debate that bears will keep poking.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🟢 **Genuine deleveraging:** net leverage 0.5x from 2.1x a year ago — a transformed balance sheet.
🟢 **NVIDIA $2B equity stake + multi-year CPO supply agreement through end of decade** — a strategic anchor that validates the photonics platform and funds the capex.
🟢 **Supply-constrained, not demand-constrained** — record backlog, orders into CY28, LTAs to end of decade with customer upfront CapEx + minimum-demand commitments (skin in the game on both sides).
🟢 **Margin expansion delivered** — 7 of 8 quarters, +530bp, with a structural cost lever (6-inch InP at <½ the cost / >4x devices) still mostly ahead.
🟢 **New management executing** — Anderson/Luther under-promising and over-delivering on the InP ramp.
🟢 **Multiple stacked growth vectors** (OCS, CPO, multi-rail, thermal) layering on top of the core transceiver ramp — real optionality, not vaporware (NVDA anchors CPO).

🔴 **Deeply negative free cash flow** — FQ3 FCF −$383M, TTM FCF negative; capex $290M and *rising*. Defensible as growth capex, but the equity is priced for a cash-machine it is not yet.
🔴 **Valuation is the whole bear case** — ~$76B market cap, ~52x forward P/E, ~9–10x EV/sales, stock +171% in 12 months (from $77 to ~$440 high). A massive amount of CPO/OCS/multi-rail optionality is *already priced in* before it generates a dollar of revenue.
🔴 **>42% GM target not yet hit** (39.6%) — ~250bp owed; the bears' "fall-through" question is unresolved.
🔴 **Single shared macro driver:** the entire thesis rides AI-capex durability — the same factor under every other name in the scale-out basket. No resolution-driver independence.
🔴 **Industrial segment soft** — declining YoY/QoQ; the diversification leg is shrinking, concentrating the company on the AI-optical bet.
🔴 **OCS/CPO competitive share unquantified** — management deflected the LITE comparison; COHR's actual share of the next-gen layers is unproven.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Pricing power is a quiet tailwind:** "pricing optimization" cited repeatedly as a *gross-margin driver* — and "very healthy" pricing dynamics, with ASP step-ups at each data-rate transition (1.6T > 800G). In a supply-short market, COHR has pricing leverage it didn't have two years ago. This is a margin engine the bears under-model.
- **Vertical integration as moat language:** management leans hard on "most components internally sourced" — InP devices, fiber, lens arrays, isolators, TECs, the external laser source module. The CPO pitch is "we provide all the ingredients, not just the laser," which buffers input-cost inflation and is the differentiation vs. assemblers. This is the real moat claim: *photonics-materials vertical integration + manufacturing scale.*
- **TAM inflation as a tell:** OCS TAM *doubled* to >$4B and CPO sized at >$15B "probably conservative" within a single quarter — bullish, but TAM-expansion rhetoric is also how a richly-valued stock keeps the narrative ahead of the print. Watch whether OCS/CPO revenue actually materializes on the H2-CY26 timeline.
- **"Allocate InP capacity to maximum margin dollars"** — a disciplined operator's capital-allocation framing (Anderson's Lattice DNA), distinct from the old volume-chasing II-VI culture. A genuine cultural shift.
- **The 6-inch InP ramp is the master variable** — it appears in nearly every answer as both the volume unlock *and* the margin lever. If it slips, both the growth *and* the margin theses wobble simultaneously.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Classification: STRENGTHENING.** This is a genuinely transformed business — deleveraged, margin-expanding, demand-saturated, under new operator management executing on dated milestones, with a strategic NVIDIA anchor. The fundamental trajectory is among the strongest in the scale-out universe.

**The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing:** *how much of the AI-optical optionality is already in the price.* The fundamentals are excellent; the **valuation is not cheap** (~52x forward P/E, ~9–10x EV/sales, +171% in a year, near all-time highs). The CPO/OCS/multi-rail/thermal growth vectors that justify the multiple are *future* revenue (H2-CY26 → CY27), and the company burns FCF today. This is a "great company, demanding price, single-factor (AI-capex) macro dependence" setup — the risk is not the business, it is paying up for priced-in optionality into a basket already concentrated on the same AI-capex wall.

**MOAT:** **NARROW, trending toward WIDER.** Moat type = **intangible (proprietary photonics IP + 6-inch InP process technology) + scale-cost (vertical integration across the optical BoM) + emerging switching-costs (multi-year LTAs, CPO design-in with NVIDIA).** The returns evidence is mixed: ROIC ~4% and ROE ~5% TTM are *depressed* by the negative-FCF growth-capex phase and the heavy acquired-intangible base (intangibles ~43% of assets) — these are not yet high-return numbers, so the moat is not provably *wide* on the financials. But **margins are rising, not falling** (GM +530bp, op margin expanding), pricing power is real, and the InP/CPO vertical integration is hardening into a switching-cost/scale moat. This is a **widening** moat that has not yet shown up in returns because the capital base is being built out. It is NOT an eroding moat.

**SECULAR THREAT: MANAGEABLE (and arguably a secular *beneficiary*).** COHR sits on the *winning* side of the dominant secular force — it is a direct picks-and-shovels supplier to AI scale-out/scale-up optical interconnect, the physical bottleneck every hyperscaler capex dollar must pay. The terminal-value risk is **not** structural disintermediation; it is (a) *cyclicality* of AI-capex (a demand-durability question, not a moat-erosion question) and (b) *technology-transition risk* (could a competing interconnect approach — copper persistence in scale-up, a rival CPO architecture, or a SiPho-vs-EML roadmap fork — strand COHR's InP-centric bet). Both are manageable given the breadth of COHR's laser-technology roadmap (EML, SiPho, VCSEL, CW) and its NVDA design-in. The cheapness question is moot — this is **not cheap**, so it is neither a fear-print nor a value trap; it is a high-quality growth name where the debate is entirely about *how much future is already paid for.*
