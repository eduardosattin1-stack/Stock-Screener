# Forensic Dossier — ALTEN S.A. (ATE.PA)

*A deep dive on management and the setup. Prepared for the investment committee. No earnings-call transcript was bundled with the inputs; this dossier is built from ALTEN's own FY2025 annual results release (published early 2026), the Q1 2026 revenue-call commentary (reported via Investing.com), and the screener's financial trajectory. Where the public record is thin, that is stated rather than filled in.*

ALTEN is a European leader in engineering and technology consulting — it rents out engineers (about 51,800 of them, inside a total workforce of roughly 57,400) to run research-and-development and information-technology projects for big industrial clients in autos, aerospace and defense, telecoms, life sciences, rail and semiconductors. It is, in plain terms, a high-end staffing and project house: it bills skilled labor at a margin. That model explains the financial shape you see — thin reported gross margin (about 8%), a modest net margin (about 3%), but real cash generation and, unusually for the sector, a net-cash balance sheet.

## 1. Narrative arc

The story over the last four years is a clean cyclical round-trip. ALTEN rode the post-pandemic R&D spending boom — revenue jumped from €2.93B in 2021 to €3.78B in 2022 and earnings per share more than doubled to €13.21 — then plateaued (€4.07B in 2023, €4.14B in 2024) and has now rolled over (€4.10B in 2025, down about 1%, and down about 4.5% on a like-for-like "organic" basis that strips out acquisitions and currency). Earnings per share has fallen every year since the 2022 peak, to €3.03 in 2025.

Management's framing has shifted in step. Two years ago the emphasis was growth and international expansion; the most recent commentary is squarely about "gradual sequential stabilization," defending margin, and waiting for individual end-markets to turn. The growth driver that was quietly dropped is organic momentum in the big industrial verticals — autos above all. The driver that has been promoted is mergers and acquisitions: management now talks about a busy bolt-on pipeline ("more than 5 deals underway," "more than 10" signed awaiting closure, plus the announced Itsclinic deal, a roughly €60M-revenue data/cloud consultancy due to close in early Q3 2026). In other words, with organic growth stalled, the company is buying growth — financed comfortably out of net cash.

One important data-hygiene note for the committee: the screener's "trailing-twelve-month free cash flow of €736M" and "trailing diluted EPS of €8.36" do **not** reconcile with ALTEN's own audited disclosures (FCF of €240M, EPS of €3.03 for 2025). The company's reported figures are the ones to trust; the €736M almost certainly reflects operating cash flow before working-capital and lease items, not true free cash flow. Sizing should be done off the €240M figure.

## 3. Tone & confidence trajectory

**Declining, then cautiously steadying.** The language has migrated from offensive (growth, expansion) to defensive (stabilization, margin discipline, "limited visibility"). The most recent tone is not panicked but is hedged — management repeatedly flags uncertainty and declines to call a sharp recovery. On geopolitics they were precise rather than evasive: "The war in Ukraine has so far no consequence on the dynamic of our customers. It is a zero effect." The honest admission on semiconductors — "We have exactly the same kind of question" about why semis clients are not recovering despite an industry upturn — reads as candor, not spin.

## 4. Guidance credibility

**Medium.** ALTEN does not give precise revenue guidance; it frames ranges (organic growth of "0% to −0.5% for the first half" of 2026) and directional margin commentary. The screener shows a 50% earnings-beat rate (one of two quarters), which is unremarkable. The company's track record on the one thing it controls — cash generation and the balance sheet — is excellent and fully delivered. Its record on *calling the cycle* is the weak spot: it has been waiting for a turn for several quarters. There is no evidence of sandbagging; if anything the commentary is conservative.

## 5. Analyst pressure points

The recurring pressure points are clear from the Q1 2026 exchange: (1) the semiconductor disconnect — why ALTEN's chip-sector clients (notably ASML's subcontracting ecosystem in the Netherlands) are not yet benefiting from the broader semi recovery; management conceded the subcontractors "do not profit from it" yet; (2) the German automotive collapse, where management described a deliberate "double hypothesis" — hold auto activity flat and pivot toward higher-margin fixed-price work packages; and (3) currency, with reported numbers dragged below organic by foreign-exchange. None of these were deflected, but none were resolved either — they are open risks the market will keep testing.

## 6. Red flags / green flags

🔴 Organic revenue is still negative on a reported basis; the recovery is concentrated in aerospace/defense while autos, telecom and semis remain in decline.
🔴 A €67.4M goodwill impairment signals that some past acquisitions are worth less than paid — relevant given the company is now leaning harder on M&A for growth.
🔴 Earnings power is well below its 2022 peak (EPS €3.03 vs €13.21) and the recovery to prior peak is years away, if ever.
🟢 Net cash of about €390M (roughly €11 per share against a ~€53 price) and negative gearing — rare resilience for a labor-services firm in a downturn.
🟢 Free cash flow stayed strongly positive (€240M) through the trough; the dividend (€1.50 per share, paid June 2026) is easily covered.
🟢 Aerospace, defense and naval are genuinely inflecting upward (+10% in defense/naval), and Q1 2026 organic growth turned positive at constant currency — the first green shoot.
🟢 Founder-led, disciplined capital allocation: external growth and dividends self-financed without leverage.

## 7. Hidden signals

The most telling shift is the pivot in Germany from pure staff augmentation toward "work packages where there's going to be a stronger margin" — a structural move up the value chain in response to the auto downturn, not just cyclical patience. The busy bolt-on M&A pipeline, financed from cash, suggests management sees the trough as the time to consolidate smaller rivals cheaply. And the explicit, repeated refusal to promise a margin "explosion" is a deliberate expectations-management choice that lowers the bar the company has to clear.

## 8. Capital-allocation verdict (for the director)

**Trajectory: STABLE, bottoming.** This is a well-run, founder-disciplined, net-cash compounder caught in a genuine cyclical trough in industrial R&D spending, with the first signs of an aerospace-led turn. The single most important thing to weigh before sizing: **this is a cyclical recovery bet, not an event.** There is no hard, dated catalyst — no activist, no breakup, no forced seller, no announced sale. The re-rating depends on the industrial R&D cycle (especially German autos and semis) actually turning, on management's timetable, which has already slipped. The balance sheet means you are paid to wait and the downside is cushioned, but patience is the price of admission.

On the moat: ALTEN has a **narrow** economic moat — scale in a fragmented engineering-services market, deep multi-year client embedding, and a reputation that wins blue-chip R&D mandates — but it is a labor-arbitrage model with thin switching costs, and return on invested capital (about 10.6%) has been *falling* from its peak as utilization and pricing soften. That makes the moat trend mildly **eroding** on the cycle, though not structurally broken. The relevant secular question is generative-AI displacement of engineering and IT-services labor: this is a **material** (not yet terminal) threat. AI tools that lift engineer productivity could, over time, compress the billable-hours model that funds the business — but high-end, regulated, safety-critical R&D (aerospace, defense, automotive systems) is among the more defensible niches against pure automation. The cheapness here reads more as a cyclical fear-print than a melting-ice-cube value trap, but the AI overhang is real and caps how much credit the low multiple deserves.
