# MYR Group (MYRG) — Forensic Dossier

*Interrogator pass · Opus 4.8 · evaluated within the AI scale-out / scale-up build-out (scale-out Basket B — GRID). Source: web (Q1-2026 earnings call transcript + Q4/FY-2025 + FY-2024 results, live fundamentals June 2026).*

MYR Group is a US/Canada electrical-construction contractor in two segments: Transmission & Distribution (T&D) and Commercial & Industrial (C&I). It is a labor-and-execution business that bids, builds, and closes out high-voltage lines, substations, and mission-critical electrical fit-outs (data centers, water/wastewater, semiconductor, healthcare). Its role in the AI build-out is the physical electrical contractor who actually energizes the grid interconnects and wires the data halls.

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The story over the last ~6 quarters is a **margin-recovery + structural-demand-inflection** arc, and it has dramatically strengthened.

- **FY-2024 (the trough):** MYRG was a cautionary tale of execution. Net income collapsed to $30.3M, EPS $1.83, gross margin 8.6%. The narrative was about a handful of bad clean-energy/large C&I projects, change-order disputes, and weather/productivity inefficiencies. Management's framing was defensive — "de-risking the contract book," "selectivity," "not chasing the first-in-on-a-megaproject."
- **FY-2025 (the turn):** Revenue $3.66B, net income $118.4M (+291%), EPS $7.53, gross margin recovered to 11.6%. The story pivoted from "fixing the book" to "structural demand is here": grid modernization, electrification, and the early data-center wave.
- **Q1-2026 (the inflection confirmed):** Record everything. Revenue $1.0B (+20% YoY), net income $46.8M, EPS $2.99, gross margin 13.4%, EBITDA $81.5M, record backlog $2.84B. FY revenue guide raised from "10-ish" to "12-ish percent," and operating-margin guidance ranges were RAISED in BOTH segments (T&D to 8–11% from 7–10.5%, C&I to 6–9% from a lower base). New growth drivers were introduced and quantified: "Data center construction starts are up nearly 100% year-over-year" (Egan, C&I COO), plus water/wastewater as the strongest C&I grower.

The CEO explicitly anchored the T&D opportunity to a multi-year, nationwide build-out — comparing it to the 2013–2015 CREZ Texas transmission boom but "going across the United States over the next decade" rather than "one area." The narrative has expanded from a single-quarter recovery into a secular-capex story. Nothing material has been quietly dropped; if anything the additions (DC, water, large-transmission visibility into 2027+) are accretive and corroborated.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY — **Rising**

- FY-2024 calls: defensive, "de-risk," "selectivity," apologizing for project inefficiencies.
- FY-2025: "recovery on track," confidence returning.
- Q1-2026: clearly **offensive** — raising guidance twice (revenue + both margin bands), CEO invoking a decade-long nationwide transmission build, COO quantifying DC starts at +100%. Yet the CEO retained disciplined hedges: "we continue to be selective on the larger projects," "we don't want to be the first in," "we haven't seen margin pressure from these new entrants." This is the right tone — bullish on demand, disciplined on bidding. The shift from defensive (cost/efficiency) to offensive (growth/investment, 3%-of-revenue capex above historical norms) is unambiguous and EVIDENCE-backed.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY — **Medium-High**

The relevant track record is short and asymmetric: FY-2024 was a MISS (Q4-2024 missed EPS + revenue), so reliability cannot be scored "High" on a clean multi-year beat streak. BUT the subsequent four-quarter recovery culminating in a RAISED FY-2026 guide (10%→12% revenue, both margin bands up) has so far been delivered or exceeded each quarter through Q1-2026. The pattern is credible-and-improving rather than serially-sandbagging. The CFO's volunteered caution on DSO drifting from "mid-50s" to "low-60s" (a working-capital headwind from MSA mix) is a credibility GREEN flag — management is pre-disclosing the friction rather than letting it surprise. Score: **Medium-High (4/5)** — held back from 5 only by the recency of the FY-24 miss and the close-out-flattered Q1 margin.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **Sustainability of the 13.4% gross margin** — analysts repeatedly probe whether the margin is a new floor or a close-out-driven peak. Management answers honestly (lumpy; guide to the bands, not the print), but this is THE contested variable.
- **Book-to-bill / backlog conversion** — pressure on why backlog (+7.7%) lags revenue (+20%); management deflects to "large transmission projects come into backlog in future quarters" with revenue in "2027 or later." That deflection is plausible but UNVERIFIED until the awards actually book.
- **New competition in data centers** — analysts pushed on whether new entrants compress margin; CEO's "we haven't seen margin pressure" is a claim to watch, not yet a proven defense.
- **Labor and materials availability** — management itself flagged clients worried about "where are they going to get the material" and "how do they get their labor secured." This is the real governor on the build-out; it's a both-sided risk (constrains supply → supports pricing, but also caps MYRG's own throughput).

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- Terminology shift from "de-risking the book" (2024) to "selective, don't want to be first-in" (2026) — same discipline, but now from a position of strength (demand-rationing) rather than damage-control. Subtle but real.
- Capex guided ABOVE historical norm (3% of revenue, T&D-weighted) — management is investing into the cycle (equipment, prefab capacity), a tell that they believe the demand is durable enough to underwrite owned capacity.
- M&A language activated: "some high-quality companies out there," cash earmarked for "acquisitions...or stock buybacks." After a multi-year stretch of pure organic focus, capital-allocation optionality is back on the table — a positive but also a use-of-cash watch item.
- MSA work now ~70% of T&D revenue (recurring master-service-agreement maintenance) — a quietly important quality-of-earnings improvement: more recurring, less lumpy-bid revenue, at the cost of higher DSO.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Trajectory: STRENGTHENING.** The fundamental business is unambiguously improving — margins, returns, backlog, balance sheet, and demand visibility all moved the right way, corroborated by hard numbers, with credible and pre-disclosing management.

**The single thing the director must weigh before sizing:** the gap between the BUSINESS (excellent, strengthening) and the STOCK (already priced for perfection at ~50x trailing / 27x EV/EBITDA, +176% in a year, ABOVE the analyst median target and at the top of the peer cohort). This is a great company whose AI-capex optionality is largely ALREADY in the price. The risk is not that the build-out fails; it is that a strong build-out is already the base case, so the marginal surprise has to be even-stronger-than-record to drive further multiple expansion — and book-to-bill <1.0x is the first hairline crack in that perfection narrative.

**Moat:** Scale-cost + execution-reputation + bonding-capacity/skilled-labor intangible. Electrical construction is fragmented, low-switching-cost, and re-bid project-by-project; MYRG's edge is scale, a 100-year reputation, union skilled-labor access, bonding capacity, and a long data-center track record ("doing data centers since data centers first started"). That is a **NARROW** moat — real but not a fortress; there is no recurring lock-in beyond MSAs, and new entrants are appearing (margin pressure not yet visible but flagged). The moat is **STABLE-to-modestly-WIDENING**: rising ROIC/margins and the MSA shift improve recurrence, but the structure remains competitively contestable. I grade it NARROW / STABLE (the returns are rising but off a recovery trough, and the business is structurally re-bid).

**Secular threat:** This name is a secular WINNER of electrification/grid build-out, not a victim of a decline theme. The terminal-value risk is CYCLICAL (a capex digestion / build-out air-pocket), not structural impairment. AI/automation does not displace the physical act of energizing high-voltage lines. **Secular threat: none-to-manageable.** The cheapness question is moot — it is not cheap; it is expensive on a strengthening base, so the risk is a high-multiple de-rate on any growth wobble, not a value trap.
