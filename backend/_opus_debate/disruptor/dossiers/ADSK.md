# Autodesk (ADSK) — Interrogator Forensic Dossier

*Source material: five consecutive earnings calls (FY25 Q4 dated 2025-02-27 through FY26 Q3 dated 2025-11-25), plus the screener financial trajectory and live TTM/balance-sheet corrections. Web-verified through the FY26 Q4 print (2026-02-26).*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The story across these five quarters is remarkably stable in its **strategic core** and meaningfully evolving in its **financial framing**. From the first call (FY25 Q4, 2025-02-27) onward, every quarter opens with the identical mantra: "the convergence of design and make in the cloud, enabled by platform, industry clouds, and AI." That thesis never wavers — it is repeated almost verbatim five times. What *changes* is the surrounding wrapper.

- **FY25 Q4 (Feb 2025)** introduced two simultaneous shocks: a **restructuring/layoff announcement** ("the optimization phase of our sales and marketing plan") and the retirement of the legacy **10%–15% long-term growth range**. New CFO Janesh Moorjani (just arrived) explicitly reset expectations downward: "it felt inappropriate to have a range out there if we have not delivered against that in the last couple of years." This was the low-confidence trough of the window — a defensive, house-cleaning quarter.
- **FY26 Q1–Q3 (May → Nov 2025)** show a steady **rebuild of offensive tone**. Each quarter "topped the higher end of our guidance ranges," management raised full-year guidance "across the board" (Q2 and Q3), and the AECO segment emerged as the explicit growth engine, repeatedly tied to "sustained investment in data centers, infrastructure, and industrial buildings, which is more than offsetting softness in commercial."
- A genuinely **new** growth driver was introduced mid-window: at the Q3 call, management leaned hard into **AI monetization** — "neural AI foundation models," "MCPs and APIs… another source of monetization," and "consumption, and outcome-based business models." This was absent from the early calls and represents a real thesis *expansion*, not a pivot away from anything.
- The **margin narrative** is the most important evolution. What began (Q4 FY25) as a vague "best in the industry" aspiration crystallized by Q2 FY26 into a hard quantitative commitment: **"reported non-GAAP operating margin to be 41% in fiscal '29 or about 45% on an underlying basis,"** a ~500/900 bps improvement. This is not coincidental — it directly answers Starboard Value's public proxy campaign (web-confirmed) demanding ~45% underlying margins by FY28.

Nothing was quietly dropped. The "new transaction model" and "annual billings transition" — two mechanical distortions — are discussed with unusual transparency every quarter, with management repeatedly pre-warning that the billings/FCF tailwinds "will significantly diminish next year."

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Trajectory: RISING** (off a defensive Feb-2025 trough).

- **FY25 Q4 (defensive):** "it felt inappropriate to have a range out there if we have not delivered… we've been hovering around the low-end of that range." New CFO managing expectations down. Restructuring/layoff defensiveness dominates.
- **FY26 Q1 (stabilizing):** "strong momentum and performance… set us up well for the year," but laced with caution — "additional caution in our underlying growth assumptions to reflect greater macroeconomic uncertainty," modeling a downside "at roughly the same rate as during the pandemic."
- **FY26 Q2 (offensive):** "raising our guidance for the full year," and the proactive FY29 margin target — a confident, forward-leaning act. "These efficiency gains, combined with inherent operating leverage set us up well to expand our operating margin over time."
- **FY26 Q3 (most offensive):** Andrew Anagnost: **"I've never been more confident in the long-term value we are creating… Our best days and greatest opportunities lie ahead."** Guidance raised "across the board" again. This is peak confidence in the window.

The arc moved decisively from **defensive (efficiency/cost/macro) → offensive (growth/AI monetization/margin expansion)** over nine months. Guidance grew *more* specific over time, not less — the FY29 margin target and explicit billings/FCF normalization warnings are the opposite of obfuscation.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Reliability: HIGH.** The screener records a **100% beat rate (7/7 quarters)**, and the transcripts corroborate it: every single quarter "topped the higher end of our guidance ranges." Q1 FY26 guidance was raised at Q2, then raised again at Q3 — a clean sandbagging-then-delivering cadence. The Feb-2025 reset of the 10–15% range was a *credibility-enhancing* move (removing an aspirational target they couldn't hit), not a credibility-destroying miss.

Web-confirmed: the FY26 Q4 print (2026-02-26) beat again — Q4 revenue $1.96B (+19%), non-GAAP EPS $2.85 vs $2.64 expected. The beat streak is intact. There is mild **sandbagging** evident in the repeated "we built some risk into our guidance range" / "prudence on our part" language — management consistently under-promises. Score: **High.**

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **The retired 10–15% growth range** was the dominant Q4-FY25 pressure point (Wong, Wong, Turrin all circled it). Management's answer — "what you're not hearing is a reduction in… long-term growth potential" — was a reframe, but the subsequent acceleration to 12–13% FY27 guidance partially vindicates them.
- **Billings-guide mechanics** (Tilton, Wong, FY26 Q1): analysts repeatedly struggled to reconcile constant-currency-ex-transaction-model billings being cut while reported was reiterated. Management's answers were technically dense but coherent; this is genuine reporting complexity, not evasion — and management explicitly promised "Autodesk will be easier to analyze and understand" once the transitions end.
- **Margin upside vs Starboard** (Turrin, Radke, FY26 Q2): "Why give that FY29 outlook now?" Management's answer — they wanted to update the internal long-term view — is plausible but clearly *also* a response to the activist. The risk analysts are NOT fully pressing: whether 45% underlying is genuinely achievable or whether it requires under-investing in the AI roadmap.
- **AI monetization timing** (Ikeda, FY26 Q3): "any update on… timing of the monetization opportunity." Management's answer was vague — "will play out over time" — the one area where the narrative outruns demonstrated dollars. This is the soft spot to watch.

No analyst-flagged worry in this window later turned into bad news; the deflections were on complexity and timing, not on deteriorating fundamentals.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🟢 **Gross margin held at 91% on +17.5% revenue** — the cleanest pricing-power signal possible; no commoditization despite Siemens/Dassault/PTC competition (FY26 throughout).
🟢 **100% beat rate, guidance raised twice in FY26** (Q2, Q3) — execution credibility is high.
🟢 **Hard, dated FY29 margin commitment (45% underlying)** — converts a vague aspiration into an accountable target; aligns with activist pressure (Q2 FY26).
🟢 **Buyback +50% to ~$1.3B, funded from net cash** — disciplined capital return at a depressed multiple (~$280–306 avg repurchase price, *above* today's ~$198).
🟢 **AECO durably strong** — "sustained investment in data centers, infrastructure, and industrial buildings… more than offsetting softness in commercial" (Q2, Q3) — diversified, multi-year demand.

🔴 **Headline GAAP EPS optically flat (+2.1% FY26)** — restructuring ($105M) + SBC catch-up ($54M) muddy the print; requires investors to look through to TTM/non-GAAP. A naive screen reads this as stalled.
🔴 **AI monetization is still narrative, not dollars** — "will play out over time" (Q3) is the one claim ahead of the evidence.
🔴 **New-business growth remains soft** — management repeatedly notes "headwinds to new business growth"; the strength is renewals/EBAs/transaction-model mechanics, not raw new-logo acquisition. The growth is high-quality but not broadening as fast as a pure secular-disruptor narrative would imply.
🔴 **Billings/FCF tailwinds "significantly diminish next year"** (Q3) — FY27 optical growth rates step down as the annual-billing transition laps; a poorly-informed market could misread the deceleration as fundamental.
🔴 **Insider conviction is weak** — open-market only: 4 buys (5,489 sh) vs 22 sells (52,832 sh). No insider is backing up the truck at $198.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **CFO voice change matters.** Moorjani (arrived Feb 2025) is visibly more numbers-disciplined than the prior regime — he retired an aspirational range, introduced explicit downside-scenario framing ("at roughly the same rate as during the pandemic"), and committed to a dated margin target. This is a tonal upgrade toward shareholder-aligned, Starboard-style rigor.
- **Terminology shift toward "automation/efficiency/self-service"** in the GTM discussion — the language of an *optimizing* business, not a hyper-growth one. The disruptor is maturing into a cash-compounder.
- **The "new transaction model is working well… customers migrating nicely onto the direct model"** (Q1) — direct revenue +35% c/c, 47% of total — is a quiet margin-mix and customer-intimacy upgrade buried in mechanical billings noise.
- **Board refresh** (four new directors, Q1 FY26) plus the FY29 margin target read as a coordinated **governance-and-margin response to Starboard**, even as management declines to credit the activist.
- **AI framed as "for the physical world"** with "decades of digital data" as the moat — management is staking out a defensible data-advantage claim against generic AI entrants (Q3).

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Classification: STRENGTHENING.** This is a dominant, 91%-gross-margin workflow monopoly in AEC and a strong #2/#3 in manufacturing CAD, executing a credible margin-expansion program (activist-validated, FY29 45% underlying target) on top of durable secular demand (data-center/infrastructure construction backlog at multi-year highs), trading at only **17.7x FCF** after a ~30% de-rating to ~$198 near its 52-week low. The earning power is *inflecting* (TTM EPS 6.86, FY27 guide $12.29–$12.56) while the headline GAAP print and billings-normalization optics make it *look* like it's stalling.

**The single most important thing to weigh before sizing:** distinguish the **optical deceleration** (billings/FCF tailwinds lapping, restructuring-muddied GAAP EPS) from any **fundamental deceleration**. The forensic evidence says the slowdown is almost entirely mechanical/optical — underlying revenue growth is steady at ~11–12% c/c, margins are expanding, and the multiple has compressed to a level that prices in terminal stagnation that the numbers do not support. The bear's only real foothold is that *new-business* growth is soft and AI monetization is unproven — i.e., the durability of the *growth rate*, not the *cash flows*. Size this as a high-quality compounder bought at a forced-seller discount, not as a high-velocity catalyst trade.
