# HII — Huntington Ingalls Industries — Forensic Dossier (Interrogator)

*Signal type: disruptor (defense_tech) | Transcripts: Q1 2025 → Q1 2026 (5 calls) | Price $297.68 vs scan fair value $171.21 (MoS −42%)*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

Across five consecutive calls the story did not pivot — it **slowly inverted from apology to confidence** while the underlying business model stayed exactly the same: a sole-source/duopoly naval shipbuilder grinding through a contract-mix transition.

- **Q1 2025 (May)** opened defensively. Revenue actually *fell* 2.5% YoY, Newport News was "modestly behind plan," and the dominant theme was **CVN-80 supply-chain failure** ("the most significant variance in Newport News resides with CVN 80… late major equipment"). Management leaned on external tailwinds it did not yet control — two Trump executive orders on "maritime dominance," an MOU with HD Hyundai, the Babcock relationship — to distract from a soft print. The SAS (Shipbuilder Accountability/Submarine investment) language was *pulled* from the final executive order, and Kastner spent a full Q&A exchange explaining it away ("SAS is something that is a name at this point that could or could not happen").
- **Q2 2025 (Jul)** was the inflection: revenue turned positive (+3.5%), $11.9B of awards (DDG 145/146, LPD 33, 2 Block V subs), backlog jumped to $56.9B, FCF $730M in the quarter. The C3 AI throughput partnership was introduced. Tone shifted from "behind plan" to "stable."
- **Q3 2025 (Oct)** delivered **record sales $3.2B, shipbuilding +18% YoY**, book-to-bill 1.25x at Mission Technologies, and a flurry of unmanned partnerships (Shield AI, Thales, Babcock, ROMULUS USV launch). This is where the *defense-tech disruptor* sub-narrative was deliberately elevated.
- **Q4 2025 (Feb 2026)** is the most important call: management **raised medium-term shipbuilding growth guidance from ~4% to ~6%**, all three divisions hit record revenue, and 2026 throughput target set at +15% (on top of +14% in 2025). But the same call **cut the 2026 FCF guide to $500–600M** and disclosed shipbuilding margin staying pinned at 5.5–6.5%.
- **Q1 2026 (May)** kept the offensive tone — shipbuilding +18% YoY again, $4B awards, Mission Technologies won positions on a $25B microelectronics IDIQ and a $151B MDA Shield ceiling — and notably **introduced Kari Wilkinson (President, Newport News)** to the call, a signal that the Newport News execution story is now front-and-center and being personally owned.

**Growth drivers introduced:** unmanned/autonomy (REMUS, Lionfish, ROMULUS USV, Odyssey software), C3 AI throughput, distributed shipbuilding/outsourcing, international (Babcock/AUKUS, HD Hyundai).
**Quietly dropped / faded:** SAS (gone by Q2), the specific "$50B of awards across 2025–2026" framing (softened to per-award commentary), and the early CVN-80 supply-chain crisis (resolved by Q3 once modules arrived).

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Kari Wilkinson added to the Q1'26 call** as President of Newport News. New voice = management putting a named, accountable owner on the problem yard. Read as a maturation/governance positive.
- Terminology drift from "**throughput**" (2025) to "**autonomy/Odyssey/manned-unmanned teaming**" (2026) — management is consciously repositioning the equity story toward the higher-multiple defense-tech narrative, even though shipbuilding is still ~76% of revenue.
- Capital allocation language is unambiguous and unchanged: "**leading with reinvestments into our shipyards**" — i.e., **CapEx first, shareholders later.** Yield/buyback investors are not the priority for the next several years.
- The "open source / Navy standards" admission on autonomy IP is a subtle but important margin ceiling — HII is an *integrator*, not necessarily an *IP owner*, in its fastest-growing segment.
- Repeated "we'll build whatever the Navy asks" is monopsony deference — pricing power sits with the customer, consistent with the flat gross margin.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director) — **STRENGTHENING (operationally) on a STABLE/monopoly chassis**

**CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION — explicit:** HII's top customer is the **U.S. Government (Navy), which is effectively ~100% of revenue** — a monopsony. There is no diversified commercial book; the "top-customer revenue share" is essentially total. This is the defining structural fact: it delivers decades-long backlog visibility and sole-source/duopoly protection, but it caps pricing power (cost-plus), exposes the company to appropriations/shutdown timing (FY26 CR lapse noted Q3'25), and means political risk (carrier-design reviews, SAS removal, budget reconciliation) is the real tail.

The trajectory is **STRENGTHENING on operations** (throughput, hiring/retention, awards capture, Mission Technologies momentum) but the **economics are STABLE-to-flat** (gross margin pinned ~12.7%, FCF *declining* near-term, ROIC only 6.4%). The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing: **this is a monopoly with no pricing power and a cash trough that lasts until the post-COVID contracts mature (2027+).** The revenue/throughput story is real and improving; the *cash and margin* story is a 2027–2030 promise that has been reiterated for five straight quarters without delivery. Size it as a high-quality, low-ROIC monopoly compounding slowly — NOT as a re-rating disruptor — and only if entered well below the SoP, because at $297 the stock already prices the recovery the cash flow hasn't yet shown.
