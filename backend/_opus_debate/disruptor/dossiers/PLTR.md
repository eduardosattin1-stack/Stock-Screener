## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The story is one of **acceleration, not maturation** — the rarest arc for a company of this scale. Across four calls the central claim never wavered, it intensified:

- **Q1 2025 (May):** Revenue +39% YoY; Rule of 40 at 83%. The framing is "AI demand vs. AI supply" — Shyam Sankar's thesis that the LLMs (the "supply") are commoditizing while Palantir monetizes the "demand" via ontology. US commercial +71% YoY, surpassing a $1B run-rate. The hook word is **"ontology"** and the strategic frame is the "self-driving company."
- **Q2 2025 (Aug):** Revenue accelerates to +48% YoY; Rule of 40 jumps to 94%. US commercial +93%. First $1B *quarter*. New narrative artifact: **"AIP isn't just software our customers use, it's software our customers are building their software on"** — the re-platforming / Ontology Web Services thesis. The $10B Army enterprise agreement (consolidating 75 contracts) lands.
- **Q3 2025 (Nov):** Revenue +63% YoY; Rule of 40 hits **114%**. US commercial +121%. New product artifacts: **AI FDE** (autonomous forward-deployed engineering) and **AI Hivemind**. Headcount up ~10% while revenue grew 63% — the operating-leverage proof point.
- **Q4 2025 (Feb):** Revenue **+70% YoY** (highest as a public company); Rule of 40 at **127%**. US commercial +137%. Top-20 customers now $94M/yr trailing revenue (+45% YoY). FY2026 guide implies ~61% growth — *up from a ~31% initial 2025 guide*.

What was **introduced**: AIP re-platforming (Q2), AI FDE/Hivemind autonomy (Q3-Q4), Warp Speed / reindustrialization / ShipOS as a defense-manufacturing vector (Q3-Q4). What was **dropped**: essentially nothing — the narrative is additive. There is no quiet abandonment of a growth driver, which is itself a credibility signal.

**Trajectory: RISING (to the point of euphoria).**

- Q1 2025: "things are accelerating... we expect more surprises, not less" — confident.
- Q2 2025: "my enthusiasm for our business is at an all-time high" (Taylor); Karp tells investors to "stop talking to all the haters... they're suffering."

The only credibility caveat is that the *forward* guide (61%+) now embeds the market's expectation of continued miracles — the bar they must clear is no longer low.
