# FICO (Fair Isaac Corporation) — Interrogator Forensic Dossier

*Source: five consecutive earnings calls Q2'25 (2025-04-29) through Q1'26 (2026-01-28), cross-referenced against screener financials. Signal type: profitable secular disruptor / fintech-rails toll-taker.*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

Across these five calls the FICO story shifts from a **"price + mortgage-volume" duet** into a **"control the distribution rail" land-grab**, and that is the single most important evolution in the arc.

- **Q2'25 (Apr 2025):** The story is clean and confident — Scores up 25%, mortgage originations revenue up 48%, B2B up 31%, FICO Score 10T adoption building ("$284B in annualized mortgage originations... ~$1.43T in eligible portfolio servicing signed up"). Software is the laggard (revenue +2%, ARR +3%) and management leans on the "land and expand" platform sub-narrative (platform ARR +17%, platform NRR 110%). Tone: serene, almost bored — "it won't happen fast."
- **Q3'25 (Jul 2025):** Acceleration. Scores +34%, mortgage origination revenue +53%, B2B +42%. The new thread introduced: an FHFA announcement has occurred ("Even since the recent FHFA announcement, we signed our latest lender deal just last week"). Management's posture is defiant — they reframe regulatory risk as irrelevant because "we have a better score" and share shift "won't happen fast." Record FCF ($276M) and the largest buyback in history (>$0.5B).
- **Q4'25 (Nov 2025):** **THE PIVOT.** Management unveils the **FICO Mortgage Direct License Program** — selling scores directly to tri-merge resellers (Xactus signed), "eliminating reliance on the 3 nationwide credit bureaus." A new **performance pricing model** ($4.95 per-score historical + a $33 per-closed-loan performance fee) is introduced to "capture some of that IP value" historically given away downstream. This is a deliberate move to disintermediate Equifax/TransUnion/Experian and monetize the entire downstream usage chain (rating agencies, MBS investors, insurers, regulators). The defensive monologue on "FICO score is the standard... only score with known predictable performance through... the Great Recession" is materially longer and more rehearsed than prior calls — a tell that the competitive threat is now real.
- **Q1'26 (Jan 2026):** Execution-mode on the Direct License Program — Xactus, Cotality, Ascend, CIC Credit, MeridianLink added; "resellers representing about 90% of mortgage volume" engaged; one reseller "close to completing production integration testing." Plaid/UltraFICO partnership added (cash-flow data). Gartner Decision Intelligence leader. Platform revenue +37%. The thesis is now explicitly two-engine: (1) extract maximum mortgage-score economics via direct licensing + price, (2) grow FICO Platform software.

**Growth drivers quietly de-emphasized:** B2C Scores (consistently +5–6%, a non-event), and non-platform software ARR (in structural decline, -2% to -13% YoY) — the legacy software base is melting while platform grows off a small base.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rating: Stable-to-defensive (offense narrowing, defense lengthening).**

- Q2'25 — *offensive/serene:* "won't happen fast… we have a better score."
- Q3'25 — *offensive/defiant:* "the market share we're not very worried about, to be frank."
- Q4'25 — *defensive pivot:* a long, lawyerly recitation of why FICO is "the independent standard… the only score with known predictable performance through a complete economic cycle." When a management team spends a full paragraph re-litigating why its product is the standard, the standard is under attack.
- Q1'26 — *execution/hedged:* heavy "conservatism" language on guidance ("a little bit more of a haircut… so much uncertainty"), repeated "your guess is as good as ours" on volumes and the Trump 10% card-rate cap. Confident on bookings momentum, evasive on mortgage volume and regulatory split.

The shift from **growth/pricing offense** to **distribution-control + standard-defense** is unmistakable.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Reliability: HIGH.** FICO raised FY25 guidance in Q3'25 and then "exceeded fiscal '25 guidance on all metrics" (Q4'25). Screener corroborates: beat 6/7 quarters (86%), EPS +29.8% YoY, forward consensus EPS growth +26.1%. Management explicitly describes a **deliberate sandbagging process** — "we build a model… then we will take that and haircut that expectation… we've done that again this year" (Q4'25/Q1'26), and "you'd probably still think we were being too conservative" (Q1'26). This is a credible, repeatedly-validated sandbagger. The only caveat: the FY26 guide leans on mortgage **volume** assumptions that are explicitly outside their control and now also exposed to share risk.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **Mortgage pricing / the $33 performance fee** — analysts (O'Neill, the Oppenheimer line) probe repeatedly; management will not quantify per-loan revenue or the per-score/performance split ("we frankly don't know what the split is going to be," Q4'25). **Unanswered and load-bearing.**
- **Market-share risk from VantageScore/FHFA** — pushed in Q3'25, Q4'25, Q1'26; deflected with "we're not very worried" and "it'll take years." This is exactly the topic that turned into the 2026-04-22 GSE dual-adoption (§ below) — **analysts were right to push; management was dismissive.**
- **Software/PS lumpiness** — recurring questions on professional-services timing and CCS usage softness ("customers chose to delay or downsize… due to macro volatility," Q2'25). Management concedes "we can't really control the usage… I don't know which way it goes" (Q2'25).
- **CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION:** FICO **does not disclose** a top-customer revenue share, and none is given in the inputs — so no single-name concentration figure can be stated. However the dossier must flag the real concentrations: (1) **channel concentration** — the 3 nationwide bureaus (Equifax/TransUnion/Experian) have historically been the distribution chokepoint FICO is now trying to bypass; (2) **geographic concentration** — **86–87% of revenue is Americas** (Q2'25/Q3'25), a heavy US-macro/US-mortgage dependence; (3) **use-case concentration** — mortgage-origination revenue is **~42–44% of total Scores revenue** and the dominant growth driver, so the thesis is disproportionately a bet on US conforming-mortgage economics.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🔴 **VantageScore + FICO 10T dual GSE adoption (2026-04-22)** introduces a credible, ~10x-cheaper competitor into FICO's crown-jewel conforming-mortgage channel — the exact risk management spent three calls dismissing.
🔴 **Aggressive 2026 royalty (to $10/score) + $33 performance fee** has triggered a **Senator Hawley / FTC pricing probe** — monetizing harder right as the political/regulatory spotlight turns on.
🔴 **Net debt $3.44B and negative equity (ROE −110.6%)** from buyback-funded leverage; buybacks executed near the highs ($1,707 in Q1'26 vs ~$1,179 now).
🔴 **Insider open-market: 0 buys / 4 sells** into a 20% drawdown.
🔴 **Software segment growth is only +3%** despite the "platform momentum" narrative; non-platform legacy in structural decline.
🟢 **Best-in-screener pricing power:** GM 82.2% and **expanding** on +16% revenue — textbook moat lie-detector passing.
🟢 **ROIC 41%, FCF margin ~39%, Piotroski 8/9, Altman-Z 10.6** — exceptional cash economics.
🟢 **Direct License Program** is a genuine strategic counter — if it works, FICO disintermediates the bureaus and captures previously-unmonetized downstream IP value (rating agencies, MBS investors, insurers).

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Terminology shift from "score" to "the standard" / "the independent standard."** When a monopolist starts defending its standard-ness rather than selling its product, the moat is being tested.
- **The downstream-monetization reveal (Q4'25):** management openly admits "there's a lot of score volume that happens downstream that we had historically not monetized… per closed loan pricing was designed to capture some of that IP value." This is the real engine of the 2026 price story — and also exactly what attracts regulatory scrutiny.
- **"We don't break that out" / "we don't disclose" recurs** on credit-card exposure, the one-time insurance license, the per-score/performance split, and auto/card pricing — a consistent opacity around the precise mix that is driving the headline growth.
- **New sales leadership** in software ("a new sales leader… excitement around that," Q4'25) and an explicit, multi-year shift to indirect/SI channels — a tacit admission that direct distribution has been under-built relative to the IP ("our IP with our distribution strength… are mismatched," Q2'25).

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Classification: STRENGTHENING operationally, PIVOTING strategically — with a freshly-opened competitive flank.**

FICO is one of the highest-quality, highest-moat compounders in the universe: 82% and expanding gross margin, 41% ROIC, ~39% FCF margin, a near-mandatory toll on US consumer credit, and a credible-sandbagger guidance record. The business is strengthening. **But** the thesis is in the middle of a deliberate strategic pivot — disintermediating the bureaus and monetizing downstream IP via aggressive 2026 pricing — at the exact moment the GSEs (2026-04-22) blessed a ~10x-cheaper VantageScore alternative and a US Senator opened a pricing probe.

**The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing:** whether the mortgage-score growth engine — ~42–44% of Scores revenue, built on *price* not volume — is durable through GSE dual-adoption, or whether VantageScore's $0.99-vs-$10 pricing forces FICO to either hold price and cede conforming share, or defend share and surrender the pricing that drives the whole model. The moat in *non-conforming* and in the broader credit ecosystem is intact; the question is purely how much of the *2026 mortgage price story* survives the new competitive regime. Valuation (forward P/E ~29x, a steep premium to bureau peers at ~12–20x) leaves little margin for the answer to be "less than management implies."
