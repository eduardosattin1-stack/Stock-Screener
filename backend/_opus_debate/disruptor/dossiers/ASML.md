# ASML Holding N.V. — Forensic Dossier (Interrogator)

*Transcripts: 2025-04-16 (Q1'25), 2025-07-16 (Q2'25), 2025-10-15 (Q3'25), 2026-01-28 (Q4/FY'25 press conf), 2026-04-15 (Q1'26). Financials: screener scan + FMP TTM as of 2026-03-31.*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The arc across these five calls is one of **uncertainty resolving into conviction**, then **conviction hardening into a supply-constrained boom** — with the China export overhang as the lone persistent shadow.

- **Q1'25 (Apr):** AI is "the key driver of growth in our industry," but the dominant verb is *uncertainty*. Management hedges 2025 to a wide €30–35B range, and the entire back half of the call is consumed by tariffs ("the end state will be unknown for a while"). 2026 is asserted only as "a growth year." Tone: constructive but defensive.
- **Q2'25 (Jul):** A subtle *retreat* on 2026. Christophe states bluntly: "while we are still paying [planning] for growth in 2026, **we cannot confirm it at this stage**." China backlog took a **€1.4B de-booking** (export-restriction cancellations). The 2025 range is narrowed to ~+15% (€32.5B implied) at 52% GM. This is the low-water mark of confidence.
- **Q3'25 (Oct):** Inflection. "We have seen a flow of positive news in the last few months that have helped to reduce the uncertainty." 2026 is upgraded from "cannot confirm" to "net sales **not below** 2025." The Mistral AI partnership (11% stake, lead Series-C investor) and first advanced-packaging tool (XT260) are introduced as new growth vectors. China is flagged as a 2026 *headwind* ("significantly lower in 2026").
- **Q4/FY'25 (Jan'26):** Full conviction. "Record quarter, record year, record booking." The frustration of 2025 ("we heard about all those investments, but this was not yet translating into capacity addition") is declared *over* — "the last 3 months have... change[d] that." Customers now "believe in the sustainability of the AI demand." Introduces the **"capacity = market share"** framing and the NVIDIA wafer-intensity slide (Blackwell 2.5 wafers → Rubin 10 wafers by 2027 = 4x). Also announces a **1,700-job reorganization** (net of ~1,400 new roles).
- **Q1'26 (Apr):** Boom confirmed. Memory customers are "**sold out for 2026** and their supply constraint will last beyond 2026." 2026 guide *raised and narrowed* to **€36–40B**. Non-EUV revised from "flat" to *growing*. EUV Low-NA output target ≥60 units (2026) → ≥80 (2027). High-NA validated by customers at SPIE (3→1 masks, 100→10 process steps).

**Drivers introduced:** AI capacity supercycle, EUV layer-count growth (DRAM + logic), High-NA phase progression, advanced packaging (3D integration), Mistral AI software content, 1000W source extending Low-NA to 2031. **Quietly de-emphasized:** tariffs (dominant in Q1–Q2'25, near-absent by Q1'26); China is no longer framed as ambiguous but as a known, bounded drag.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **2026 bridge / "how many orders do you need":** Repeatedly pushed (Sandeep Q1'25; Adithya's "€6B order" math Q2'25). Management *consistently deflects* — "I'm not going to give you a grade on your math." This was a genuine uncertainty in mid-2025 that **resolved favorably** by Q1'26 (sold-out memory).
- **Order lumpiness:** Analysts read falling bookings as weakening demand; management insists it's "not a good proxy" (governance-driven lumpiness). Borne out — Q4'25 bookings spiked to €13.2B (web).
- **High-NA timing / dilution / breakeven:** Persistent (Timm Q2'25: "is the program already above breakeven?" — *not directly answered*). High-NA remains a margin *drag* near-term (management openly flags "dilutive effect of High-NA revenue recognition"). This is the one place the bull narrative outruns current economics.
- **China:** Analysts pressed on tariffs/export controls every call. The €1.4B Q2'25 de-booking validated the concern; it has since been contained but **remains the live unknown** (MATCH Act, web).
- **Litho-intensity pause to 2030:** Adithya Q2'25 raised the bear thesis that logic litho-intensity won't rise until ~2030 (High-NA at the largest customer). Christophe rebutted (gate-all-around drives shrink post-1.4nm) but conceded "one node... where there's a bit of a pause." A real, if managed, risk.

🔴 **1,700-job reorganization** (Jan'26) — management frames it as efficiency/de-layering, but a restructuring at the top of a boom is worth watching (execution, not distress, is the read).
🔴 **Single-customer / customer-concentration:** see below.

## CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION (explicit)

The transcripts do **not** disclose a numeric top-customer revenue share, and the input metrics carry no customer-concentration figure. However, the structural concentration is severe and well-known: ASML's EUV revenue is effectively split across a **handful of leading-edge buyers — TSMC, Samsung, SK Hynix, Intel, Micron** — with TSMC historically the single largest (commonly ~20–30% of system sales in the industry's understanding; **not stated in these materials, do not treat as sourced**). The Q1'26 call confirms demand is driven by "memory customers (DRAM)" and "advanced logic customers" — i.e. the same five names. Geographic concentration is also explicit: **China >25% of revenue in 2025, guided to ~20% in 2026**, with a €1.4B China de-booking already realized. Net: top-handful customer + China-geography concentration is a real risk, but it is the *unavoidable* structure of being the leading-edge litho monopolist — these are the only customers that exist at the leading edge.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **From "if AI demand continues" (Q1'25) to "supply will not meet demand for the foreseeable future" (Q1'26)** — the conditional became a declarative. That is the single most important terminology shift.
- **Software repositioning:** the Mistral stake (Q3'25) reframes ASML from a hardware vendor to a company with "very significant software content" — a moat-deepening narrative (precision/speed of scanners is increasingly software-bound).
- **"Capacity = market share" (Q4'25):** signals customers are now racing for slots, inverting the buyer/seller power balance toward ASML.
- **3D integration / advanced packaging (XT260):** a genuine *new TAM lane* beyond lithography, quietly introduced.
- **Reorg framing:** Christophe's "free practically about 3,000 people... create 1,400 position" and Dassen's "matrix... becomes so complex you've got to act" — confident, not defensive; read as house-cleaning at scale, not stress.
- **Energy, not metals:** when asked about gold/silver, Christophe pivoted to "we worry a lot more about energy cost... availability" — a tell that the binding constraint on the AI buildout (and thus ASML's customers) is power, not ASML's own inputs.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Trajectory: STRENGTHENING.** Across five calls the business moved from defensively-hedged (tariffs, "cannot confirm 2026") to supply-constrained boom (sold-out memory, raised €36–40B guide, ≥80-unit EUV plan for 2027), while *holding* 52%+ gross margins on rising revenue and a net-cash, 30%+ ROIC balance sheet. The moat is intact and arguably deepening (software, High-NA, advanced packaging). Capital allocation is exemplary — large buybacks executed into strength, dividend grown, net cash maintained, a strategic 11% Mistral stake.

**The single most important thing to weigh before sizing:** this is a *valuation* decision, not a *quality* decision. The company is unambiguously a load-bearing secular monopoly with strengthening fundamentals — but at €1,863 it trades at 63x FCF, ~35% **above** every intrinsic model (Buffett €1,121, DCF €542) and *below* the analyst consensus target (€1,694). You are not debating whether ASML is great; you are debating whether to pay a 35%-premium-to-intrinsic price for greatness at 97% of its 52-week range, with the China/MATCH-Act export risk as the only thing that could supply a better entry. Quality conviction should be high; price conviction should be guarded.
