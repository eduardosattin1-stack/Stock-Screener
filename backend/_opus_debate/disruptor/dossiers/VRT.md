# Vertiv Holdings Co (VRT) — Forensic Dossier

*Signal type: disruptor (profitable secular disruptor) | Sector: Industrials | Transcripts: Q1 2025 → Q1 2026 (5 calls)*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

Across five consecutive calls the story does not pivot — it **compounds and accelerates**, which is itself the most important narrative fact. The through-line set in Q1 2025 ("the digital revolution is happening… data centers remain fundamental to all of it," Cote) is repeated almost verbatim through Q1 2026 ("We're still in the early stage of the infrastructure build out for AI," Cote, Q1'26). What *changes* is the magnitude of the proof points stacked behind the claim:

- **Backlog**: $7.9B (Q1'25) → $8.5B (Q2'25) → $9.5B (Q3'25) → **$15B (Q4'25, "more than double last year's")** → reaffirmed >$15B (Q1'26). The backlog roughly doubled in a single year, with Q4'25 alone adding ~$5.5B (+57% sequential).
- **Orders**: TTM organic orders growth migrated from 20% (Q1'25) → 11% (Q2'25, tough comp) → 21% (Q3'25) → **81% (Q4'25)**, with Q4'25 orders up 252% YoY and book-to-bill of **2.9x**. This is the single most violent data point in the file.
- **Margin**: adjusted operating margin climbed 18.5% (Q2'25, tariff-pressured) → 22.3% (Q3'25) → 23.2% (Q4'25) → guided to 23.3% for Q1'26 (printed 20.8%, still +430bps YoY and +180bps vs guide).
- **Guidance ladder**: FY25 sales guide raised serially ($10B target by Q3); FY26 EPS guide stepped from $6.02 (set Q4'25) to **$6.35 (raised Q1'26, +51% YoY)** with op-profit raised to $3.2B.

Two growth drivers were *introduced* mid-stream and are now load-bearing: (1) **converged prefabricated systems** — OneCore (full-DC blocks, 12.5MW increments, Hut 8 / Compass references) and SmartRun (white-space fit-out) — reframing Vertiv from a component vendor into a "connective tissue between gray and white space" systems integrator; (2) **services as a moat** ("one of Vertiv's superpowers," Q3'25), bolstered by WeiLay/predictive-analytics and the ThermoKey / Strategic Thermal Labs / fluid-management bolt-ons.

One driver was *quietly de-emphasized*: **EMEA**. It went from "lags" (Q1'25) to "flat" (Q2'25) to "-4%" (Q3'25) to "-14% / -29% organic" (Q4'25 / Q1'26), reframed each quarter as a "coiled spring" about to uncoil "in the second half of 2026." This is the one place the narrative runs ahead of the numbers (see §2).

A material *disclosure change*: starting Q4'25, management **stopped reporting quarterly orders and backlog** ("it just seems to lead to excessive volatility… not beneficial to our investors," Q4'25). They will disclose annual figures in the 10-K only. This is defensible given genuine order lumpiness (252% YoY swings) but it removes the single best leading indicator from the public's hands at the exact moment the cycle is hottest — a reduction in transparency that the director must weigh.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY — **RISING**

- **Q1'25** (defensive-tinged): heavy tariff hedging — "we'll continue to focus on what we can't control… we'll adapt as the situation becomes clearer." Confident on demand, cautious on cost.
- **Q2'25**: tariff still front-of-mind ("temporary costs… higher than we initially estimated"), but "momentum in our business is accelerating."
- **Q3'25** (inflection to offensive): "this is a very strong quarter by any measure… I wonder what would have happened if we hadn't blown the doors off of every single metric" (Cote). Tariff demoted to "another cost to our business."
- **Q4'25** (peak offensive): "the coiled spring is uncoiling"; "winning now and winning later"; "I'm more confident today than I absolutely have ever been."
- **Q1'26**: "What we're seeing in customer conversations is different than 6 months ago. The urgency has increased. The scale [of] deployment is larger." Tariffs reframed as a *competitive advantage*: "they raise the bar in ways that favors established players like us."

Decisively a shift **from defensive (tariff/cost) to offensive (share-gain/capacity/urgency)** language as 2025 progressed. Guidance specificity *increased* (explicit FY26 EPS, op-margin, op-profit targets) even as orders disclosure was withdrawn.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY — **HIGH**

The metrics block shows **7/7 quarterly beats (100%)**. The transcript corroborates a serial *raise-and-beat* pattern: FY25 EPS guide held at $3.55 midpoint under tariff pressure (Q1'25) then walked up to $3.80 (Q2'25) and beaten; FY26 EPS set at $6.02 (Q4'25) and raised to $6.35 within one quarter (Q1'26), with Q1'26 itself beating its own guide by $0.19 EPS and +180bps on margin. ## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Terminology evolution**: "component/product" → "converged system / connective tissue / OneCore / SmartRun" — Vertiv is deliberately repositioning up the value chain from box-seller to system architect, raising switching costs (you can't easily route around an integrated 12.5MW block) and defending content/MW.
- **TAM/MW disclosure creep**: management narrowed the per-MW TAM toward the *upper* end ($3.5M) — a quiet upgrade buried in a Q&A answer, signaling rising content capture per deployment.
- **Capital allocation shift**: M&A moved from absent (Q1'25) to "important element… not shy about cutting bigger checks" (Q2'25 Great Lakes → Q1'26 ThermoKey/STL). CapEx guided up to 3-4% of sales (from 2-3%) — investing ahead of the curve, consistent with a land-grab, not harvest.
- **CFO transition**: David Fallon (12+ calls) retired; Craig Chamberlin took over Q4'25. New CFO's voice is more conservative on incrementals ("guiding the lower end… ramp-up has a little pressure") — a fresh, cautious hand on the margin bridge, which *adds* credibility to the sandbagging read.
- **"Lumpiness" framing**: management has pre-conditioned the market for volatile quarters, insulating the stock (and themselves) from a single soft order print — savvy expectation management, but also a shield.
