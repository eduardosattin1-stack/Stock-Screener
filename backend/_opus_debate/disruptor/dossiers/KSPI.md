## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The story Kaspi tells did not change in substance across these five calls — it *contracted in ambition for the bottom line while expanding in geography*. The constant spine is the same one Lomtadze has repeated for years: "we are a transactional business... we allow sellers to sell, buyers to buy" (Q2'25), monetized through three platforms (Payments, Marketplace, Fintech) over a single super-app with world-leading engagement (~7 transactions/consumer/month, a number cited unchanged in every call).

What *evolved*:

- **From a clean three-platform domestic compounder to a two-geography build-out.** Early-2025 calls were about Kazakhstan verticals (e-Grocery, restaurants, advertising, Kaspi QR). By FY'25 and Q1'26 the center of gravity shifted to **Turkey via Hepsiburada** (acquired end-Jan 2025, fully consolidated by Q1'26) plus the **Rabobank Turkey license** ($300M initial funding, regulatory close "by summer" 2026 — still pending as of Q1'26). The narrative widened from "dominant in Kazakhstan" to "replicate the Kaspi playbook in a 5x-larger market."

- **A growth driver was quietly *demoted*, not dropped: smartphones.** Introduced in Q1'25 as a "temporary" GMV drag from a Kazakh phone-registration law (~7pts off e-commerce growth), it was re-litigated as temporary in *every subsequent call* (Q2: "temporary setback"; Q3: iPhone-17 supply shortage, "~8% impact on GMV, ~3% on consolidated net income"; FY'25: still listed among external headwinds). A "temporary" problem that persists across a full year and four calls is a narrative liability — management kept the demand intact by pointing to ex-smartphone growth (+25–31%).

- **Capital allocation flipped from suspension to resumption.** 2025 was an "extensive year for investments" (Turkey, banking license, euro/USD bonds) with dividends paused; by Q3'25 they pulled forward a $100M buyback "considering where we are in the stock," and by FY'25/Q1'26 reinstated a dividend (KZT 850/ADS, ~64% payout). The pivot from "prioritize investment" to "balance investment and return of capital" is the single biggest narrative shift.

- **A new emphasis on *engaged* vs raw consumers.** Q1'26 and FY'25 leaned hard on "we would rather have 1 million engaged consumers than 10 million one-offs" — a framing that *pre-empts* skepticism about Turkey's slower, marketing-heavy ramp.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**STABLE, leaning faintly defensive.** Lomtadze's register is unusually consistent call-to-call — confident, long-horizon ("5, 10 years' perspective"), and explicitly de-emphasizing competition ("we are focused to the lesser extent on competition," Q2'25).

- Q1'25: matter-of-fact, "within our expectations," first acknowledgment of smartphone drag and high rates.
- Q2'25: confident on deposits (+207%), Kaspi QR (+128%); first heavy Turkey/Rabobank framing.
- Q3'25: most *defensive* call — leads with results "without excluding effect of external factors," itemizes five headwinds (smartphones −8% GMV, securities tax, reserve requirements, base rate 15.25%→16.5% = −4% net income). The pulled-forward $100M buyback reads as a confidence signal *into* a soft tape.
- FY'25: re-offensive — dividend reinstated, Pay-by-palm "unprecedented adoption," Turkey progress.
- Q1'26: streamlined call format (a tell that management wants fewer detailed quarterly inquisitions); CEO discloses he *personally bought stock alongside Tencent* — a deliberate confidence gesture.

Shift from offensive→defensive→offensive tracks the Kazakh rate cycle precisely. The defensive vocabulary is always *macro/regulatory*, never *competitive* — consistent with a monopoly absorbing exogenous shocks.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY — **HIGH**

Management guides specifically and reconciles honestly:
- Q3'25 flagged Q4 marketplace deceleration ("you will see marketplace growth moderate in the fourth quarter") — delivered.
- Q1'26 fintech: guided TFE +5% for the year, *printed −2%*, and explained it as a deliberate mix shift toward longer-duration, higher-revenue loans (avg portfolio +23%, duration 7→9.3 months). They missed the origination number but framed the *better* underlying economics — a credible, non-evasive miss.
- Dividend: said in H1 cash returns would start from the beginning of the year; started the buyback early to honor it.
- The recurring "underlying vs reported" framing is the one credibility risk — it is a real adjustment (external taxes, reserve rules, rates are genuinely exogenous) but it lets a flat GAAP year be narrated as +18%. Sophisticated, defensible, but worth watching for normalization. Reliability: **High**, with a yellow flag on the adjusted-vs-reported gap.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **Payments take-rate decay** (asked Q1'25, Q1'26): management consistently concedes a structural grind toward the 0.95% Kaspi QR floor as B2B/QR gain mix — *answered straight, not deflected*, but it is a genuine long-run revenue headwind on the largest, most mature platform.
- **Dividend/payout magnitude** (Reggie Smith JPM, multiple calls): repeatedly pushed for a payout ratio; management refused to commit ("too early," "we'll find the balance"). Eventually resolved at ~64%.
- **Rabobank/Hepsiburada structure** (Wood & Co, Q1'25): pointed question on why Rabo was bought through the Kazakh entity not Hepsi (which has minorities) — David Ferguson conceded "Hepsi doesn't have the money" and that the structure would be "structured in lots of different ways" later. A minority-overhang / related-party complexity analysts are circling.
- **Turkey boycott drag** (Q1'25): deflected to "a question for the Hepsi management team."
- **Cost of risk "too low for the macro"** (Q1'25): management reframed it as a volume choice, not a credit-quality concern — plausible but the kind of answer that ages badly if NPLs turn.

No question turned into a later *blow-up* in this window; the deflections are on structure and payout, not on asset quality or accounting.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)
