# DLO (dLocal Limited) — Interrogator Forensic Dossier

*Source: 5 consecutive earnings calls, Q1 2025 → Q1 2026 (calls dated 2025-05-14, 2025-08-13, 2025-11-12, 2026-03-18, 2026-05-14). Cross-referenced against screener financials (Rev +55.8% YoY, 3-yr CAGR +37.7%, FCF margin 32.9%, TTM FCF $400M, TTM diluted EPS $0.64, latest-Q EPS YoY -7%, NET CASH $911M).*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The core story is remarkably **stable and reiterative** across all five calls — almost monotonously so, which is itself a signal (management is selling consistency). The single organizing idea never changes: *"This is a race to scale… focused on TPV growth, focused on gross profit dollar growth and being scale leaders across emerging markets"* (Q4'25). Every call repeats the same flywheel — high TPV growth in a $2T-and-doubling TAM, ~150% net revenue retention, asset-light high-cash-conversion model, an expanding regulatory/licensing moat (37→38 licenses across 26 markets, 16 applications in process incl. the US).

What **evolves** is the *defense of the weak point*. In Q1'25 the bear concern management pre-empts is take-rate: *"the bearish thesis that the repricing last year was the first shoe to drop. By now, we can eliminate that thesis"* — then, in the same breath, concedes *"there's a secular trend towards take rate decline. We need to manage around that."* Across the year the language hardens from "we can eliminate that thesis" to an open admission that take rate is **an output, not a managed metric**: *"We continue to see take rate as an output metric. The metrics we manage [are] TPV growth"* (Q4'25). That is a real, quiet thesis reframing — from "take-rate stabilizes" to "stop looking at take rate, look at gross-profit dollars."

New growth vectors are *introduced* steadily and mostly *kept*: stablecoins (Circle/BVNK/Fireblocks/Felix, "full suite" launched ~Apr 2026), BNPL "Fuse" (live in 6 countries, +88% QoQ in Q4'25), Smart APMs / APMs-on-file (27 APMs / 16 countries), card-present/offline POS (still not live, "second half of this year," funded by one large client), Asia build-out (Vietnam/Philippines), and AI-agent payments (Google AP2). These are real and additive but **all still small in the '26 guidance** — management is candid about this (*"those are still quite small in terms of their impacts in the '26 guidance"*).

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rating: Stable-to-Rising on growth conviction; quietly Defensive on monetization.**

- Q1'25 — defensive on Brazil/Mexico ("no structural issues, this is about execution… let's see what happens over the next few quarters") and on take rate ("eliminate that thesis").
- Q2'25 — more offensive: S-curve framework introduced, *"early stages of the S-curve,"* product launches (SmartPix, BNPL, stablecoins).
- Q3'25 — confident: first >$10B TPV quarter, NRR 149%, *"complexity and fragmentation… is not waning, but rather increasing"* (reframes the moat as *growing*).
- Q4'25 — peak offensive: 10-yr/82% CAGR retrospective, *"flywheel is accelerating,"* <2% market share / ~10% share-of-wallet runway.
- Q1'26 — celebratory (decade + IPO anniversary) but with a clear **defensive pivot to cost**: repeated emphasis that *"the pace of incremental headcount and the pace of incremental investment… will significantly decelerate so that the operational leverage can begin to kick in."*

The tone shift over the year is from *"invest to grow"* to *"we've invested, now leverage shows up in H2'26."* That is a confident message but it is a **promissory** one — the operating leverage is repeatedly deferred to "the next few quarters / second half."

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Reliability: Medium-High.**
- TPV guidance has been consistently *beaten* — guided 50-60% TPV growth, delivered +60% FY25 and +73% in Q1'26. Strong positive surprise on volume.
- Take-rate guidance was **accurate and honest** — they told us in Q1-Q2'25 that take rate would be "slightly down, almost flat… above 100bps full year" and it landed there before stepping down again. No sandbagging, no overpromising on monetization.
- The repeated promise that *operating leverage kicks in "in the second half"* is the one **un-delivered** item to watch — it has been deferred across multiple calls. Q1'26 bottom line (+11% ex-one-offs, EPS -7%) shows leverage has **not yet** arrived. Credibility on leverage = **Medium until H2'26 proves it.**

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

The Q&A is dominated, call after call, by **the same two questions**, which tells you exactly what the street worries about:
1. **Take rate / net-take-rate compression** (Cassie Chan, Matt Coad, Neha Agarwala, James Friedman — every single call). Management's answer is always a redirection to "manage to TPV / gross-profit dollars; take rate is an output." Analysts keep pushing because the answer never fully satisfies — the APM mix shift is *structurally* lower-take-rate (Q3'25: APMs "lower in net take rate because they tend to be cheaper"), and new merchants are *not* reliably accretive (Q4'25: "not that easy to generalize").
2. **OpEx / when does leverage arrive** (John Coffey, Neha Agarwala, James Friedman — repeatedly). Management keeps guiding opex *up* through '25 and defers leverage to H2'26.

Topics analysts pushed on that became (mild) bad news: Brazil/Mexico softness flagged in Q1'25 → "normalization" again in Q1'26; Egypt share-of-wallet loss (Q3'25). Underaddressed worry: **customer concentration** — management actively *reframes* it (cohort-basis argument that concentration "decreases over time") rather than disclosing the hard top-10 number, which the 20-F puts at **61%**.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🔴 **Take rate in secular decline, now openly conceded as un-managed** (1.05%→0.84% GP/TPV in 4 quarters) — the moat is *not* pricing power.
🔴 **Customer concentration 61% in top 10** (20-F) while management talks around it with cohort math; 2026 growth explicitly *"concentrated in a merchant perspective"* and dependent on delivering large global contracts.
🔴 **Bottom-line deceleration** — FY25 NI +63% but Q1'26 NI +11% ex-one-offs, EPS -7% YoY; operating leverage repeatedly deferred.
🔴 **FX / EM macro fragility** — Argentine peso bond hit (Q2'25), Mexico tariff/de-minimis risk, "global macro… how that flows through to FX" named as the biggest uncontrollable risk (Q1'26).
🟢 **Best-in-class retention** — TPV retention 158%, NRR 145-149%, durable across years. This is the real moat datapoint.
🟢 **TPV accelerating off a larger base** (+73% in Q1'26) — secular EM digitalization demand is real and broadening (44→60+ countries, vertical diversification, top-3 markets <50% of rev).
🟢 **Fortress balance sheet** — NET CASH $911M, asset-light, ~97% FCF conversion; optionality for accretive M&A of "subscale funded fintechs."
🟢 **Genuine regulatory/licensing moat** — 38 licenses / 26 markets, local card-scheme coverage (Mada, Verve, Meeza) that incumbents cannot replicate quickly; +20pp conversion uplift vs international acquiring.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Terminology shift**: take rate goes from something to be "managed" (Q1'25) to an "output metric" you should stop modeling (Q4'25) — a deliberate de-emphasis of the metric the bears care about.
- **Moat reframed as *increasing* complexity**: from Q3'25 onward management argues fragmentation is *growing* ("not waning, but rather… increasing"), recasting EM messiness as a durable tailwind rather than a risk.
- **CFO turnover**: Diego Cabrera Canay departed; Jeffrey Brown served as *Interim* CFO through 2025; Guillermo López Pérez installed as permanent CFO by Q4'25. The new CFO's voice is notably more granular on opex annualization and H2'26 leverage mechanics — a tightening of cost discipline messaging.
- **M&A posture turned active** (Q1'25: "Corp Dev team is spending their time" on subscale fintech targets at "attractive valuations") — watch for capital deployment given the $911M cash pile.
- **Card-present pivot**: management reversed its prior "offline isn't fast-growing, we don't want it" stance — now building card-present *because a large client is funding the build*. Reactive, not strategic, and still not live.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Classification: STRENGTHENING (on volume/franchise) but with a STRUCTURAL MONETIZATION DRAG.**

dLocal is a genuinely strengthening franchise — accelerating TPV, best-in-class retention, a real and widening regulatory/licensing moat, fortress net-cash balance sheet, high cash conversion. This is not a broken story. **But the single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing is the gross-margin lie-detector: the moat is load-bearing for *access* but it is NOT pricing power.** Take rate has fallen 1.05%→0.84% in four quarters and management has stopped defending it, redirecting attention to gross-profit *dollars* and a *promised* H2'26 operating-leverage inflection that has been serially deferred. The bull case requires (a) that promised leverage to actually arrive and (b) TPV growth to outrun take-rate decay so GP-dollar compounding continues. The bear case is commoditization — that the rails become a low-margin utility whose 61%-concentrated mega-merchants extract the pricing. Size to the GM trajectory and the H2'26 leverage proof, not to the headline TPV.
