# GE Aerospace (GE) — Forensic Dossier

*Interrogator pass. Transcripts: Q1'25 (2025-04-25) through Q1'26 (2026-04-21), six consecutive calls. Financials per screener scan + FMP TTM as of 2026-03-31.*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The core story is remarkably **stable and self-reinforcing** across all six quarters — this is not a thesis that mutated, it is one that **compounded**. The spine never changed: GE Aerospace is a pure-play jet-engine OEM running a "razor-and-blade" installed-base toll model (~80,000 engines in service, ~70% of revenue from recurring high-margin services), throttled not by demand but by **supply-chain throughput**, with FLIGHT DECK (the proprietary lean operating system) as the lever that converts a fixed backlog into shippable revenue.

What *evolved* is the magnitude and the framing of constraint:

- **Q1'25 → the constraint is supply, and we're losing a little.** "Total engine units were down 6% with LEAP down 13%... lighter than we expected." The backlog was ~$170B; tariffs were the live macro worry (a ~$500M net cost after mitigation). Tone was confident-but-hedged: "operating from a position of strength" while "watching demand closely."
- **Q2'25 (Investor Update) → the constraint is loosening, raise the long-term bar.** Management raised the 2028 outlook to ~$11.5B operating profit / ~$8.5B FCF (each +$1.5B), lifted the services growth algorithm from "high-single-digit" to "double-digit," and added +$4B to the 2028 services revenue line vs the prior March view. Backlog ~$175B. The Air India AI171 tragedy was acknowledged; Paris Air Show was pre-empted by this update.
- **Q3'25 → the supply chain is unlocking, and it's showing up in output.** Revenue +26%, profit +26%, EPS +44%, FCF conversion >130%. LEAP deliveries +40% YoY (a record), defense units +83% (second straight quarter >80%). LEAP full-year delivery growth raised from "15–20%" to ">20%." This is the inflection quarter where the narrative ("we will accelerate output") became fact.
- **Q4'25 / FY → milestone pulled forward.** Orders +74% in the quarter, +32% FY; backlog jumped to ~$190B (+$20B YoY). Management declared it would hit the ~$10B operating-profit milestone **in 2026, two years early**. 2026 guide: revenue up low-double-digits, op profit $9.85–10.25B, EPS $7.10–7.40, FCF $8.0–8.4B.
- **Q1'26 → a genuine macro shock arrives, and the toll model absorbs it.** The Middle East conflict (Operation Epic Fury, late Feb '26) cut Middle East departures (~5% of GE's total) and pushed full-year departures guidance down from "mid-single-digit" to "flat-to-low-single-digit." Yet orders were +87%, revenue +29%, EPS +25% to $1.86, commercial services +39%, LEAP deliveries +63% to 520 units. Management held full-year guidance and signaled "trending toward the high end." The key tell: when air-traffic softens, services lag and then "spring back" — demand "gets pushed out as opposed to going missing indefinitely."

**Drivers introduced and kept:** LEAP fleet tripling by 2030; external/third-party MRO channel (now ~15% of LEAP shop visits, targeted to ~30% by 2030); CFM56 longevity (retirements ~1.6%, two-thirds of fleet has not yet had a *second* shop visit). **Drivers quietly de-emphasized:** tariffs (dominant in Q1'25, barely mentioned by Q1'26 after the U.S.–U.K. zero-for-zero aerospace deal). Nothing material was dropped to hide a problem — the dropped items genuinely resolved.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rising → Stable-High, with disciplined hedging.** This is the rare profile where confidence rose *as* a macro shock landed, because the shock hit the *demand* line and the model monetizes *installed base*.

- Q1'25: measured. "We are watching demand closely... we're operating from a position of strength." Tariffs front-of-mind.
- Q2'25: assertive. Raised 2028 targets; "GE Aerospace is an exceptional franchise with real tremendous competitive advantages."
- Q3'25: confident-operational. "Operational momentum is building." Refused to claim a single "breakthrough" — credibility-positive ("it's the cumulative compounding effect... it's that simple, ain't that hard").
- Q4'25/Q1'26: confident-but-prudent. Q1'26 leans deliberately defensive on the macro overlay ("conservative... cautious... prudent, whatever words you want to use") while the underlying order book screams offense (+87% orders). Culp explicitly says absent the conflict "we would have raised our guidance."

No shift from offensive to defensive in *substance* — only a thin macro-prudence veneer layered over an accelerating order book. The language stayed offensive on capacity/R&D ($3B/yr, $1B/yr U.S. capex two years running) throughout.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY — **HIGH**

The record is exceptional. **Beat 7/7 quarters (100%).** Specific, dated, multi-year guides that were subsequently *raised*, not walked back:
- 2028 op-profit target raised +$1.5B (Q2'25) and reaffirmed.
- The ~$10B op-profit milestone **pulled forward two years** (Q4'25) — and on track to hit at the 2026 midpoint.
- LEAP delivery guide raised intra-year (15–20% → >20%, Q3'25), then delivered.
- Q1'26: held the full-year guide through a real macro shock and is "trending to the high end."

There is a mild **sandbagging pattern** (the Q1'26 "we'd have raised but for the conflict" framing preserves upside optionality), which is a *positive* credibility signal, not a negative one. **Reliability: HIGH.** The only caveat is base-effect: YoY growth rates must decelerate from here, and one quarter (latest-Q EPS −1% YoY) already shows the law of large numbers biting.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

Analysts circled the same three pressure points every quarter; management's answers held up:
1. **Aftermarket decoupling from air traffic** (Mikus Q3'25, Poponak Q2'25): "has anything structurally changed... that could cause aftermarket growth to decouple from ASK growth?" Management's answer — pent-up shop visits below 2019 levels, heavier work-scopes, external-channel growth, price — is credible but **is the load-bearing assumption of the whole thesis.** If departures stay weak for *years* (not quarters), the "demand gets pushed out, not lost" claim is the one that breaks.
2. **CES margin trajectory** (Kahyaoglu, Seifman Q4'25/Q1'26): margins guided *flat* in 2026 despite strong services, because OE/LEAP mix and GE9X losses are headwinds. Management concedes margin expansion is back-half-2028+ ("9X losses peak in 2028... beyond that is when we expect accelerated margin expansion"). This is an honest deferral, but it means **near-term margin upside is capped** — a point the multiple ignores.
3. **Supply chain — "what broke open?"** (Poponak Q2'25, Khanna Q3'25/Q1'26): management consistently refuses to claim a silver bullet, attributing gains to cumulative FLIGHT DECK work. Honest, but it also means the ramp is *operationally earned every quarter* and can stall.

No question turned into hidden bad news across the window — the deflections were genuine "we don't know yet" answers (e.g., duration of pent-up demand), not evasions of a known problem.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🟢 **Gross margin STABLE at 36.8% on +18.5% revenue growth** — the moat lie-detector reads clean. Pricing power on aftermarket parts is holding even as volume ramps; no commoditization signal.
🟢 **Beat 7/7; 2028 targets raised then reaffirmed; $10B milestone pulled forward 2 years.** Elite execution credibility.
🟢 **Backlog $170–190B, +$20B YoY; 95%+ of 2026 spare-parts revenue already in backlog.** Revenue is contracted, not hoped-for.
🟢 **FCF conversion >100–130%; FCF +97.5% YoY.** Earnings are cash.
🟢 **Macro shock absorbed:** held guidance through the Middle East conflict because the model monetizes installed base, not current departures.

🔴 **Valuation: −54% margin of safety to Buffett FV ($154 vs $335); P/FCF 49.3x; DCF FV $80; EVERY intrinsic model −40% to −100%.** The price embeds flawless execution through 2028+.
🔴 **ROIC 4.0%** vs ROE 20.1% — consolidated invested-capital base is inefficient (legacy goodwill/intangibles); incremental ROIC on services is high but the headline is weak.
🔴 **Insider conviction is negative:** 0 open-market buys vs 6 sells (38,198 sh). No one inside is buying at this price.
🔴 **Base effect / decelerating second derivative:** latest-Q EPS −1% YoY; consensus forward EPS growth +13.9% vs the +36% just printed. The hyper-growth phase is maturing.
🔴 **Near-term margins guided FLAT (2026)** — GE9X losses and OE mix cap the margin story until ~2028.
🟡 **Single-platform demand concentration on air traffic:** if the departures air-pocket extends beyond "several quarters," the "pushed out, not lost" assumption is the thesis's structural fault line.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Terminology shift from "supply chain" framing to "Technology & Operations (T&O)" institutionalization.** In Q4'25 management folded T&O into CES under Mohammad Ali and elevated the customer-facing org to report directly to Culp — they are **hardwiring the lean gains into the org chart**, a signal they believe the throughput improvement is structural, not a one-off.
- **External MRO channel is being deliberately *grown*, not defended.** Management is licensing third parties (Delta TechOps, Iberia, MTU Dallas — now 7 Premier MROs) to service LEAP. Counter-intuitively bullish: they are choosing volume/spares-parts pull-through over capturing 100% of the labor, because parts is where the margin lives. This is a confident, share-of-wallet-over-share-of-labor strategy.
- **"Sold out through the decade" on LEAP and GEnx** (Q3'25) — language shifted from "growing backlog" to capacity being the only binding constraint. Demand is no longer the question.
- **Russell Stokes retirement (29 yrs)** announced Q4'25 — orderly succession, no governance red flag; new voices (Ghai as CFO throughout, Mohammad Ali on CES/T&O) are operationally consistent with Culp's playbook.
- **CFM56 "two-thirds of fleet has not had a second shop visit yet"** (Q1'26) — a quietly enormous tell: the highest-margin, highest-work-scope aftermarket revenue is still *ahead*, not behind. This is the under-appreciated annuity.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Classification: STRENGTHENING.** This is a genuinely strengthening franchise — an entrenched aftermarket toll-taker (with Safran via CFM, ~70% of single-aisle) whose backlog, output ramp, pricing discipline (stable GM), and execution record (7/7 beats, milestone pulled forward two years) are all corroborated by the hard financials and verified by current external sources (Q1'26: orders +87%, LEAP +63%, backlog ~$190B). FLIGHT DECK is being institutionalized; the second-shop-visit annuity is largely ahead.

**The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing:** *This is the right business at the wrong price.* The business deserves a 4–5; the **stock at $335 trades at ~49x P/FCF / ~43x forward P/E with a −54% margin of safety to even a generous Buffett fair value**, discounting a flawless ramp through 2028 with zero allowance for the departures air-pocket extending. The capital-allocation question is not "is GE a great company" (it is) but "is there any margin of safety left after a stock that has already round-tripped from near-zero to an 89th-percentile 52-week position." The forensic answer: operationally STRENGTHENING, but the price has out-run even an elite trajectory.
