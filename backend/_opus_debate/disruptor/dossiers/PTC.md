# PTC Inc. (PTC) — Forensic Dossier

*Interrogator pass — six consecutive quarterly calls, Apr 2025 (FQ2'25) through May 2026 (FQ2'26), cross-referenced against the screener financial trajectory. Industrial software / PLM-CAD-ALM-SLM. signal_type = disruptor (robotics_automation theme).*

---

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The story over these six quarters is one of **deliberate portfolio focus + a self-inflicted go-to-market (GTM) rebuild, narrated through a worsening-then-stabilizing macro**.

- **FQ2'25 (Apr-2025):** Two narratives collide. Management leads with execution ("10% ARR growth, 13% FCF growth") and a 5-pillar focus (PLM, ALM, SLM, CAD, SaaS), but the call is dominated by the **April-2025 tariff shock**. Barua cuts the ARR high-end from 10% to 9% and *introduces* a new 7% low-end, explicitly framing it as "about the potential timing and sizing of projects, not about fundamental demand." The thesis seed planted here: digital transformation is *non-discretionary*, so the macro is a delay, not a demand-kill.
- **FQ3'25 (Jul-2025):** Tone inflects from defense to cautious offense — "we are past the point of maximum disruption." A **new growth vector is introduced**: the NVIDIA relationship and "physical AI" — product data as "connective tissue" for AI. This is the first time the AI narrative becomes load-bearing rather than a feature checklist.
- **FQ4'25 (Nov-2025):** The pivotal quarter. Management announces the **divestiture of Kepware + ThingWorx (the legacy IoT businesses) to TPG** for up to $725M, sharpening the company to CAD/PLM/ALM/SLM. Simultaneously introduces "**record deferred ARR under contract**" as the new visibility metric and reframes the whole company around an "**Intelligent Product Lifecycle**" vision. FY26 ARR guide set at 7-9% (7.5-9.5% ex-divestiture); $1B FCF target.
- **FQ1'26 (Feb-2026):** "Turning the corner" phase. New CFO (Jen DeRico) debuts. Five proof-points of momentum, AI moves to center stage ("customers don't want AI as another standalone system... they want AI embedded directly into the systems of record they already trust"). Garrett Motion competitive-displacement win (Windchill+ and Codebeamer+) is the marquee. Deferred ARR is now the headline — "most will ramp in fiscal 2027 and fiscal 2028."
- **FQ2'26 (May-2026):** Divestiture **closed (Mar-13-2026)**. Now a "more focused company." The entire prepared remark is an AI thesis: systems-of-record evolving to "systems of action," embedded agents that "can access product data that no general-purpose AI model can reach," nearly doubling AI releases in 2026. Capital return is aggressive — $1.225-1.325B of buybacks in FY26, new $2B authorization through FY28.

**Drivers introduced:** NVIDIA/physical AI (FQ3'25), Intelligent Product Lifecycle + deferred ARR (FQ4'25), embedded AI agents + "systems of action" monetization (FQ2'26). **Drivers quietly dropped:** the standalone IoT/ThingWorx growth story (sold), and the original "high end of 10%" ARR ambition (reset down to a 7.5-9.5% normalized band).

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Trajectory: Declining-then-Rising (a V).**

- **FQ2'25 — most defensive:** "we recognize growing uncertainty related to global trade dynamics"; "we have already lost 30 days"; customers "in paralysis mode." Explicitly cut and widened guidance. Defensive/macro language dominant.
- **FQ3'25 — inflecting:** "past the point of maximum disruption"; "green shoots" (analyst Jeffries' word, management embraced). Still hedged ("too early to call a trend").
- **FQ4'25 — confident:** "The go-to-market momentum we described last quarter didn't fade, it accelerated"; "record deferred ARR."
- **FQ1'26 — explicitly bullish:** "Every transformation has an important turning-the-corner phase... This is where PTC sits today"; "ramping reps more than double productivity year over year."
- **FQ2'26 — offensive:** entire script is an AI-leadership manifesto ("systems of record... evolving to systems of action"; "unfair advantage"; "best agents... bar none"). FCF *beat* the range.

Shift from offensive→defensive (FQ2'25) and back to offensive (FQ1-2'26) is clean and tracks the tariff cycle. Guidance specificity *increased* over time (introduced net-new-ARR dollar ranges, a $950M normalized FCF base for FY27 modeling). This is a confidence-building, not confidence-eroding, arc.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Reliability: HIGH (4/5).**

- The financials show **beat 7/7 quarters (100%)** on EPS — a perfect short-window record.
- FQ2'25 they widened ARR to 7-9% on the tariff shock; FY25 delivered 8.5% CC ARR — **landed in the upper half of the lowered band**, and *raised* the FCF low-end the same quarter they cut ARR (a credibility-positive: under-promise cash, protect FCF).
- FQ4'25 set FY26 at 7.5-9.5% ex-divestiture; FQ2'26 reaffirmed it and ran at 8.5% (high end of Q2 guide) with FCF *above* range. **Consistent delivery.**
- **One credibility watch-item:** deferred ARR. Analysts (Tilton, FQ1'26 and FQ2'26) explicitly invoked prior "deferred ARR that... didn't pan out." Management's answer — tighter sales/customer-success linkage means "low" risk of slippage — is *assertion, not evidence yet.* The FY26 shape is heavily back-loaded ("more significant step-up in Q4"), which is the highest-risk way to guide.

No sandbagging pattern beyond prudent FCF conservatism; no evidence of a guide-down they buried.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

1. **Deferred ARR realization (the dominant recurring worry).** Tilton (Wolfe) pressed it FQ1'26 ("PTSD... that kinda didn't pan out") and again FQ2'26 ("instill confidence this deferred ARR balance will come through"). Management answers with process-discipline narrative, not hard conversion proof. **This is the single most-deflected topic and the one most likely to turn into bad news if Q4'26 net-new-ARR step-up disappoints.**
2. **Path back to double-digit growth (Radke, Citi, FQ4'25).** Management refused to commit to a durable growth rate — "at that point we'll be able to come back and say here's the durable growth rate." Non-answer; the durable rate is structurally ~8-9%, below the old 10%+ aspiration.
3. **AI monetization specifics.** Heavy on vision ("agent-driven value capture over time," "clear monetization pathways") but **no dollars, no pricing, no attach metrics** — "We'll detail these as they take shape." This is a promise, not a P&L line.
4. **Auto vertical depth (Vleeschhouwer).** Repeatedly probed whether ALM-led auto wins broaden to Windchill/CAD; management bullish but admits "CodeBeamer is the tip of the spear" — i.e. still narrow.

🟢 **FCF margin 31.3%, FCF 3-yr CAGR +27.9%, $850-950M normalized FY26 FCF** — cash conversion is elite and growing.

🟢 **Competitive displacement wins** (Garrett Motion, largest-ever Codebeamer and Onshape deals) — share gains are real, not just renewals.

🔴 **Insider signal: 0 open-market buys vs 16 sells (66,970 sh)**; analyst-target velocity -7%; smart-money score 32/100 — no smart-money conviction at this price.
🔴 **Margins 1.78σ above historical peak** — limited room for further margin-led EPS upside; future growth must come from ARR, which is decelerating.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Metric migration:** management steadily shifted the goalposts from "ARR growth %" → "deferred ARR under contract" → "net new ARR dollars + FY27 $950M FCF base." Each shift moves the scorecard toward a metric that is *harder to falsify in real time* and pushes the payoff into FY27/FY28. Bullishly: it's how SaaS ramp-deals genuinely work. Bearishly: it's a way to defer accountability.
- **CFO change** (Talvitie → DeRico, FQ1'26) at the exact moment the deferred-ARR/"trust us on FY27-28" narrative takes over — new CFO inherits and ratifies the back-loaded story.
- **AI framing escalation:** from "feature in each product" (FQ2'25) → "physical AI / NVIDIA connective tissue" (FQ3'25) → "systems of record become systems of action... agents bar none" (FQ2'26). The rhetorical intensity is rising *faster than disclosed monetization*, a classic pattern where narrative front-runs revenue.
- **Competition framing:** management almost never names competitors but increasingly describes "competitive displacements" — quietly signaling share-take from Siemens/Dassault/Autodesk in PLM/ALM without saying so.
- **Customer concentration:** no single-customer concentration disclosed in the transcripts or metrics; revenue is broad across verticals (auto, aero/defense, med-tech, industrial) and geos (Americas/Europe/APAC each high-single/low-double-digit). **No customer-concentration red flag** — a structural positive for durability.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Classification: STRENGTHENING (with a back-loaded execution caveat).**

PTC is a genuinely high-quality industrial-software compounder — 83.8% expanding gross margin, 31% FCF margin, 100% EPS-beat rate, deleveraged-then-returning ~$1.3B/yr via buybacks at a price near its 52-week low. The portfolio focus (Kepware/ThingWorx sale) is strategically sound and the secular PLM/CAD-into-AI thesis is real and multi-year, not a capex air-pocket.

**The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing:** the entire upgrade from "8.5% compounder" to "re-accelerating disruptor" rests on the **deferred-ARR balance converting into a Q4'26 net-new-ARR step-up and an FY27/FY28 ramp that management has so far only asserted.** The hard numbers prove a durable ~8-9% grower with elite economics; they do NOT yet prove re-acceleration. Buy the quality and the buyback-at-the-lows; do not pay up for an AI/deferred-ARR re-acceleration that is still a promise. The macro caveat (tariff/auto-OEM paralysis) has eased but not vanished, and insiders are net sellers at this level.
