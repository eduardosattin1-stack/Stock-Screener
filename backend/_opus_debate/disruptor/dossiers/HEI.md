## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The story barely "evolves" — and that is the point. Across eight quarters of transcripts HEICO repeats a single, remarkably stable thesis: **record results, double-digit organic growth in both segments, an acquisition machine funded by internally generated cash, and a culture-as-moat ("do the right thing," "real cash, not artificial GAAP metrics").** What changes is not the thesis but its *amplitude* — the numbers attached to the same words get bigger every quarter.

- **Q2'25 (May 2025):** Sets the template. FSG +19% net sales / +14% organic; consolidated NI +27%. The frame is "core business strength + accretive acquisitions," with the Wencor combination ("the gift that keeps on giving") as the marquee proof point.
- **Q3'25 (Aug 2025):** Same arc, louder. NI +30%; FSG +18% / +13% organic; ETG record. Introduces the "36-year, fund-growth-with-operating-cash, never lever past ~2x EBITDA" framing as the *durability* argument. Gables (3rd-largest acquisition ever) added.
- **Q4'25 (Dec 2025):** NI +35%; FSG +21% / +16% organic. First call after **Larry Mendelson's death** — heavy on succession-as-culture ("that was his succession plan... it worked perfectly"). Golden Dome / $1.5T defense budget introduced as a *new, multi-year* growth vector layered onto the existing aftermarket story.
- **Q1'26 (Feb 2026):** NI +13% (optically soft — but distorted by a smaller stock-option tax benefit vs PY and a lumpy space-shipment dip); FSG +21% OI / +15% sales. The "secret sauce" monologue ("obedience to the unenforceable," properly reserving inventory, "never had a one-time unusual charge") is the most explicit moat-as-accounting-quality pitch in the set.
- **Q2'26 (May 2026):** The crescendo. **NI +49%, OI +41%, sales +25%; ETG +34% sales / +17% organic; FSG +21% sales / +19% organic.** Defense backlog now explicitly inflecting ("we are now experiencing this in our defense sales, orders and backlog... multiyear tail"). Artemis II / NASA program wins surfaced as the space proof point.

**Growth drivers introduced and kept:** PMA aftermarket pricing arbitrage (the structural disruptor), Wencor cross-sell, serial M&A, defense (Golden Dome / replenishment), space (new-space + legacy). **Quietly dropped:** essentially nothing — this management does not retire drivers; it stacks them. The only "contraction" is honest and minor (space organic down high-single-digits in Q1'26, explicitly flagged as a *shipment-timing* artifact, not order weakness).

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Reliability: HIGH.** HEICO famously gives **no formal numeric guidance** — so credibility is judged on (a) directional promises kept and (b) the beat cadence. Both are excellent:

- **Beat 7/7 quarters (100%)** per the financials block. Q2'26 EPS YoY +48% (latest-Q EPS) corroborates the live FMP TTM diluted EPS of $5.60 against the scan's $4.90 fiscal-year figure — earnings are *accelerating*, not decelerating.
- Every "accretive within the first year" acquisition promise (Rosen, Gables, Exxelia framing, Axillon, Sherwood, Ethos) is restated and tracked; none has been walked back.
- Carlos's *forward* color is conservative and then beaten: ETG margin guided "22–24%," then upgraded to "24–26%" in Q2'26 ("my thinking has come up a little"). That is the textbook **sandbag-then-beat** pattern.
- The one place to keep them honest: the ~$15–20M Q2'26 defense pull-forward means a sliver of Q3'26 was borrowed — but they disclosed it unprompted.

No missed promises detected across the eight quarters. Score: **High.**

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **Margin ceiling / centralization** (Kristine Liwag, Q3'25): repeatedly probed on whether centralizing the decentralized supply chain would unlock margin. Management *declines* the easy margin grab ("not worth disrupting our happy customer base... 3–5 years out at best"). Non-answer, but a *principled* one — and it foreshadows that the gross-margin story will stay mix-driven, not cost-out-driven.
- **PMA pricing headroom** (Liwag, Q3'25): pushed on raising prices on deep-discount mature parts. Eric explicitly refuses ("not the HEICO way... do not model that") — leaves visible margin on the table, which is both a moat-preserving choice and a near-term margin ceiling analysts keep circling.
- **Space lumpiness** (Siegmann, Q1'26): pressed on the high-single-digit space organic decline; management frames it as shipment timing, "don't read too much into it." This is the one claim that could later turn into bad news if shipments don't recover — worth a follow.
- **Dual-class collapse / Class A discount** (Khanna, Q4'25): perennial governance question; management says "status quo," calls Class A "a screaming value." No resolution — a latent governance/valuation overhang, not a near-term catalyst.
- **Boeing MAX / OE destocking** (Ciarmoli, Q3'25): probed; management concedes "a little bit" of OE destocking but pivots to aftermarket strength. Adequately addressed.

**What analysts are NOT pressing that they should:** the *contracting gross margin* and the *10% blended ROIC* relative to the ~60x multiple. The Q&A is overwhelmingly demand- and program-focused; valuation discipline is conspicuously absent from the call.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

**🟢 Green flags**
- 🟢 OCF consistently ~100–130% of net income; TTM FCF $926M — earnings are cash-backed (Q3'25, Q2'26).
- 🟢 De-levers organically (2.06x → 1.74x) *while* deploying $630M+/yr on M&A (Q3'25–Q2'26).
- 🟢 Voluntary, quantified disclosure of the $15–20M defense pull-forward (Q2'26) — the opposite of earnings management.
- 🟢 Open-market insider conviction: **21 buys vs 13 sells**, and directors specifically bought the *Class A* "screaming value" (Q4'25) — real signal, not grant-inflated.
- 🟢 Defense backlog explicitly inflecting with a "multiyear tail" (Q2'26), corroborated by the FY26 defense bill / Golden Dome funding.

**🔴 Red flags**
- 🔴 **Gross margin contracting (39.8%, flagged)** even as operating margin expands — acquisition mix and amortization are diluting the high-GM PMA core. The single most important number to track.
- 🔴 **Valuation, not the business:** P/FCF 54.2x, ~60x forward P/E, MoS −6%, every intrinsic model (DCF/EPV/Owner-Earnings/IV15) at −97% to −100% MoS. The forensic quality is high; the *price* prices in perfection.
- 🔴 **Blended ROIC 10%** on a goodwill-heavy balance sheet — the compounding is real but acquisition premiums dilute returns; Piotroski 5/9.
- 🔴 **Key-man / founder transition:** Larry Mendelson died (Q4'25). Culture is deep and the sons are seasoned, but the founder-as-moat narrative now rests on succession holding.
- 🔴 Dual-class structure entrenches the family; Class A persistently trades at a discount with no resolution path.

**Classification: STRENGTHENING.** Organic growth is accelerating (Q2'26 the strongest quarter in the set), cash conversion is pristine, leverage is falling, the M&A flywheel is intact, and two secular tailwinds (commercial aftermarket up-cycle + defense replenishment/Golden Dome) are independently verified as current and multi-year. This is a genuine moat-compounding disruptor, not a narrative.

The business deserves a premium; the question is whether ~60x forward earnings / 54x FCF / −6% margin of safety leaves any room for error against a **contracting gross margin** and a **10% blended ROIC**. There is no catalyst here to arbitrage — only durability to underwrite. Size for a fairly-to-richly-priced compounder where the entry multiple, not the thesis, is the risk.
