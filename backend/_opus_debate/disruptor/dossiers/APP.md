# AppLovin Corporation (APP) — Forensic Dossier

*Source: six consecutive earnings calls, 2025-05-07 (Q1'25) through 2026-05-06 (Q1'26), cross-referenced against screener financials. Stock-page audience.*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The story compresses and sharpens across these six quarters from a two-engine company into a single-engine, AI-advertising pure-play with an optionality wrapper.

- **Q1'25 (2025-05-07):** The pivotal structural event — Adam Foroughi announces the *definitive agreement to sell the games business in its entirety* to Tripledot. The narrative pivots from "ad-tech + game studio" to "we are an advertising platform." Three 2025 priorities are set: (1) keep improving the ML models, (2) build out e-commerce/web advertising, (3) automated ad creation. Web advertising is framed as "less than 0.1% of the potential market." The TikTok bid is floated as a "long shot."
- **Q2'25 (2025-08-06):** Apps business sale *closes*; results restated to advertising-only with games in discontinued ops. The self-serve **AXON ads manager** is introduced as "the foundation for our next decade of growth," with a referral-basis open on **October 1, 2025** and a global public launch flagged for **1H 2026**. Management asserts confidence in "20% to 30% YoY growth driven by just gaming," with web as upside on top.
- **Q3'25 (2025-11-05):** **S&P 500 inclusion** — a credibility milestone management explicitly leans on ("we now carry the expectations of a much broader set of investors"). Oct 1 self-serve launch executed "without any significant hiccups." First quantified self-serve datapoint: spend from self-serve advertisers "grow around roughly 50% week-over-week" (caveated as "too soon to be significant").
- **Q4'25 (2026-02-11):** Defensive register — Foroughi opens by directly addressing the *short-seller / AI-disruption narrative* ("a real disconnect between market sentiment and the reality of our business"). Introduces the **AI-as-tailwind** counter-thesis: cheaper game creation = content abundance = discovery scarcity = more value for AppLovin's matching engine. Rule-of-40 score quoted at **150**.
- **Q1'26 (2026-05-06):** Tone resets to pure offense — "No preamble on stock price, no addressing short sellers." Renames e-commerce the **"consumer vertical,"** discloses it is "growing even faster than gaming," exited the quarter with **March spend ~25% above January** and **April a record month** above any Q4 peak. Hard date set: **public platform open in June 2026** after "14 years as a closed platform." New optionality stacked on: lead-gen (cost-per-lead) model, connected-TV supply, agent-native dashboard, AI ad-creative generation.

**Drivers introduced:** self-serve AXON, consumer vertical, AI-creative tools, agent-compatible infra, lead-gen, CTV supply. **Drivers quietly dropped:** the TikTok bid (mentioned Q1'25, never revisited — correctly buried as the long shot it was). The arc is *expansion*, not pivot — the core gaming engine is reaffirmed every quarter while the TAM-extension layer is added on top.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Trajectory: Rising, with a defensive dip in Q4'25 that fully resolved by Q1'26.**

- Q1'25 — offensive but distracted: "After seeing the stock price rise roughly 50 times in two years, we faced short seller scrutiny, which we've addressed comprehensively and won't revisit." Spends real airtime on TikTok and tariffs.
- Q2'25 — confident, product-forward: "the foundation for our next decade of growth."
- Q3'25 — proud, milestone-driven: S&P 500 inclusion, "no major bugs."
- Q4'25 — **most defensive quarter**: opens with "the recent volatility warrants addressing" and "a real disconnect between market sentiment and the reality of our business." This is the only call structured *around* the bear case rather than the business.
- Q1'26 — **most offensive quarter**: "No preamble on stock price, no addressing short sellers... the future has never looked better." Hosts a "Gaming CEO Summit" and reports "energy this year was unlike anything I have seen."

Guidance became *more* specific over time (precise revenue and adj-EBITDA ranges plus sequential-growth percentages each quarter). No shift from offensive to defensive in substance — the Q4'25 defensiveness was a *response to the stock*, not a deterioration in the *business* language, which stayed growth/investment-oriented throughout.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Reliability: HIGH.**

Every guide in this window was beaten. Q2'25 guided Q3 advertising rev $1.320–1.340B → delivered $1.405B. Q3'25 guided Q4 $1.570–1.600B → delivered $1.66B. Q4'25 guided Q1'26 $1.745–1.775B → delivered $1.84B. This matches the screener's **beat 7/7 qtrs (100%)**. The pattern is *systematic sandbagging*: Stumpf explicitly described the guidance philosophy (Q2'25) as guiding "towards the component of growth... that we feel very confident in" — i.e., the ~3–5%/quarter reinforcement-learning baseline — and treating model step-changes and new cohorts as upside. This is a deliberately beatable bar, and they clear it every time. Reliability is as high as it gets for a hyper-growth name.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **Attribution / incrementality (Q1'25, Rob Sanderson):** "you're obviously serving a lot of ads on inventory you'd be buying otherwise" — the recurring worry that AppLovin's self-attribution overstates the value it delivers. Management's answer (the short measurement window "forces us to work at a higher level") is a *deflection dressed as a feature*; this is the single most durable bear pressure point and remains unresolved by hard third-party data.
- **Inventory ceiling (Q3'25, Nat Schindler):** "when do you run out of inventory on your core gamer market?" Foroughi's answer — "we don't know is the simple answer" — is candid but underscores that the growth math depends on *conversion-rate* lifts, not impression growth.
- **Self-serve quantification (Q3'25 James Heaney, Q1'26 Martin Yang):** analysts repeatedly press for hard self-serve numbers; management offers only "50% week-over-week" (Q3) and qualitative March/April color (Q1'26). The *refusal to size the cohort* is a persistent non-answer.
- **"Are you doing too much?" (Q1'26, Jonathan Kees):** lead-gen + CTV + agent-dashboard + consumer verticals all at once. Management waves it off ("we've never reached the point of too much") — execution-breadth risk that analysts are right to flag.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🔴 **Insider selling, zero buys:** 0 open-market buys vs 99 sells (124,566 sh). No insider has put a dollar of new money in at these levels.
🔴 **Self-attribution / incrementality opacity:** the value AppLovin claims to deliver is measured largely by its own pixel on the web side; analysts have pressed this for a year without a satisfying third-party answer.
🔴 **Refusal to quantify the new engine:** the consumer-vertical and self-serve cohorts — the entire bull-thesis extension — are described only qualitatively. "Trust us, it's a record month."
🔴 **Repeated short-seller targeting:** management spent the Q1'25 and Q4'25 calls partly rebutting bear/fraud allegations (CapitalWatch later *retracted* one report — net green, but the recurrence is a flag).
🟢 **GM expansion on hyper-growth:** adj-EBITDA margin 68%→85% while revenue compounds 59–70% — textbook pricing power, the opposite of commoditization.
🟢 **Clean structural simplification:** the games-business divestiture (announced Q1'25, closed Q2'25) removed a lower-margin, capital-intensive segment and left a near-pure software/ad engine.
🟢 **Self-funded buyback shrinking the count:** 346M→336M diluted shares, $2.58B FY25 repurchase entirely from FCF.
🟢 **7/7 guidance beats** with a transparently conservative methodology.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Terminology evolution:** "e-commerce" (2025) → "web advertising" (mid-2025) → **"consumer vertical"** (Q1'26). Each rename *widens* the claimed TAM — from online shops to any transactional website to "any business of any size." Watch this: the broadening label outpaces the disclosed traction.
- **Competition framing got more sophisticated, then more confident:** Q1'25 "we embrace it" (generic) → Q4'25 the detailed MAX-auction "as bid density goes up, the pie expands... we get to charge the winning bidder 5%" argument (a genuine, non-obvious moat explanation) → Q1'26 silence on competitors entirely (confidence signal).
- **The AI-disruption inversion (Q4'25):** management pre-empted the "AI commoditizes game-making, so AppLovin loses" bear thesis by arguing the opposite — content abundance makes *discovery* (AppLovin's product) the scarce, valuable resource. This is the most important new strategic argument in the window.
- **Capital-allocation discipline is consistent:** organic growth first, then buybacks; no dividend, no M&A spree (TikTok bid quietly abandoned). Stumpf reaffirms ~75% FCF/EBITDA conversion for 2026.

## 8. CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION

No single top-customer revenue share is disclosed in the transcripts or the financials block. Management instead emphasizes the *opposite* property: extreme spend density across a *small* advertiser count — Q1'25 cited "$11 billion plus of ad spend" from advertisers "in the low thousands," and Q3'25/Q4'25 reiterated "low thousands" of advertisers against 1B+ daily users. This implies meaningful **revenue concentration in a small set of large mobile-gaming advertisers** (the historical "$10B+ annually" spender base Foroughi referenced in Q1'25), even though no named-customer percentage is given. The entire self-serve/consumer push is explicitly a *de-concentration* strategy. **Concentration is real but undisclosed and trending down by design.**

## CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Classification: STRENGTHENING.**

This is a structurally cleaner, faster-growing, higher-margin business at the end of the window than the start: it shed the games segment, compounded revenue at 59–70%, expanded EBITDA margin to 85%, generated $4.4B TTM FCF, and self-funded a share-count reduction — all while opening a genuine second growth engine (consumer vertical + June-2026 public AXON launch). Guidance is reliably sandbagged and beaten 7/7.

**The single thing the director must weigh before sizing:** this is a *valuation and verifiability* problem, not a business-quality problem. The screener shows price $496.77 vs Buffett fair value $334 (−33% MoS) and a DCF at $121.5; every per-method MoS reads −100%. The new growth engine that justifies the multiple (the consumer vertical) is the *one thing management refuses to quantify*, and the only true conviction signal from insiders is **99 sells / 0 buys**. You are paying a premium price for a verified-great gaming engine plus an *unverified* consumer-vertical option, into a stock that has already round-tripped from ~$614 (June 1) to ~$497 (June 12). Size for the volatility, not the narrative.
