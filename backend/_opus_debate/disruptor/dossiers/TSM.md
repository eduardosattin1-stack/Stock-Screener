# TSM — Forensic Dossier (Interrogator)
**Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. | Technology / Semiconductor Foundry | signal_type: disruptor**
Transcript window: Q1 2025 → Q1 2026 (six consecutive quarterly calls). Financials: screener scan + FMP TTM/latest-Q overlay (as of 2026-03-31).

---

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

Across six quarters the core story did not pivot — it **compounded**. What changed is the *confidence interval* around it, which narrowed dramatically.

- **Q1 2025 (2025-04-17):** Story opens defensively. A Lunar New Year earthquake scrapped wafers; smartphone seasonality dragged Q1 revenue down 5.1% in USD. Management leans on resilience and reiterates "close to mid-20s percent" full-year USD growth. Tariff uncertainty is the dominant macro overhang ("we have not seen any change in our customer behavior so far"). The bullish anchor is already in place: C.C. Wei commits to **"double our AI revenue this year."** N2 framing introduced — tape-outs "much higher than the 3-nanometer and 5-nanometer in the same period of time."
- **Q2 2025 (2025-07-17):** Revenue inflects up 17.8% QoQ to $30.1B. The narrative shifts from "weathering the quake" to "AI/HPC is the engine" — HPC hits 60% of revenue. FX (NT appreciation) becomes the new headwind to margin, replacing the earthquake. Management raises the AI conviction language: "explosive growth in token volume," sovereign AI introduced as a *new* demand vector.
- **Q3 2025 (2025-10-16):** Full-year USD growth guide raised twice over the year — from "mid-20s" → "close to **mid-30s percent**." Crucial new disclosure: **"we directly received very strong signals from our customers' customers, requesting the capacity"** — i.e., demand visibility now reaches two tiers down the chain. Foundry 2.0 (front-end + advanced packaging) formalized; advanced packaging "approaching close to 10%" of revenue.
- **Q4 2025 / FY recap (2026-01-15):** The year lands at **+35.9% USD revenue, GM 59.9%, EPS +46.4%.** CapEx guide for 2026 stepped up hard to **$52–56B** (from $40.9B actual 2025). New multi-year anchor introduced: **AI accelerator revenue mid-to-high-50s % CAGR, 2024→2029.** Dividend floor raised to "at least TWD 23/share in 2026" (from TWD 18 in 2025).
- **Q1 2026 (2026-04-16):** GM explodes to **66.2%** (guide was 63–65%), op margin 58.1%. CapEx guide nudged to "**high end**" of $52–56B. Two *new* risk vectors surface honestly: Middle East situation (chemical/gas prices, LNG/energy) and rising component prices pressuring consumer-end demand. Agentic AI introduced as the next step-up in token consumption. AI CAGR reaffirmed at "higher 50s."

**Drivers introduced and kept:** AI/HPC, sovereign AI, advanced packaging (CoWoS/SoIC), N2 ramp, agentic AI. **Drivers quietly de-emphasized:** smartphone (now spoken of only as "mild recovery"), the buyback debate (management flatly refused a buyback in Q1 2025 and the topic disappeared). Nothing material was promised and dropped — a rare clean arc.

**Where the story is richer than the numbers:** the AI mid-to-high-50s CAGR to 2029 is a *forward* claim the financials cannot yet validate — it rests on hyperscaler capex holding (verified externally, see §6). **Where the numbers are richer than the story:** margin. Management keeps anchoring "53%+" while *printing 66%.* This is the single strongest credibility signal in the file — they under-promise on the one metric (GM) that is the moat lie-detector, then beat it every quarter.

**The GROSS-MARGIN TRAJECTORY (the moat test):** FY2023 ~54.4% → FY2024 ~56.1% → FY2025 **59.9%** → Q1'26 **66.2%**. *Expanding gross margin on +36% revenue growth.* This is the textbook signature of pricing power, not commoditization — the opposite of what a topping/competed business shows.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY — **RISING**

- **Q1'25 (guarded):** "We might get a better picture in the next few months… manage our business prudently." Defensive, earthquake/tariff framed.
- **Q2'25 (warming):** "the demand for semiconductors is very fundamental… Recent developments are also positive to AI's long-term demand outlook."
- **Q3'25 (offensive):** "our conviction in the AI megatrend is **strengthening**"; "we directly received very strong signals from our **customers' customers**."
- **Q4'25 (peak offensive):** Five-year AI CAGR guide issued — management almost never gives 5-yr numbers. "Our headache right now, if I can call it a headache, is a demand and supply gap."
- **Q1'26 (confident but prudent):** Reaffirms higher-50s CAGR; layers in Middle East/component-cost caution *honestly* rather than hiding it. "AI-related demand continues to be **extremely robust**… agentic AI… another step-up in tokens."

The arc moved from **defensive (earthquake/FX/tariff) → offensive (megatrend, multi-year CAGR, demand>supply).** Guidance became *more* specific over time (single-quarter → full-year → 5-year CAGR), which is the inverse of a deteriorating story.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY — **HIGH (5/5)**

Track record within the window:

- Q4'25 GM guided 59–61%; **actual 62.3%** (beat high end by 130bps).
- Q1'26 GM guided 63–65%; **actual 66.2%** (beat high end by 120bps).
- Full-year USD revenue guide *raised twice* (mid-20s → mid-30s) and **delivered +35.9%.**
- Revenue beat the guidance range in essentially every quarter (Q2'25 "exceeded," Q3'25 "slightly ahead," Q4'25 "slightly ahead," Q1'26 "slightly ahead").

**Clear sandbagging pattern on margin** — the 53% "long-term floor" is repeated as a ritual while actuals run ~10pts higher. This is deliberate expectation management by a management team that prefers to beat-and-raise. **Reliability: HIGH.**

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **Buyback / shareholder return (Brett Simpson, Q1'25):** Management *flatly refused* to adopt buybacks despite a fortress balance sheet — "the sustainable and steadily increasing dividend is a better way." A genuine governance friction point: TSM trades at a discount to US mega-cap peers partly *because* it returns cash inefficiently (huge net cash idle). This is the one place management is least responsive to the market.
- **Capital intensity (Mehdi Hosseini, Q2'25):** Pushed on whether N2 forces capital intensity back to 40%+. Management deflected to "capital intensity is less meaningful than before" — a soft non-answer; the 2026 CapEx jump to $52–56B partially validates the analyst's worry.
- **GPU vs ASIC mix (Krish Sankar, Q3'25):** Analysts probing whether in-house ASICs (which could route around NVDA) change TSM's economics. Management: "no differentiation in front of TSMC. We support all kinds." — strategically the correct answer; TSM wins regardless of who wins the accelerator war.
- **Samsung winning the LPU (Charles Shi, Q1'26):** A pointed needle — a customer's LPU business sits at Samsung Foundry. Management acknowledged it and committed to "win back" next-gen. The *only* competitive concession in six quarters, and a minor one.
- **Token-vs-revenue gap (Bruce Lu, Q4'25 & Q1'26):** Analysts note token consumption growing ~15%/quarter vs AI revenue ~15%/year — implying either a supply bottleneck (bullish for pricing) or eventual digestion risk. Management: "still the bottleneck is TSMC's wafer supply." Frames it as scarcity, not demand risk.

No topic management deflected later turned into bad news *within the window.* The recurring deflection (capital intensity) is a margin-of-future-returns question, not a thesis-breaker.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Terminology escalation:** "robust" (Q1'25) → "very fundamental" (Q2'25) → "strengthening conviction" (Q3'25) → "extremely robust" (Q1'26). The adjective inflation is real and *earned* by the prints.
- **New demand vectors layered in, never retracted:** sovereign AI (Q2'25) → enterprise AI (Q3'25) → agentic AI (Q1'26). Each call adds a *structural* (not cyclical) demand source — a deliberate widening of the TAM narrative that the margin trajectory backs up.
- **"Foundry 2.0" reframing (Q3'25):** Management is repositioning TSM from a pure wafer foundry to a *system-level* enabler (front-end + advanced packaging). This is moat-deepening — CoWoS/SoIC are the new lock-in layer NVDA/AMD/AAPL cannot source elsewhere at scale.
- **CFO voice consistency:** Wendell Huang (transcribed "Jen-Chau Huang"/"Jeff Su" artifacts aside) is methodical and quantitative across all six calls — the "6 factors of profitability" framework is repeated verbatim, a sign of a stable, disciplined finance function. No CFO/CEO turnover noise.
- **CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION (explicit, per mandate):** The transcripts and external reporting make clear TSM's leading-edge book is *highly concentrated*. **Apple is reported to hold ~50%+ of 2026–2027 N2 allocation**, and the top handful of customers (Apple, NVIDIA, AMD, Broadcom, Qualcomm) account for a large majority of leading-edge wafer revenue — single-digit number of names drive the bulk of the <5nm book. This is a double-edged signal: it concentrates demand risk on a few hyperscaler-adjacent balance sheets, but those exact customers (per §6 web-verify) are the ones *raising* 2026 capex 77% to ~$725B. Concentration on the **strongest-funded buyers on earth** is the benign form of this risk — but it is real and must be sized.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director) — **STRENGTHENING**

TSM's trajectory across these six quarters is unambiguously **STRENGTHENING**: accelerating revenue (+36% USD), *expanding* gross margin into the ramp (54%→66% — the moat lie-detector flashing green), beat-and-raise guidance with a sandbagged margin floor, demand visibility two tiers deep, a fortress net-cash balance sheet funding a CapEx super-cycle without leverage, and a credible 5-year AI CAGR.

**The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing:** this is *not* a mispricing or a hidden-asset SoP — TSM is a transparent, well-followed, fairly-to-richly priced compounder whose entire thesis is **leveraged to the durability of hyperscaler AI capex.** The load-bearing question is binary and macro, not company-specific: *if the ~$725B 2026 hyperscaler capex cycle proves to be a digestion-prone air-pocket rather than a multi-year secular build, TSM's 66% gross margin and 30%+ growth normalize hard* — there is no segment-level hidden value to cushion it (86% of revenue is one segment: Wafer). The company is the highest-quality, lowest-execution-risk way to own the AI-infrastructure theme, but it *is* the theme — size it as a high-conviction theme bet whose chief risk is the customer-concentrated capex cycle topping, not as an idiosyncratic value unlock.
