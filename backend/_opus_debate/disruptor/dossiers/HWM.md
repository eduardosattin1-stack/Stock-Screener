# Howmet Aerospace (HWM) — Forensic Dossier

*Interrogator pass on the bundled FY2025 earnings-call sequence (Q1 2025 → Q4/FY2025), cross-referenced against the screener's hard financial trajectory.*

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The Howmet story over these four calls is the rare case where the narrative *strengthened monotonically* and management's confidence problem was that growth kept outrunning their own framing. The arc:

- **Q1 2025 (May)** opened defensively colored — not on the business, but on the macro overlay. John Plant spent the bulk of the outlook section walking analysts through *tariff drag* ("net $15 million in 2025," "imports from Europe… and China") and *rare-earth exposure* (yttrium, gadolinium, erbium — "our supplier has approximately 10 years of inventory"). The underlying business was already a "record," revenue +6%, EBITDA margin 28.8%, but the framing leaned cautious.
- **Q2 2025 (July)** the macro caveats receded and the spares engine moved to center stage: revenue growth *accelerated* to +9%, spares +40% YoY and 20% of total revenue ("as a compare, total spares in 2019 was 11%"). The competitive-tailwind subplot was introduced — Precision Castparts' (PCC) Jenkintown facility accident converted into Howmet share gains ("we've moved much closer to $40 million" of redirected fastener orders).
- **Q3 2025 (Oct)** growth *accelerated again* to +14%, and management reframed the industrial line: oil & gas + IGT were merged into a single **"gas turbines"** market because "many turbines now have increasing end use for data centers." This is the moment the data-center power theme was explicitly grafted onto the thesis. Commercial-aero part sales +38%.
- **Q4/FY2025 (Feb 2026)** closed at +15% Q4 / +11% full year, with a CFO handoff (Ken Giacobbe → Patrick Winterlich). Full-year spares +33% to $1.7B (21% of revenue), gas turbines +25%, net leverage to a record-low 1.0x.

**Drivers introduced and kept:** engine spares (legacy + next-gen), data-center gas turbines, PCC share-shift, F-35 defense. **Quietly dropped:** the tariff/rare-earth anxiety of Q1 — by Q4 it had become a footnote, validating management's "drag, not damage" call. Nothing material was dropped that should have been kept; the contraction was in the *caveats*, not the thesis.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Rising — unambiguously.**
- **Q1:** Cautious overlay — "tariffs… increased the uncertainty and reduced confidence in air travel"; "a little bit more caution because of that Commercial Truck business."
- **Q2:** Offensive — "we are actually really pleased to deploy the capital and have more opportunities than we're actually capacitizing for"; on heavy forging presses, "those are pretty important assets… absolutely critical."
- **Q3:** Expansive — "we don't deploy capital just because it's fun… backed by clear-eye thinking about customer utilization, customer commitment and economic return"; "the greatest pleasure that I'm going to have next year is being able to deploy that amount of capital or more."
- **Q4:** Confident handoff with a raised dividend and record FCF.

The shift is the *opposite* of the usual offensive→defensive decay: HWM moved from defensive (tariffs/macro) to offensive (capacity, content, price). Plant repeatedly *declines to over-claim* on 2026 ("I'll wait till February") — a tell of a manager who under-promises.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY — **HIGH**

The scan shows **beat 6/7 quarters (86%)**. Every quarter in the sequence "exceeded the high end of guidance" (their words, corroborated by the EPS step-up). The FY26 guide issued at the Q4 call was explicitly conservative ("baseline scenario") and was beaten + raised within one quarter (see §5 web-verify). This is a *sandbagging* pattern, not a stretch-and-miss pattern. Reliability: **HIGH (5/5).** The only soft spot is that guidance is given on adjusted (ex-special-items) figures — but the 93% FCF/NI conversion validates that adjustments aren't masking cash leakage.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **Incremental margins / over-earning on spares** (Poponak, Ciarmoli, Q3): the recurring worry is that the 60%+ incrementals and 33% spares growth are a cyclical peak. Plant's answer — "no case of over-earning in the immediacy… spares increase every year through the end of the decade" — is a confident *assertion* analysts have not been able to falsify, and the Q1'26 print (+36% spares) supports him so far.
- **Margin step-down in the back-half guide** (Kahyaoglu, Q1): analysts caught a guided 100bps margin step-down vs only 15bps of tariff; Plant attributed it to Commercial Truck caution and labor ingestion. The truck weakness *did* materialize (wheels volume −13% FY) but margins did **not** step down — they expanded, so the caution was conservative.
- **Heavy-forging strategic asset** (Mikus, Q2): analyst flagged HWM owns 2 of 4 >35,000-ton presses in the US ("only one… maybe in Russia"). Plant admitted *no* DoD conversations — a latent, un-monetized moat/optionality the market may not price.
- **Supply-chain canaries** (Liwag, Q2): narrow-body engine build-out (LEAP/GTF) is the bottleneck Plant watches "closer to home." This is the single load-bearing external dependency.

No deflections turned into bad news across the window. The pressure points were all *upside* surprises relative to the cautious answers.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🟢 **Margin expansion on accelerating revenue** — the moat lie-detector passes cleanly (GM 30.7% expanding; EBITDA 28.8→30.1% exit). Pricing power, not commoditization.
🟢 **PCC share-shift** — a competitor's accident ($25M→$40M+ of redirected fastener orders) is converting structural share to HWM.
🟢 **Data-center gas-turbine optionality** — a new, secular, non-aero demand leg explicitly added in Q3, growing 25–32%.
🟢 **Balance-sheet fortress** — net leverage 1.0x, 3 notches into IG, all unsecured fixed-rate debt, Altman-Z 10.0.
🟢 **Sandbag guidance** — beat-and-raise cadence with HIGH reliability.
🔴 **Valuation, not the business** — P/FCF 75x, scan fair value $164.54 vs price $264.67 (MoS −38%); every intrinsic model (DCF/EPV/Graham/OwnerErn/IV15) reads −100% MoS. The business is excellent; the *price* embeds the excellence.
🔴 **Insider conviction (open-market): 0 buys vs 12 sells (1.197M shares).** Management is a net *seller* into strength — consistent with a fully-valued stock.
🔴 **Buybacks at rising prices** ($124→$215 avg) — accretive to EPS but management is repurchasing into a 75x multiple, reducing the margin of safety of the capital-return program.
🔴 **CapEx ramp / labor ingestion** — ~1,500 net new employees FY25, record $453M CapEx; near-term margin drag is real and the incremental-ROIC of the new turbine/IGT capacity is asserted, not yet demonstrated.
🟡 **Commercial Truck (Forged Wheels) secular drag** — volumes −13% FY; offset by premium mix and aluminum pass-through, but a structurally weak leg.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **The "gas turbines" rename (Q3)** is the most important terminology shift: management deliberately merged oil&gas + IGT because "many turbines now have increasing end use for data centers." This is HWM quietly repositioning a sleepy industrial line as an AI-power-demand play — and the appendix history (Page 19) shows it's already a ~$1B, +25–32% business they expect to double.
- **CFO transition (Q4):** Ken Giacobbe → Patrick Winterlich. Winterlich's first call was on-message and conservative ("baseline scenario") — no narrative break, no kitchen-sinking. Clean handoff, John Plant remains the dominant voice and capital-allocation author.
- **Spares mix is the silent re-rating engine:** spares went 11% (2019) → 17% (2024) → 21% (2025) of revenue. Higher-margin, recurring, aftermarket-like — this is *quality-of-earnings improvement* the multiple is partly (correctly) rewarding.
- **Forging-press optionality** — Plant's unprompted admission that HWM owns scarce heavy presses "vital to the defense industry" and that he *hadn't* approached DoD signals un-monetized strategic value (F-47/F-55 next-gen fighter content).

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT — **STRENGTHENING**

Across the window Howmet shifted from defensive macro framing to offensive organic-reinvestment, expanded margins on accelerating revenue, deleveraged to a record-low 1.0x, raised the dividend 69%, and sustained a 19-quarter buyback streak — all while a competitor's misstep handed it share. This is a structurally strengthening franchise occupying a genuine aerospace chokepoint (nickel-superalloy turbine airfoils specified into GE LEAP / F135 for program-life).

**The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing:** this is a *valuation* debate, not a *business-quality* debate. The franchise is A-grade and the theme (commercial-aero ramp + spares + data-center gas turbines + defense) is durable and multi-year. But at P/FCF ~75x / ~60x P/E, with every intrinsic model at −100% MoS and insiders selling, the market has *already* priced the strengthening. The capital question is not "is this a great company" (yes) but "is there any margin of safety left, or am I underwriting flawless execution of the 2027–2028 capacity fill at a premium multiple, into a narrow-body engine ramp that is the one external dependency that could air-pocket the spares-and-OE thesis."
