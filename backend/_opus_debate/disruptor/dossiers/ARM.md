# ARM Holdings plc — Forensic Dossier (Interrogator)

*Six consecutive earnings calls reviewed: FY25 Q4 (2025-05-07), FY26 Q1 (2025-07-30), FY26 Q2 (2025-11-05), FY26 Q3 (2026-02-04), FY26 Q4 (2026-05-06). Financials cross-referenced against the screener trajectory (FY22–FY26) and the TTM/latest-quarter block (as of 2026-03-31).*

---

## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

The core story did not pivot across these calls — it **escalated in scope and then expanded its business model**. The constant thread is "AI requires compute, compute requires energy efficiency, and Arm is the only platform that spans 'milliwatts to megawatts'" (later inflated to "milliwatts to **gigawatts**" by Q3 FY26). What changed is the center of gravity and, critically, the monetization model:

- **FY25 Q4 (May 2025):** The headline was *records* — first \$1B+ quarter, first \$4B+ year, first \$2B+ royalty year. The story was diversification ("broad-based... all major markets") and Armv9/CSS ASP uplift ("smartphone royalties jumped 30% YoY vs 2% unit growth"). Data center was a proof point, not yet the lead.
- **FY26 Q1–Q2 (Jul–Nov 2025):** Data center moves to the front. "Neoverse... more than doubled YoY" (Q2). The Meta partnership is announced (Q2). CSS is the royalty-rate engine — "highest royalty rates we have seen to date." Management starts pre-warning that **R&D will outgrow revenue** because "we are accelerating investment."
- **FY26 Q3 (Feb 2026):** Reorganization around **Edge AI / Physical AI / Cloud AI** — a structural signal that data center is now strategic core. First explicit forward claim: "in a few years our data center business [will] be our largest business, larger than mobile." Agentic-AI framing introduced as the demand driver for higher CPU core counts.
- **FY26 Q4 (May 2026):** The **thesis expansion** — Arm announces it is *building its own silicon* (the Arm AGI CPU), a historic first that moves it from pure-IP licensor toward merchant chip vendor. Meta is lead co-developer. Management quantifies a brand-new revenue vector: ">\$2 billion of customer demand across FY27–FY28... on track towards \$15 billion by fiscal '31," plus the IP business "expected to double to \$10 billion." A \$100B+ data-center CPU TAM by 2030 is asserted.

**Growth drivers introduced:** custom hyperscaler silicon → CSS royalty-rate uplift → Agentic AI core-count thesis → own-silicon (AGI CPU). **Drivers quietly de-emphasized:** the "diversification across smartphone/auto/IoT" framing of FY25 narrowed sharply — by FY26 Q4, smartphone royalty is described as growing "despite end market weakness," and the entire bull narrative now leans on data center. Management also **stopped disclosing Armv9 penetration %** as of Q1 FY26 ("we're now just going to update this on an annual basis"), reducing a metric analysts had been tracking.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Trajectory: RISING (offensive throughout, accelerating).** This is the rare deck where confidence climbed every quarter.

- FY25 Q4: "could not be a better time to be working in this industry" — but tempered by tariff caution: "we do not consider it prudent to issue full year guidance."
- FY26 Q1: "growth trajectory of the company is unparalleled."
- FY26 Q2: "the future has never been brighter."
- FY26 Q3: "the fundamentals of the Arm business have never been stronger."
- FY26 Q4: "This was an amazing quarter... 2 growth vectors... structural growth... very, very strong and very sustainable."

The language is relentlessly **offensive (growth/investment)**, never defensive. The one recurring hedge — macro/tariff "lower visibility" — appeared in FY25 Q4 and FY26 Q1 and then **dropped out entirely** by mid-FY26 as AI capex overwhelmed any macro caution. Guidance specificity actually *increased*: from "no full-year guide" (FY25 Q4) to concrete multi-year dollar targets (\$15B FY31, \$10B IP) by FY26 Q4.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Reliability: HIGH (on the near-term operating model); UNPROVEN (on the new multi-year silicon targets).**

Track record on the bounded guidance is strong. Screener shows **beat 5/7 quarters (71%)**. Each call's revenue landed at or above the midpoint:
- FY25 Q4 rev \$1.24B "upper end of guided range"; EPS \$0.55 "high end of \$0.48–0.56."
- FY26 Q1 rev \$1.05B "above the midpoint"; EPS \$0.35 "above midpoint."
- FY26 Q2 rev \$1.14B "exceeded midpoint by \$75M"; EPS above high end.
- FY26 Q3 rev \$1.24B; EPS \$0.43.
- FY26 Q4 rev \$1.49B "above the midpoint"; record EPS \$0.60.

There is a **mild sandbagging pattern**: ACV guided to "mid-to-high single digit" repeatedly while actually printing 28% / 28% / 22% — management deliberately anchors low on licensing then over-delivers. This is credibility-positive but means the "mid-to-high single digit license growth" long-term target is a floor, not a forecast.

The **uncredentialed** piece is the AGI CPU \$15B-by-FY31 target — a first-ever foray into merchant silicon by an IP company, at ~30% GM vs its 92.5% IP GM, with execution/wafer/memory-supply risk management itself flagged ("until we work through some of the wafer and memory shortage issues"). No delivery history on that vector exists.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

- **R&D outrunning revenue (the margin question).** Asked repeatedly (Andrew Gardiner, FY26 Q3). Management's answer is consistently "growth after Q1 will moderate" but never commits to opex discipline — they explicitly *want* to keep spending. This is the single recurring tension: analysts probe operating leverage, management deflects to "investing through the cycle."
- **China exposure.** China rose from ~14% → 21–22% of revenue across FY26 (Sebastien Naji, Lee Simpson questions). Management waves it off as "tracks the global market" and "as strong as we've ever seen" — but a 21% revenue concentration in China for a UK/US-listed IP firm amid export-control volatility is a risk management is *under-addressing*.
- **Licensing lumpiness.** Analysts keep being redirected to ACV because reported license revenue swings (e.g., FY26 Q1 license -1% YoY off a hard comp). Management has trained the street to ignore the GAAP line — convenient, and so far justified.
- **The chip-business margin dilution.** Harlan Sur (FY26 Q4) pushed directly on AGI CPU opex/accretion. Management conceded first-gen GM ~30%+ and leaned on the "compute die is already our CSS" cost-sharing argument. Watch this — it is the one place the pristine 92.5% GM story could erode.

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **Terminology creep:** "milliwatts to megawatts" (FY25–Q2 FY26) → "milliwatts to **gigawatts**" (FY26 Q3) → "milliwatts to gigawatts" with a \$100B TAM attached (FY26 Q4). The TAM keeps inflating in lockstep with the share price.
- **Business-model tell:** the FY26 Q1–Q2 phrase "exploring the possibility of moving beyond our current platform into... chiplets and potentially full-end solutions" was a *pre-announcement* of the AGI CPU. Management telegraphed the silicon pivot two quarters early — a sign they were managing the market into accepting a model change (and a lower-GM revenue stream) before announcing it.
- **SoftBank dependency surfacing:** SoftBank licensing/design-services deals appear as a *recurring license-revenue contributor* (e.g., \$200M of the \$819M FY26 Q4 license line). A meaningful chunk of "license growth" is related-party. This is rarely highlighted by management and partly explains licensing strength.
- **CSS as the royalty-rate ratchet:** every CSS generation "brings the highest royalty rates we have seen to date" — the disclosed mechanism by which Arm grows royalty faster than chip units. This is the durable part of the bull case and is corroborated by stable GM.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Classification: STRENGTHENING (operationally) / PIVOTING (business model).** The underlying IP franchise is strengthening — accelerating royalty, ASP ladder, verified 50% hyperscaler share, fortress net-cash, clean beat cadence, and a *stable 92.5% gross margin that is the single best moat signal in the file*. Simultaneously the company is **pivoting** from pure-IP licensor to part-merchant-silicon vendor (AGI CPU), which adds a large speculative TAM but dilutes the GM model and introduces execution/supply risk it has never carried.

**The single most important thing the director must weigh:** This is **not a thesis question — it is a price question.** The theme is real, durable, multi-year, and Arm is genuinely load-bearing (5/5). But the stock at \$380.81 trades at **~415x P/FCF / ~190x forward earnings**, every intrinsic model reads −100% MoS, analyst consensus is **41% *below* the price**, insiders are selling and not buying, and the entire premium now leans on an unmonetized \$15B silicon vector at one-third the GM of the core. The business is excellent; the valley of death is a **multiple-compression air-pocket**, not a demand air-pocket. Size for a great company priced for perfection, where the asymmetry is to the downside until the AGI CPU revenue actually prints.
