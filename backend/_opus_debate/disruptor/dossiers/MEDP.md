## 1. NARRATIVE ARC

Medpace's story over these five quarters is a **round-trip through a biotech-funding scare and out the other side — with the exit looking shakier than the headline financials suggest**. The arc has three distinct acts:

- **Act I — The Air Pocket (Q1 2025):** August Troendle opened the year defensive. Net awards were "down sequentially and year over year, with a net book-to-bill ratio of 0.90," driven by "high pipeline cancellations." RFP flow was "strong, but quality has been variable and decisions are slowing." The whole call was about *pre-backlog* cancellations narrowing the funnel. Guidance was cut-looking: FY25 revenue $2.14–2.24B (just +1.5% to +6.2%). This was a genuine demand wobble tied to biotech VC/IPO funding scarcity.

- **Act II — The V-Shaped Recovery (Q2–Q3 2025):** The narrative inverted hard and fast. Q2: "cancellations were down across the pipeline," book-to-bill recovered to 1.03, and guidance was *raised by $280M at the midpoint*. Q3 was the euphoric peak — "record net bookings and a net book-to-bill of 1.20," awarded-but-not-booked work "up approximately 30% year-over-year," and a preliminary 2026 framing of "low double-digit" revenue growth. Crucially, a **new growth driver was introduced and quickly became load-bearing: metabolic / GLP-1.** Metabolic rose from a minor line to ~30% of revenue by Q3, and management leaned into it as the mix-shift story ("faster burning," "higher pass-throughs," GLP-1 "probably 2/3 of our obesity").

- **Act III — The Choppy Plateau (Q4 2025 → Q1 2026):** The recovery is now visibly *uneven*. Q4 book-to-bill fell back to 1.04 with "backlog cancellations… the highest they've been in over a year." Q1 2026 was worse: book-to-bill **0.88**, RFPs "down sequentially and year-over-year," and again "backlog cancels reaching their highest point in over a year." Management's framing shifted to "there is nothing we can do to alter our cancellation rate." The same metabolic engine that powered Act II is now a source of cancellation volatility (Q4: "cancellations were a little bit skewed towards metabolic").

**Quietly dropped vs introduced:** The "1.15+ book-to-bill in H2" promise from Q1 2025 was *technically* hit in Q3 (1.20) then immediately abandoned as a framing device. The new permanent fixtures are (a) the metabolic mix-shift and (b) the **elevated pass-through cost** narrative (41–42% of revenue), which is now used pre-emptively to explain why EBITDA grows *slower* than revenue — a margin-quality tell.

## 3. TONE & CONFIDENCE TRAJECTORY

**Trajectory: Declining (from a euphoric Q3 peak).** Net across the window: roughly flat-to-down, having round-tripped.

- **Q1'25 (defensive):** "decisions are slowing… quality has been variable." Hedged, macro-anxious.
- **Q2'25 (rising):** "better funding than anticipated, fewer cancellations, accelerated client decisions, rapid project start-up" — most offensive tone of the window; guidance raised $280M.
- **Q3'25 (peak confidence):** "record net bookings," voluntarily offered 2026 preliminary guidance "to avoid significant divergence between our view and analyst models" — a confident, almost cocky tell.
- **Q4'25 (defensive again):** "Cancellations were elevated again… I did not anticipate the spike in Q4. Only time will tell." Explicit admission of forecast miss.
- **Q1'26 (resigned):** "there is nothing we can do to alter our cancellation rate" — a notably fatalistic shift from a CEO who spent 2025 promising he could engineer book-to-bill higher. Tone has moved from *engineering the outcome* to *enduring the environment*.

Guidance specificity has actually *improved* (full 2026 ranges given), but the confidence behind the bookings/backlog trajectory has eroded.

## 4. GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY

**Reliability: HIGH on the P&L, MEDIUM on bookings/backlog.**

- **Revenue/EPS delivery is excellent.** FY25 revenue landed at $2.53B vs an initial Q1'25 guide of $2.14–2.24B — a +$300–390M beat over the year via three consecutive raises (Q2 +$280M midpoint, Q3 again). Beat 7/7 quarters (100%) per the screener. This is a management team that **sandbags conservatively and over-delivers on the income statement.**
- **BUT bookings/backlog guidance has missed twice.** The "1.15+ book-to-bill" was promised for "Q3 and Q4" in Q1'25; Q3 delivered 1.20 but Q4 fell to 1.04 and Q1'26 to 0.88 — both *below* the implied trajectory. Troendle explicitly conceded the Q4 spike "I did not anticipate."
- **Net:** Trust the revenue/EPS guide (they have a structural sandbag via the negative-DSO, backlog-conversion model). Discount management's *forward bookings optimism* — they have been a poor predictor of their own cancellation rate twice in two quarters.

**Score: 4/5** — high financial credibility, dented only by repeated bookings-trajectory misses.

## 5. ANALYST PRESSURE POINTS

Recurring topics where analysts pushed and management deflected or under-answered:

- **Service (ex-pass-through) revenue growth** — Eric Coldwell (Baird) and William Blair pressed repeatedly on what *direct* revenue growth is, stripping out pass-throughs. Management **refused to disclose** ("we don't provide a 605 basis look," "we don't provide guidance on direct service revenue"). This is the single most-deflected question and the most important — because pass-throughs are pass-through (≈zero margin), the **real organic growth rate is materially lower than the 20–26% headline.** Coldwell's own math pegged 2026 service-revenue growth at "mid- to upper single digits… up to low double digits." That is the honest growth number.
- **Pre-backlog ("awarded but not booked") bucket size** — analysts repeatedly asked for the magnitude (Eric Coldwell: "is it $4B, $5B, $6B?"). Management gave one data point under pressure ("somewhere under $4 billion") then refused further. Low transparency on the actual leading indicator.
- **GLP-1 / metabolic concentration** — Windley (Jefferies) pushed in Q4'25 and Q1'26 on whether GLP-1 concentration is creating booking volatility. Management confirmed GLP-1 is "2/3 of obesity" and that Q4 cancellations skewed metabolic — i.e., the growth engine is also the volatility engine.
- **Pricing pressure** — analysts probed competitive pricing from top-3 CROs every quarter. Management consistently downplayed ("seems to be the same as it was 5 years ago"), but conceded "anytime there's a slowdown… there tends to be more price competition."

The deflection that "later turned into bad news": the H2'25 book-to-bill promise — management was confident, analysts were skeptical, and Q4/Q1 cancellations proved the skeptics right.

## 6. RED FLAGS / GREEN FLAGS

🔴 **Backlog growing +3–4% while revenue grows +20–26%** — the company is out-burning its book. This cannot continue indefinitely; revenue growth must converge down toward bookings growth.
🔴 **Book-to-bill 0.88 in Q1'26** — below 1.0 means backlog is shrinking on a bookings basis; two of the last three quarters showed "highest cancellations in over a year."
🔴 **EBITDA margin compression** (22.8%→22.0% FY, 21.1% in Q1'26) masked by pass-through-inflated revenue — the "expanding gross margin" screener flag is a mix artifact, not pricing power.
🔴 **Insider behavior:** 0 open-market buys vs 27 sells (441,679 shares) — zero insider conviction at these levels; the grant-inflated buy_ratio in the scan is misleading.
🔴 **President Jesse Geiger departing** (Q1'26) after 18.5 years; CEO Troendle (founder, ~70s) re-absorbing President duties with no named successor — key-man/governance overhang.
🔴 **Customer concentration creeping up:** top-5 went 22%→21%→23%→25%→**28%** and top-10 32%→31%→33%→35%→**37%** across the five quarters — concentration is *rising*, and into the metabolic names that are now cancelling.

🟢 **Best-in-class economics:** ROIC 40.1%, ROE 56.1%, FCF margin 26.9%, negative-DSO working capital (customers fund the business) — genuinely elite asset-light model.
🟢 **Fortress balance sheet:** net cash $530M (cash $653M vs debt $122M).
🟢 **Disciplined, well-timed buyback:** $913M repurchased in 2025, front-loaded into the Q1 fear at ~$327/sh, then paused on recovery.
🟢 **100% beat rate (7/7)** and a structural sandbag in the conversion model.
🟢 **Real secular tailwind:** GLP-1/obesity trial demand is multi-year and expanding (FDA approvals of Wegovy HD and orforglipron in 2026; multiple new Phase 3 obesity programs initiating).

## 7. HIDDEN SIGNALS

- **From "we can engineer book-to-bill" to "nothing we can do to alter our cancellation rate"** — the most important terminology shift. Troendle has surrendered narrative control over the leading indicator.
- **Pass-through cost framing weaponized as a pre-excuse** — by Q3/Q4 management proactively guided pass-throughs to 41–42% to explain *in advance* why EBITDA would lag revenue. They are managing the Street toward judging them on EBITDA, not revenue, because the revenue line is increasingly low-quality pass-through.
- **"Large pharma is not a focus for us"** (Q4'25, Brady) — Medpace is doubling down on the *emerging-biotech* niche precisely as that client base is the most funding-sensitive and M&A-attrition-prone. The moat is the niche, but the niche is the cyclical exposure.
- **M&A attrition admission** (Q1'26): "we generally lose the client long-term" when sponsors get acquired — a slow structural leak in an active biopharma-M&A environment.
- **AI framed defensively/modestly** — "measured approach," efficiency-and-feasibility only; no claim of transformative leverage. Honest, but not a growth lever.

## 8. CAPITAL-ALLOCATION VERDICT (for the director)

**Classification: STABLE, with a DETERIORATING leading-indicator edge.**

Medpace is a genuinely elite, founder-led, asset-light compounder (40% ROIC, 27% FCF margin, net cash, 100% beat rate, disciplined countercyclical buyback). The *quality* of the business is not in doubt. What is in doubt is the **durability of the 20%+ growth rate**: revenue has been outrunning backlog and bookings for a year, the book-to-bill just printed 0.88, cancellations are at a 1-year high, and the real organic (service) growth rate — which management refuses to disclose — is mid-to-high single digits per analysts' own math, not the 20%+ headline.

**The single most important thing the director must weigh before sizing:** *You are being asked to pay ~34x forward earnings (and a 25% premium to fair value, MoS −25%) for a business whose headline 20% growth is half pass-through mix-shift and whose forward bookings are decelerating below 1.0x.* The secular GLP-1 theme is real and durable, but it is **already in the price**, and the company's own metabolic concentration is now a cancellation-volatility source rather than a clean tailwind. This is a wonderful business at an unforgiving price with a softening near-term book — a quality-at-a-bad-entry problem, not a broken-thesis problem.
